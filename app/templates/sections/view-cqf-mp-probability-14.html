1.4 Mutually Exclusive & Independent Events

Event disjoint operations, simplified unions, independent multiplications, and diagnostic rules.

1.4.1 Exclusivity & Independence Principles

Distinguishing mutually exclusive vs independent events is a key mathematical prerequisite:

Mutually Exclusive Events that cannot happen at the same time (no outcomes in common):

$$P(A \cap B) = 0$$

The Addition Rule simplifies to:

$$P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B)$$

Independent Events The occurrence of one event has no impact on the probability of the other:

$$P(A|B) = P(A)$$

The Multiplication Rule simplifies to:

$$P(A \cap B) = P(A) \times P(B)$$

Principal Protected Note (PPN)

The original structured product: a zero-coupon bond plus a call option — full money-back at maturity with equity upside, and every trade-off priced inside.

1. Construction

Invest notional $N$ for term $T$. The issuer splits it:

$$N = \underbrace{N e^{-yT}}_{\text{zero-coupon bond}} + \underbrace{\text{option budget}}_{N(1 - e^{-yT}) - \text{fees}}.$$

The ZCB grows back to $N$ at maturity (the protection); the leftover buys call options on the underlying. Payoff:

$$\text{PPN}_T = N + N \cdot PR \cdot \max\!\left(\frac{S_T - S_0}{S_0},\ 0\right),$$

with participation rate $PR = \dfrac{\text{option budget}}{\text{price of 1 ATM call on } N}$. Example: $y = 4\%$, $T = 5$ → ZCB costs 81.9%, leaving ~18% (less ~2% fees = 16%) of notional; if a 5-year ATM call costs 20% of notional, $PR = 16/20 = 80\%$.

2. What Drives the Terms

  • Rates are the fuel: high rates → cheap ZCB → big option budget → generous participation. Near-zero rates (2010s) made true 100% protection nearly impossible — issuers stretched maturities, capped upside, or moved to partial protection.
  • Volatility is the cost: high implied vol → expensive calls → lower participation. PPN buyers are implicitly selling nothing but buying vol; terms look best when vol is low and rates high.
  • Dividends: calls on price (not total-return) indices are cheaper when dividends are high — participation quietly funds itself by forgoing dividends.
  • Common variants to lift the headline rate: caps (sell a call spread instead), averaging (Asian tail — cheaper option), worst-of baskets (cheaper still, more risk).

3. The Risks That Aren't Market Risk

  • Credit risk: "protection" is an unsecured promise of the issuer — Lehman PPNs paid recovery, not principal. The investor is long a corporate ZCB, and the issuer's funding spread (which boosts the option budget) is exactly the compensation.
  • Inflation & opportunity cost: guaranteed nominal principal over 5–7 years can be a real-terms loss; the certain coupon given up is the protection's true price.
  • Liquidity/mark-to-market: before maturity the note trades at bond + option value — rising rates or falling vol can mark it well below par even with the guarantee intact.

4. Pricing & Risk View (Desk Side)

Decompose and book separately: funding desk issues the ZCB at internal funding +spread; exotics desk hedges the call (vanilla if simple, Asian/basket if structured). Greeks of the note = Greeks of the embedded option scaled by $PR$ — delta starts near $PR \times \Delta_{ATM} \approx 0.4 PR$ and the note behaves increasingly like a bond as the call moves OTM. CPPI is the dynamic-trading alternative (constant proportion portfolio insurance): same goal, path-dependent gap risk instead of option premium — the classic static-vs-dynamic protection comparison.

Key Takeaways

  • PPN = ZCB (protection) + call (upside); participation = option budget ÷ call price.
  • Rates fund the structure, vol prices the upside, dividends are silently forfeited.
  • Protection is issuer credit, not collateral — Lehman is the case study.
  • Caps, Asian tails and baskets are participation-boosters with embedded option sales.
  • Decomposition pricing (bond + option) is exact and is how desks hedge and how buyers should compare offers.

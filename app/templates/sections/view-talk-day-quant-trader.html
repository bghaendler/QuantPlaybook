A Day in the Life of a Quant Trader

The sell-side/market-making and systematic-trading seat: managing a book in real time, pricing and hedging risk, and the reflexes the curriculum builds.

1. Two Flavors of "Quant Trader"

  • Sell-side / market-making desk trader: prices client trades, manages the resulting risk book (a derivatives desk's Greeks), hedges dynamically — the option-engine and Greeks material made into a living P&L.
  • Systematic / automated trader: builds and babysits trading algorithms (HFT-style or mid-frequency), monitoring execution quality and signal health rather than clicking trades.
  • Both share a real-time, risk-on-the-line intensity distinct from the research-heavy PM seat — decisions in seconds-to-hours, not days.

2. The Daily Rhythm (Derivatives Desk)

  • Pre-open: review overnight risk (the book's delta/gamma/vega/theta — the Greeks sections), check positions against limits, read the vol surface and overnight moves.
  • Open & intraday: quote client requests (price + hedge instantly), manage the aggregate book's risk as the market moves — rebalancing the delta hedge (the delta-gamma hedging tool), watching gamma near expiries and vega across the surface.
  • Risk events: recalibrate the smile after big moves (ASV/SVI), manage pin risk at expiry, respond to vol spikes (HFT Part 08 execution under vol).
  • Close: flatten or hedge residual risk, mark the book, hand over or set overnight hedges.

3. The Skills in Action

  • Fast, correct pricing: the option-pricing engine internalized — knowing a price and its Greeks intuitively, and when the model (SABR/local-stoch-vol) is outside its comfort zone.
  • Hedging instinct: delta-gamma-vega management as muscle memory; understanding the hedging-error distribution (the simulator tool) and the cost of rebalancing (HFT).
  • Risk discipline under pressure: position limits, stop-losses, and not doubling down on a losing book — the behavioral failure mode that ends careers (ARM/AI-safety governance).
  • Market microstructure: getting hedges done at good prices (HFT execution), reading flow and liquidity.

4. Curriculum → Seat

  • Option pricing + Greeks + exotics → the products you price and the risk you carry.
  • Stochastic-vol asymptotics (ASV) + SVI fitting → marking and managing the smile in real time.
  • Execution/microstructure (HFT) → hedging and trading at acceptable cost.
  • Risk management (ARM) → limits, VaR, and the discipline to respect them.
  • The meta-lesson: a quant trader turns models into prices and risk into hedges, fast and under pressure — fluency and discipline, not just correctness.

Key Takeaways

  • Quant traders come in market-making (price + hedge a risk book) and systematic (babysit algorithms) flavors — both real-time.
  • The derivatives-desk day is risk review → quoting/hedging → recalibration on moves → end-of-day marking.
  • Skills: fast correct pricing, hedging instinct, risk discipline under pressure, microstructure execution.
  • Pricing + Greeks + exotics + ASV + HFT + ARM all converge into turning models into prices and risk into hedges.

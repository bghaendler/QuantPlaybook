1.9 Expectation & Variance Operators

Expected values (means), dispersion (variance), standard deviations, and balance concepts.

1.9.1 Expectation & Variance Definitions

The Expectation $\mathbb{E}[X]$ represents the probability-weighted average outcome (or mathematical center-of-mass) of an RV:

Discrete RV Expectation: $$\mathbb{E}[X] = \sum_{\text{all } x} x P(X = x)$$
Continuous RV Expectation: $$\mathbb{E}[X] = \int_{-\infty}^{+\infty} x f(x) \, dx$$

The Variance $\text{Var}(X) = \sigma^2$ measures the expected squared deviation from the mean (dispersion):

$$\text{Var}(X) = \mathbb{E}[(X - \mu)^2] = \mathbb{E}[X^2] - (\mathbb{E}[X])^2$$

The Standard Deviation is simply $\sigma = \sqrt{\text{Var}(X)}$.

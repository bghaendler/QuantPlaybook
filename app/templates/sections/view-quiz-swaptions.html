Question 1 Select All That Apply

1. Which of the following statements are true?

Show Mathematical Solution & Derivation

Step-by-Step Breakdown of All Statements:

  • Statement 1 (False): A receiver swaption gives the holder the right to receive a fixed rate $K$. If the strike $K$ is less than the prevailing forward swap rate $R_{swap}$, this option is out-of-the-money (OTM), because you would be receiving a rate below the current market swap rate.
  • Statement 2 (True): A payer swap paying fixed rate $K$ and receiving floating has value $V_p(t) = N A(t)(R_{swap}(t) - K)$. A receiver swap receiving fixed rate $K$ and paying floating has value $V_r(t) = N A(t)(K - R_{swap}(t))$. Thus, $V_p(t) = -V_r(t)$ holds exactly.
  • Statement 3 (False): Option prices are non-negative ($Swpt_p(t) \ge 0, Swpt_r(t) \ge 0$). They cannot be negatives of one another except in the trivial degenerate case where both prices are zero.
  • Statement 4 (True): This is the famous Payer-Receiver Swaption Parity: $$ Swpt_p(t) - Swpt_r(t) = V_{swap}(t) \implies Swpt_p(t) = Swpt_r(t) + V_{swap}(t) $$ where $V_{swap}(t)$ is the value of the forward-starting payer swap (the swap underlying the payer swaption).
  • Statement 5 (True): An at-the-money (ATM) swaption has strike equal to the forward swap rate ($K = R_{swap}(t)$). Under this condition, the value of the underlying swap is exactly zero: $V_{swap}(t) = 0$. By Payer-Receiver Parity, we get: $$ Swpt_p(t) - Swpt_r(t) = 0 \implies Swpt_p(t) = Swpt_r(t) $$

Correct Options: Statements 2, 4, and 5.

Question 2 True / False

2. Consider a cap with reset/settlement dates $0 < T_0 < T_1 < \dots < T_n$, with $T_i - T_{i-1} \equiv \delta$, and a swaption with expiry date $T_0$ and underlying swap with the same tenor structure as the cap and notional $N = 1$. Suppose that both the cap and the swaption have identical strike rate.

Is the following statement true or false?
"The price of the payer swaption is smaller than or equal to the price of the cap."

Show Mathematical Solution & Derivation

Mathematical Proof & Bounds:

Let's look at the payoffs at the swaption expiry $T_0$. The payoff of the payer swaption is:

$$ \text{Payoff}_{swaption}(T_0) = \left( \sum_{i=1}^n P(T_0, T_i) \delta (F(T_0, T_{i-1}, T_i) - K) \right)^+ $$

Using the mathematical subadditivity of the positive part function ($(\sum x_i)^+ \le \sum x_i^+$), we have:

$$ \left( \sum_{i=1}^n P(T_0, T_i) \delta (F(T_0, T_{i-1}, T_i) - K) \right)^+ \le \sum_{i=1}^n \left( P(T_0, T_i) \delta (F(T_0, T_{i-1}, T_i) - K) \right)^+ $$ $$ = \sum_{i=1}^n P(T_0, T_i) \delta (F(T_0, T_{i-1}, T_i) - K)^+ $$

Now, let's consider the value of the caplet $i$ at $T_0$. Caplet $i$ expires at $T_{i-1} \ge T_0$, and pays $\delta (F(T_{i-1}, T_{i-1}, T_i) - K)^+$ at $T_i$. Under the $T_i$-forward measure, its value at $T_0$ is:

$$ \text{Value}_{caplet, i}(T_0) = \mathbb{E}^{T_i} \left[ \delta P(T_0, T_i) (F(T_{i-1}, T_{i-1}, T_i) - K)^+ \mid \mathcal{F}_{T_0} \right] $$

Since the forward rate $F(t, T_{i-1}, T_i)$ is a martingale under the $T_i$-forward measure and option payoffs are convex, by Jensen's Inequality we have:

$$ \mathbb{E}^{T_i} \left[ (F(T_{i-1}, T_{i-1}, T_i) - K)^+ \mid \mathcal{F}_{T_0} \right] \ge (F(T_0, T_{i-1}, T_i) - K)^+ $$

Summing this over all periods $i=1,\dots,n$, we obtain:

$$ \text{Value}_{cap}(T_0) = \sum_{i=1}^n \text{Value}_{caplet, i}(T_0) \ge \sum_{i=1}^n P(T_0, T_i) \delta (F(T_0, T_{i-1}, T_i) - K)^+ $$ $$ \ge \text{Payoff}_{swaption}(T_0) $$

Taking the expectation today (discounted back to $t=0$), we prove that:

$$ \text{Price}_{swaption}(0) \le \text{Price}_{cap}(0) $$

Correct Answer: True.

Question 3 Numeric Answer

3. Consider a swap with notional $N = 1$, a cap, and a floor with identical reset/settlement dates. Consider furthermore a payer and receiver swaption with expiry date on the first reset date of the swap. Assume that the strike rates of the cap, floor and the two swaptions are equal.

In the market you observe that the **cap and the floor are both priced at $2.00\%$** and the **payer swaption is priced at $1.50\%$**. What is the price of the **receiver swaption**?

%
Show Mathematical Solution & Derivation

Theoretical Derivation & Solution:

This problem is solved by linking **Cap-Floor Parity** and **Payer-Receiver Swaption Parity** through the underlying forward-starting swap.

1. **Cap-Floor Parity:** The difference between a cap and floor with identical strikes and dates equals the value of the underlying payer swap:

$$ Cp(0) - Fl(0) = V_{swap}(0) $$

Substituting the observed market prices ($Cp(0) = 2.00\%$ and $Fl(0) = 2.00\%$):

$$ V_{swap}(0) = 2.00\% - 2.00\% = 0.00\% $$

This means the forward-starting swap is exactly **at-the-money (ATM)**, having a present value of zero.

2. **Payer-Receiver Swaption Parity:** Similarly, the difference between a payer swaption and a receiver swaption written on the same underlying swap with the same strike equals the value of that swap:

$$ Swpt_p(0) - Swpt_r(0) = V_{swap}(0) $$

Since we proved $V_{swap}(0) = 0.00\%$ from Cap-Floor Parity, we substitute this in:

$$ Swpt_p(0) - Swpt_r(0) = 0.00\% $$ $$ Swpt_r(0) = Swpt_p(0) $$

Since the payer swaption is priced at **$1.50\%$**, the receiver swaption must also be priced at exactly **$1.50\%$**.

Correct Answer: 1.50% (or 1.5).

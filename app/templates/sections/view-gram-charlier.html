Gram-Charlier Expansion (Skew & Kurtosis Adjustments)

Perturbing the Gaussian: express the return density as normal × polynomial corrections in skewness and kurtosis, and read the volatility smile directly off the moments.

1. The Expansion

Approximate a standardized density $f(z)$ by the Gaussian $\phi(z)$ corrected with Hermite polynomials:

$$f(z) \approx \phi(z)\left[1 + \frac{\gamma_1}{3!} He_3(z) + \frac{\gamma_2}{4!} He_4(z)\right],$$

where $\gamma_1$ = skewness, $\gamma_2$ = excess kurtosis, and $He_3(z) = z^3 - 3z$, $He_4(z) = z^4 - 6z^2 + 3$. Hermite polynomials are orthogonal under the Gaussian weight, so each term corrects exactly one moment without disturbing the lower ones. (The reordered Edgeworth expansion collects terms by powers of $1/\sqrt{n}$ — same ingredients, better asymptotic bookkeeping.)

2. Option Prices with Skew & Kurtosis (Corrado-Su)

Insert the expansion for the log-return density into the discounted expectation. Corrado & Su (1996) obtain

$$C \approx C_{BS} + \gamma_1 Q_3 + \gamma_2 Q_4,$$ $$Q_3 = \frac{S\sigma\sqrt{T}}{3!}\left[(2\sigma\sqrt{T} - d_1)\phi(d_1) + \sigma^2 T\, N(d_1)\right], \qquad Q_4 = \frac{S\sigma\sqrt{T}}{4!}\left[(d_1^2 - 1 - 3\sigma\sqrt{T}\,d_2)\phi(d_1) + \sigma^3 T^{3/2} N(d_1)\right].$$

Black-Scholes plus two analytic correction terms: $Q_3$ tilts value across strikes (skew), $Q_4$ adds wing value (kurtosis). Three intuitive parameters $(\sigma, \gamma_1, \gamma_2)$ fit a whole maturity's smile — a regression, effectively.

3. The Smile in Moment Language (Backus-Foresi-Wu)

Inverting to implied vol gives the celebrated approximation:

$$\sigma_{imp}(d) \approx \sigma\left[1 - \frac{\gamma_1}{6}\, d - \frac{\gamma_2}{24}\left(1 - d^2\right)\right], \qquad d \approx \text{standardized moneyness}.$$

Read directly: skewness sets the smile's slope, kurtosis its curvature. Equity markets ($\gamma_1 < 0$, $\gamma_2 > 0$): downward-sloping skew with raised wings. The map runs both ways — quote risk-reversals and butterflies, recover the market's implied $\gamma_1, \gamma_2$ per maturity. This is the cleanest dictionary between distribution moments and smile geometry in all of option theory.

4. The Catch: Negative Densities

Truncated expansions are polynomials × Gaussian — for large $|\gamma_1|, \gamma_2$ the bracket goes negative in the tails: an arbitrageable, invalid density. The positivity region is small (roughly $|\gamma_1| \lesssim 0.7$, $0 \le \gamma_2 \lesssim 4$ jointly constrained). Consequences and fixes:

  • Fit smiles only within the positivity region (constrained optimization — Jondeau-Rockinger map the admissible set).
  • For steep equity skews the expansion fails exactly where you need it; use it for mild smiles (FX, rates, short-dated equity) or as a perturbative diagnostic, not a production surface.
  • Alternatives when moments are extreme: full parametric models (Heston/Bates), SABR-type expansions, or nonparametric (spline) densities with positivity constraints.

5. Where It Earns Its Keep

  • Quick smile fits & sanity checks: three parameters, closed forms, instant Greeks.
  • Risk language: "the book is long kurtosis, short skew" — Gram-Charlier makes that a precise pricing statement.
  • Approximating awkward distributions: arithmetic Asian baskets (Turnbull-Wakeman's higher-order corrections are Edgeworth terms), portfolio VaR via Cornish-Fisher quantiles — the same expansion inverted: $z_\alpha^{CF} = z_\alpha + \frac{\gamma_1}{6}(z_\alpha^2 - 1) + \frac{\gamma_2}{24}(z_\alpha^3 - 3z_\alpha) - \frac{\gamma_1^2}{36}(2z_\alpha^3 - 5z_\alpha)$.
  • Econometrics bridge: GARCH-implied moments → Gram-Charlier prices links time-series models to option markets without simulation.

Key Takeaways

  • Gaussian × Hermite corrections: $\gamma_1$ on $He_3$, $\gamma_2$ on $He_4$ — orthogonal moment surgery.
  • Corrado-Su: $C = C_{BS} + \gamma_1 Q_3 + \gamma_2 Q_4$ — BS plus analytic skew/kurtosis terms.
  • Smile dictionary: slope ↔ skewness, curvature ↔ kurtosis (Backus-Foresi-Wu).
  • Fatal flaw: densities go negative outside a small moment region — fit constrained or go parametric.
  • Same mathematics powers Cornish-Fisher VaR and Asian-option moment corrections.

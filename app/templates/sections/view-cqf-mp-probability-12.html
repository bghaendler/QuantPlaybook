1.2.1 Diagrammatic Foundations & addition Rule

Three principal diagram types map complex experimental outcomes:

  • Two-Way sample grids: Represents bivariate outcomes (e.g. rolling two separate dice totals).
  • Tree Diagrams: Illustrates sequential trial splits with branch multiplications.
  • Venn Diagrams: Represents intersection and union sets.

For any two intersecting events $A$ and $B$, the Addition Rule states:

$$P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cap B)$$

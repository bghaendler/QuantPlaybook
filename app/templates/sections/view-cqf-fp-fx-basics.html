FX Basics

Currency pairs, quoting conventions, cross-rates and dealing spreads — the vocabulary of the world's largest market.

Plain English Notes

The Big Picture: every FX price is a fraction — the value of one currency measured in another. There is no absolute price of a euro, only EUR/USD, EUR/GBP, EUR/JPY… Currencies trade in pairs because you're always simultaneously buying one and selling the other.

  • Read EUR/USD = 1.0950 as a price tag: one euro (the base) costs 1.0950 dollars (the quote). Base first, quote second, always.
  • The dollar is the hub: ~90% of all FX trades have USD on one side. Exotic pairs are usually two dollar trades chained together — which is exactly how cross-rates are computed below.
  • A pip is the market's tick: the 4th decimal place for most pairs (the 2nd for JPY pairs). EUR/USD moving 1.0950 → 1.0951 is "one pip" — on a standard €1m trade, about $100.
  • The spread is the dealer's fee in disguise: you buy at the higher ask and sell at the lower bid — there's no commission line, but every round trip pays the gap. The demo below prices it.

Foreign Exchange Structure

Currencies are traded strictly in pairs (e.g. EUR/USD). The US Dollar acts as the global primary reserve currency, participating in the vast majority of trades.

Terminology: Base Currency / Quote Currency.

For a quote pair $C_1 / C_2 = X$, $1$ unit of base currency $C_1$ buys $X$ units of quote currency $C_2$.

  • Majors: EUR/USD, USD/JPY, GBP/USD, USD/CHF — the deepest, tightest-spread pairs.
  • Crosses: pairs without USD (EUR/GBP, EUR/JPY) — historically priced through the dollar legs.
  • Market convention decides who is base: EUR, GBP, AUD, NZD quote against the dollar as base (EUR/USD); most others quote the dollar as base (USD/JPY, USD/CHF). It's convention, not mathematics.

In plain English: 2 pips sounds like nothing — and per trade it is (~0.02%). But spreads are how the entire $7-trillion-a-day FX market pays for itself: high-frequency strategies live or die on them (the microstructure course), and retail platforms quoting 50-pip spreads on "commission-free" trades are charging 25× the institutional rate, silently.

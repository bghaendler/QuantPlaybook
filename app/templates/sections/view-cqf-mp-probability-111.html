1.11 Moments, Skewness & Kurtosis

Central moments, asymmetry metrics, tail fatness, and peakedness classifications.

1.11.1 Central Moments & Tail Risk

Moments summarize the structural layout of probability functions. The $n$-th moment is defined as $\mathbb{E}[X^n]$:

Skewness (3rd Central Moment) Measures asymmetry:

$$\text{Skewness} = \frac{\mathbb{E}[(X - \mu)^3]}{\sigma^3}$$

Symmetric normal curves have a skewness of 0. Positive values indicate a right fat tail.
Kurtosis (4th Central Moment) Measures tail weight / extreme risk:

$$\text{Kurtosis} = \frac{\mathbb{E}[(X - \mu)^4]}{\sigma^4}$$

A standard normal curve has Kurtosis = 3. Excess Kurtosis ($\text{Kurtosis} - 3$) isolates tail anomalies.

peakedness Classifications:

  • Leptokurtic (Kurt > 3): High peak with thick, heavy tails (fat-tailed risk).
  • Platykurtic (Kurt < 3): Broad, flat top with thin tails.
  • Mesokurtic (Kurt = 3): Matches standard normal distribution curve.

Higher-Order Greeks (Vanna, Volga, Charm, Color)

Second- and third-order sensitivities: how delta and vega themselves drift with spot, vol and time — the language of smile risk and hedge decay.

2. Vanna: The Skew Greek

Vanna is positive for high-strike (OTM call) options and negative for low strikes: vol up moves call deltas toward ½ from both sides. A book that is delta-neutral but vanna-long gains delta as vol rises. Because equity vol rises when spot falls (negative spot-vol correlation), vanna couples directly to the skew: risk-reversals (long call / short put) are the canonical vanna trade, and the "vanna-volga" method prices first-generation FX exotics by charging for exactly this exposure. Dealer vanna positioning is a popular explanation of index pinning and "vanna flows" around option expiries.

3. Volga: The Smile-Curvature Greek

Volga $= \text{Vega}\, d_1 d_2/\sigma$ is positive for OTM and ITM options but ≈ 0 ATM (where $d_1 d_2 < 0$ it can dip negative just around the money). Long wings = long volga = long vol-of-vol. This is why the smile exists at all under stochastic vol: OTM options carry convexity in $\sigma$ that flat-vol Black-Scholes does not price. Butterflies (long wings, short body) isolate volga; the FX market's quoted butterfly is its price.

4. Charm & Color: Hedges That Age

Charm tells a trader how today's delta hedge will be wrong tomorrow even if nothing moves: OTM option deltas bleed toward 0, ITM toward $\pm e^{(b-r)T}$. Material for overnight risk near expiry and for weekly-option market-makers (charm flows around expiries move index futures). Color is the same statement for gamma: gamma concentrates violently into the ATM strike as $T \to 0$, so a book that looks gamma-flat today can be dangerously peaked on expiry morning.

5. Taylor Expansion of P&L

The full second-order P&L of an option book over $(\delta t, \delta S, \delta\sigma)$:

$$\delta V \approx \Delta\,\delta S + \text{Vega}\,\delta\sigma + \Theta\,\delta t + \tfrac{1}{2}\Gamma\,\delta S^2 + \tfrac{1}{2}\text{Volga}\,\delta\sigma^2 + \text{Vanna}\,\delta S\,\delta\sigma + \text{Charm}\,\delta S\, \delta t + \dots$$

Desk risk reports are exactly this expansion. Cross-terms matter: with equity spot-vol correlation ≈ −0.7, the vanna term $\text{Vanna} \times \delta S \delta\sigma$ is a systematic, not random, contribution — ignoring it biases hedged P&L every day in the same direction.

Key Takeaways

  • Vanna = ∂Δ/∂σ — the skew/risk-reversal Greek; Volga = ∂²V/∂σ² — the smile-curvature/butterfly Greek.
  • Charm and color quantify overnight decay of delta and gamma hedges; critical near expiry.
  • Wings are long volga; ATM is nearly volga-flat — stochastic vol prices the wings up.
  • Second-order P&L expansion with cross-terms is the desk's actual risk report.
  • Vanna-volga pricing charges BS prices for vanna and volga exposure to fit the FX smile.

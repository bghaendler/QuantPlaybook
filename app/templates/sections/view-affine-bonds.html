3. Affine Bond Prices (Vasicek)

By showing that the time-integrated short rate $\int r(s)ds$ is conditionally normal, we can use the moment generating function to compute the bond price expectation. The Vasicek model yields exponential affine bond prices:

$$ P(t, T) = e^{A(T-t) - B(T-t)r(t)} $$

The functions $A(\tau)$ and $B(\tau)$ solve a system of Riccati differential equations:

$$ A'(\tau) = \kappa\theta B(\tau) - \frac{\sigma^2}{2}B(\tau)^2 \quad ; \quad B'(\tau) = -\kappa B(\tau) + 1 $$

with initial conditions $A(0)=0$ and $B(0)=0$. These have analytical closed-form solutions.

Plain English Notes

The Big Picture: "Affine" is math-speak for "looks like a straight line equation in the exponent".

  • Why it's Brilliant: Instead of running millions of slow Monte Carlo simulations to find the bond price, we just plug the current short rate $r(t)$ into a simple formula.
  • Pre-calculated Factors: The functions $A$ and $B$ only depend on the time to maturity ($T-t$). They are pre-calculated weights.
  • Example: The price of a 10-year bond simply depends on today's short rate, shifted and scaled by factors ($A$ and $B$) that are instantly calculated.

Practice Exercise: Calculating Analytical Prices

You are a quant pricing a 5-year zero-coupon bond ($T=5$) under the Vasicek model. The parameters are $\kappa = 0.5$, $\theta = 0.04$, and $\sigma = 0.02$. The current short rate is $r(0) = 0.03$. Calculate the price $P(0, 5)$ of a $100 face value bond.

Hint: $B(\tau) = \frac{1 - e^{-\kappa\tau}}{\kappa}$ and $A(\tau) = \left(\theta - \frac{\sigma^2}{2\kappa^2}\right)(B(\tau) - \tau) - \frac{\sigma^2}{4\kappa}B(\tau)^2$

👉 Show Step-by-Step Solution
  1. Calculate $B(5)$:
    $B(5) = \frac{1 - e^{-0.5 \times 5}}{0.5} = \frac{1 - e^{-2.5}}{0.5} \approx \frac{1 - 0.0821}{0.5} = 1.8358$
  2. Calculate $A(5)$:
    First term: $\left(0.04 - \frac{0.0004}{0.5}\right)(1.8358 - 5) = (0.04 - 0.0008)(-3.1642) = (0.0392)(-3.1642) = -0.1240$
    Second term: $\frac{0.0004}{2} \times 1.8358^2 = 0.0002 \times 3.3702 = 0.00067$
    $A(5) = -0.1240 - 0.00067 = -0.1247$
  3. Calculate Price $P(0, 5)$:
    Exponent: $A(5) - B(5)r(0) = -0.1247 - (1.8358 \times 0.03) = -0.1247 - 0.0551 = -0.1798$
    Discount Factor: $e^{-0.1798} \approx 0.8354$

Answer: The price of the 5-year bond is $100 \times 0.8354 =$ $83.54.

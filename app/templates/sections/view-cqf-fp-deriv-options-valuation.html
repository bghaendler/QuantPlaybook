Option Premium Valuation & Moneyness

An option's premium is partitioned into **Intrinsic Value** (what the option is worth if exercised immediately) and **Time Value** (the extra premium charged for volatility over the option's remaining life):

$$\text{Premium} = \text{Intrinsic Value} + \text{Time Value}$$

Volatility - The Key Option Premium Input

Volatility ($\sigma$) measures the variability of an underlying asset's returns. Because options possess asymmetric payoffs (capped downside, uncapped upside), **higher volatility increases option premiums** by elevating the likelihood that the option finishes deep in-the-money.

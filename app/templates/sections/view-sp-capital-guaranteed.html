Capital Guaranteed Note

The PPN family generalized: partial guarantees, minimum coupons, capped or averaged upside — the menu of protection levels and what each headline term costs.

1. The General Payoff

A capital guaranteed note returns a guaranteed floor $G$ (as % of notional) plus participation in an underlying:

$$V_T = N\left[\,G + PR \cdot \max\!\big(\min(R_{underlying}, \text{Cap}) - \text{Strike},\ 0\big)\right],$$

where $G = 100\%$ is full protection, $G = 90\%$ a "90% capital protected" note, and a guaranteed minimum coupon $c$ shifts the floor to $G + c$. Budget identity per 100 of notional:

$$100 = \underbrace{G \cdot P_{ZCB}}_{\text{floor funding}} + \underbrace{\text{option spend}}_{\text{upside}} + \text{fees}.$$

Every headline improvement (higher participation, higher cap, minimum coupon) must be paid by weakening another term — the structuring triangle.

Partial protection can also be structured as full ZCB + sold OTM put (e.g. floor breached only below −20%): "protected unless the index falls 20%" — economically a put-spread financed floor, stepping toward the reverse convertible family.

3. Upside Engineering

  • Cap: buy call spread instead of call — cheaper, so higher participation, bounded payoff. Marketing favorite ("150% participation up to a 30% cap").
  • Asian tail: average the final 6–12 monthly fixings — reduces option cost ~15–25% and protects against a last-minute crash, at the cost of diluting a late rally.
  • Cliquet/ratchet: lock in periodic gains $\sum_i \max(r_i, 0)$ (capped per period) — forward-starting options, sensitive to forward skew, notoriously model-risky for the issuer.
  • Quanto: foreign underlying, domestic currency payoff — adds an FX-equity correlation adjustment into the forward.
  • Worst-of basket: participation on the worst of several indices — cheapest option, biggest behavioral trap.

4. Valuation & Suitability

Fair value at issue is typically 96–99% of issue price (fees + structuring margin); regulators in several jurisdictions now require publishing this. Decomposition is the analysis: price the ZCB off the issuer's funding curve (credit!), price each option leg on its own surface (smile for spreads, forward skew for cliquets, correlation for baskets). Investor checklist: who guarantees (credit), what exactly is floored (nominal? including coupons?), what is given up (dividends, caps, averaging), and the secondary-market mark sensitivity (long-dated note marks swing on rates and vol long before maturity).

Key Takeaways

  • One budget identity governs all variants: floor funding + option spend + fees = 100.
  • Protection level ↔ participation: each 5 points of floor ≈ tens of points of upside (rates-dependent).
  • Caps, Asian tails, cliquets and baskets are all option-cost reduction devices with distinct risks.
  • The guarantee is issuer credit; fair value at issue sits below par by fees and margin.
  • Decompose, price each leg on its own market, and read the terms as the option positions they are.

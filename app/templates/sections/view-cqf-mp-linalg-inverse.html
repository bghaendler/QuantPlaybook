The Matrix Inverse

Identity matrices, 2x2 inverse formulas, cofactors and minors, and adjoint representations.

1. Matrix Inverse Definition

The inverse of a square matrix $\mathbf{A}$, denoted as $\mathbf{A}^{-1}$, satisfies:

$$\mathbf{A}\mathbf{A}^{-1} = \mathbf{A}^{-1}\mathbf{A} = \mathbf{I}$$

where $\mathbf{I}$ is the identity matrix. If the inverse exists, the solution to the linear system $\mathbf{A}\underline{x} = \underline{p}$ is uniquely found via:

$$\underline{x} = \mathbf{A}^{-1}\underline{p}$$

2. Simple 2x2 Matrix Inverse

For a standard $2 \times 2$ matrix, the inverse formula is defined as:

$$\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix}^{-1} = \frac{1}{ad - bc} \begin{pmatrix} d & -b \\ -c & a \end{pmatrix} \quad \text{provided } ad-bc \neq 0$$

The term $ad - bc$ is the determinant. If the determinant is zero, the matrix is singular and cannot be inverted.

3. General N x N Matrix Inverse

For larger $n \times n$ matrices, the inverse is defined using determinants and adjoint configurations:

$$\mathbf{A}^{-1} = \frac{1}{|\mathbf{A}|} \text{adj}(\mathbf{A})$$

where $|\mathbf{A}|$ is the determinant of $\mathbf{A}$, and $\text{adj}(\mathbf{A})$ is the adjoint (transpose of the cofactor matrix):

$$\text{adj}(\mathbf{A}) = \left[ (-1)^{i+j} |M_{ij}| \right]^{\mathrm{T}}$$

Minor ($M_{ij}$):

The determinant of the sub-matrix remaining after deleting the $i^{\text{th}}$ row and $j^{\text{th}}$ column.

Cofactor ($A_{ij}$):

The signed minor value incorporating the grid parity:

$$A_{ij} = (-1)^{i+j} |M_{ij}|$$ Effect of Row Operations on Determinants:
  • $[ER_1]$ (Interchanging rows): Changes the sign of the determinant ($|A| \to -|A|$).
  • $[ER_2]$ (Multiplying a row by $k$): Multiplies the determinant by $k$ ($|A| \to k|A|$).

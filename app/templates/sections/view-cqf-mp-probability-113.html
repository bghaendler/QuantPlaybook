1.13 Key Probability Distributions (Normal, Binomial, Poisson)

Standard statistical models, parameters, expected properties, and empirical deviation boundaries.

1.13.1 Mathematical Properties

Three primary probability structures model discrete counts and continuous random asset pricing moves:

Binomial Distribution $X \sim B(n, p)$ models finite success trials: $$P(X=x) = \binom{n}{x} p^x (1-p)^{n-x}$$ Mean: $np$
Variance: $np(1-p)$
Poisson Distribution $X \sim Po(\lambda)$ models arrival rates in space/time: $$P(X=r) = \frac{e^{-\lambda}\lambda^r}{r!}$$ Mean: $\lambda$
Variance: $\lambda$
Normal Distribution $X \sim N(\mu, \sigma^2)$ models continuous shifts: $$f(x) = \frac{1}{\sigma\sqrt{2\pi}}e^{-\frac{(x-\mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}}$$ Z-transform: $Z = \frac{X-\mu}{\sigma} \sim N(0,1)$

Mathematical Methods in Financial Markets — Part 06

Ordinary, Partial & Stochastic Differential Equations

The three species of differential equation in quantitative finance, what distinguishes them, and which financial objects each one governs.

1. ODEs: One Independent Variable

$\frac{dy}{dt} = f(t, y)$ — deterministic evolution. Financial specimens:

  • Compounding: $dB/dt = rB \Rightarrow B_t = B_0e^{rt}$ — the money market account, the simplest ODE in the subject.
  • Bond-pricing Riccati systems: affine models (Vasicek, CIR) reduce the bond PDE to ODEs for $A(t,T), B(t,T)$ in $P = e^{A - Br}$.
  • Perpetual options: time-independence turns the BS PDE into a Cauchy-Euler ODE (Part 08).
  • Classification matters for technique: order, linear vs nonlinear, homogeneous vs forced, constant vs variable coefficients.

2. PDEs: Several Independent Variables

Finance's canonical PDE is parabolic (heat-equation family):

$$\frac{\partial V}{\partial t} + \frac{1}{2}\sigma^2S^2\frac{\partial^2 V}{\partial S^2} + rS\frac{\partial V}{\partial S} - rV = 0,$$

backward in time with terminal condition $V(S, T) = $ payoff. Second-order PDE taxonomy — elliptic / parabolic / hyperbolic by the discriminant of the principal part — matters because each type has its own well-posedness and numerics; Black-Scholes transforms exactly into the heat equation $u_\tau = u_{xx}$ (diffusion of value backwards from the payoff). Boundary conditions encode the contract: barriers impose $V(H, t) = $ rebate; American constraints make it a free-boundary problem.

3. SDEs: Noise Enters the Dynamics

$$dX_t = \mu(X_t, t)\,dt + \sigma(X_t, t)\,dW_t$$

— an integral equation in disguise, $X_t = X_0 + \int\mu\,ds + \int\sigma\,dW$, where the second integral is the Itô integral (defined as a mean-square limit, since $W$ has unbounded variation). The drift steers, the diffusion shakes. The model zoo in one table:

$dS = \mu S\,dt + \sigma S\,dW$GBMStocks, FX

4. The Bridge: Feynman-Kac

The three species are one theory. If $V$ solves the parabolic PDE

$$V_t + \mu(x,t)V_x + \tfrac{1}{2}\sigma^2(x,t)V_{xx} - rV = 0, \qquad V(x,T) = g(x),$$

then $V(x, t) = \mathbb{E}\left[e^{-r(T-t)}g(X_T) \mid X_t = x\right]$ where $X$ solves the corresponding SDE. PDE solutions are expectations over SDE paths — which is why trees, finite differences and Monte Carlo must agree, and why you may always pick the cheapest representation: PDE for low dimension and early exercise, expectation/MC for high dimension and path dependence.

Key Takeaways

  • ODE: deterministic paths (compounding, affine bond coefficients). PDE: value surfaces (Black-Scholes ≅ heat equation). SDE: noisy state dynamics (GBM, OU, CIR).
  • Terminal/boundary conditions are the contract; the operator is the model.
  • SDEs are shorthand for integral equations; the Itô integral gives them meaning.
  • Feynman-Kac unifies: price = PDE solution = discounted expectation along SDE paths.

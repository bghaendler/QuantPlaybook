Mathematical Methods in Financial Markets

Itô's Lemma & Corrective Terms

The chain rule for noisy paths: why $dW^2 = dt$ refuses to vanish, where the extra $\frac{1}{2}\sigma^2 f''$ term comes from, and what it buys.

1. Why Ordinary Calculus Fails

Brownian paths move like $\sqrt{dt}$, not $dt$: over a step, $\Delta W \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \Delta t)$ so $(\Delta W)^2$ has mean $\Delta t$ — first order, not second. Taylor-expanding $f(X)$ along a path:

$$df = f'\,dX + \tfrac{1}{2}f''\,(dX)^2 + \dots$$

For smooth paths $(dX)^2 = O(dt^2)$ dies; for Itô processes $dX = \mu dt + \sigma dW$ the box rules apply:

$$dt^2 = 0, \qquad dt\,dW = 0, \qquad dW^2 = dt,$$

(the last is the quadratic-variation identity — exact in the limit, not just on average). The second-order term survives at first order.

2. Itô's Lemma

For $f(X_t, t)$ with $dX = \mu\,dt + \sigma\,dW$:

$$df = \left(\frac{\partial f}{\partial t} + \mu\frac{\partial f}{\partial x} + \frac{1}{2}\sigma^2\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x^2}\right)dt + \sigma\frac{\partial f}{\partial x}\,dW.$$

The chain rule plus one corrective term $\tfrac{1}{2}\sigma^2 f_{xx}$ — convexity times variance. Mnemonic: noise + curvature = drift.

3. The Canonical Applications

  • Log of GBM ($f = \ln S$, $f'' = -1/S^2$): $$d\ln S = \left(\mu - \tfrac{1}{2}\sigma^2\right)dt + \sigma\,dW$$ — the $-\frac{1}{2}\sigma^2$ "volatility drag": a stock with μ = 10%, σ = 40% has median growth 2%. Arithmetic mean of returns ≠ geometric: the gap is exactly $\sigma^2/2$.
  • Exponential check ($f = e^x$ on $dx = \nu dt + \sigma dW$): $\frac{dS}{S} = (\nu + \tfrac{1}{2}\sigma^2)dt + \sigma dW$ — the correction reappears with opposite sign; $\mathbb{E}[e^{\sigma W_t}] = e^{\sigma^2 t/2}$ is the same fact in expectation form.
  • $W^2$: $d(W^2) = 2W\,dW + dt$ ⇒ $\mathbb{E}[W_t^2] = t$, and $W_t^2 - t$ is a martingale — the simplest nontrivial Itô computation and a favorite interview check.
  • Black-Scholes derivation: apply Itô to $V(S, t)$, form the hedged portfolio $\Pi = V - V_S\,S$; the $dW$ terms cancel and the surviving drift identity is the BS PDE, with $\tfrac{1}{2}\sigma^2S^2V_{SS}$ — the option's convexity cost/income — coming straight from the corrective term.

4. Extensions in One Breath

  • Two processes (product rule): $d(XY) = X\,dY + Y\,dX + dX\,dY$ — the cross-variation $\rho\sigma_X\sigma_Y dt$ term drives quanto adjustments and covariance hedging.
  • Multidimensional: replace $\tfrac{1}{2}\sigma^2 f_{xx}$ by $\tfrac{1}{2}\sum_{ij}\rho_{ij}\sigma_i\sigma_j f_{x_ix_j}$.
  • Jumps: add $[f(X_{t^-} + J) - f(X_{t^-})]dN_t$ — convexity correction by finite differences instead of derivatives.
  • Stratonovich ($\circ$): the symmetric integral that obeys ordinary calculus, at the cost of a drift adjustment — physics prefers it; finance prefers Itô because the Itô integral is a martingale (no peeking into the future inside the integrand).

Key Takeaways

  • $dW^2 = dt$: quadratic variation makes second-order terms first-order.
  • Itô = chain rule + $\frac{1}{2}\sigma^2 f''\,dt$; convexity × variance becomes drift.
  • Volatility drag $-\sigma^2/2$ and BS's gamma term are the same correction in two costumes.
  • Product rule's $dX\,dY$ cross term handles correlation; jumps correct by differences; Itô (not Stratonovich) keeps martingales.

Plain English Notes

The Big Picture: Itô's Lemma is the chain rule of stochastic calculus. Because random paths are extremely jagged, regular calculus fails, and we must add a term to capture the extra variance.

  • The Correction Term: In regular calculus, a change in a function depends only on the first derivative. Itô's Lemma adds a second derivative term scaled by volatility.
  • Volatility Drag: Itô's Lemma shows why a highly volatile asset grows slower than its arithmetic return over time.
  • Black-Scholes Connection: The second-order term in Itô's Lemma is precisely where the Gamma term in the Black-Scholes PDE comes from.

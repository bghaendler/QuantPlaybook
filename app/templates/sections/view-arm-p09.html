Advanced Risk Management — Part 09

Excel Random Number Generators vs. Industry Standards

The generator under your Monte Carlo is a model assumption: what RAND() actually is, how PRNGs are judged, and the industry-standard choices for production risk engines.

1. What's Inside RAND()

  • Legacy Excel (pre-2003) used a weak LCG-style routine with documented failures; modern Excel implements Mersenne Twister (MT19937) for RAND() — statistically respectable for teaching-scale work.
  • Remaining workflow hazards: volatility (every recalculation redraws — results unreproducible unless you paste values), no seed control in the function itself (no audit trail), and the normal-conversion step: use NORM.S.INV(RAND()); never the sum-of-12-uniforms folklore, which truncates the tails VaR lives in (its support is ±6σ, and tails are badly thin long before that).
  • VBA's Rnd is a 24-bit LCG with period $2^{24}$ ≈ 16.7m — exhausted by one serious VaR run; avoid for anything measured.

2. How Generators Are Judged

  • Period: draws before repetition — MT19937: $2^{19937}-1$; PCG64: $2^{128}$; both effectively infinite vs. an LCG's $2^{24}$–$2^{32}$.
  • Equidistribution & structure: classic LCGs lay points on lattice hyperplanes in high dimensions (the RANDU disaster) — fatal for path generation where each path is one high-dimensional point.
  • Statistical batteries: TestU01 (SmallCrush/Crush/BigCrush), Dieharder, NIST — MT19937 fails a couple of linear-complexity tests in BigCrush (rarely material for finance); PCG64 and Philox pass clean.
  • Reproducibility & streams: seedable, with independent parallel substreams (jump-ahead/counter-based) — a governance requirement (Part 01): yesterday's VaR must be re-runnable bit-for-bit.
  • Cryptographic security is not the bar: risk MC needs statistical quality and speed, not unpredictability.

4. Does It Change the VaR? (The Test That Settles It)

  • Re-run Part 08's two-asset engine under MT19937, PCG64 and scrambled Sobol with matched $N$: central VaR estimates agree within sampling error — generator quality shows up in the tails and in convergence rate, not the mean of good generators.
  • Swap in a deliberately bad LCG (e.g. RANDU coefficients): tail quantiles bias visibly and path-dependent payoffs (barriers) misprice — the lattice structure interacts with the path dimension.
  • Sobol at 10k points typically matches pseudo-random at ~100k on smooth payoffs — the cheapest 10× speedup in risk computing; degrade gracefully (effective dimension) on kinked payoffs.
  • Policy: seedable PCG64/Philox for production engines with logged seeds; Sobol overlay for convergence; Excel + pasted draws acceptable for prototypes with the draw block archived.

Key Takeaways

  • Modern Excel RAND() is Mersenne-class — the real spreadsheet risks are reproducibility and the normal-conversion shortcut, not the generator.
  • Judge PRNGs by period, high-dimensional equidistribution, test batteries, and stream/seed governance.
  • Production: PCG64/Philox (+ logged seeds); GPU: counter-based; convergence: scrambled Sobol with Brownian bridges.
  • Validate by engine swap: good generators agree within sampling error — anything else is a finding.

Collateral, Margins & Central Clearing (CCPs)

The plumbing of modern derivatives markets: how counterparty credit risk is collateralized bilaterally, mutualized through clearing houses, and priced into valuation.

1. Counterparty Risk & the Collateral Response

A derivative with positive mark-to-market $V_t$ is an unsecured loan to your counterparty: if they default you lose the replacement cost. Collateralization neutralizes this by posting assets against exposure, governed bilaterally by an ISDA Master Agreement plus Credit Support Annex (CSA), which specifies eligible collateral, thresholds, minimum transfer amounts, and call frequency.

  • Variation Margin (VM): daily (or intraday) cash flows matching mark-to-market changes — keeps current exposure near zero.
  • Initial Margin (IM): a buffer against the potential move between the last VM exchange and close-out — the Margin Period of Risk (MPoR), typically 5–10 days.

2. Sizing Margin: Haircuts & IM Models

Non-cash collateral is discounted by a haircut $h$: posting value $C$ secures only $(1-h)C$. IM is sized to cover close-out losses at high confidence:

$$\text{IM} = \text{VaR}_{99\%}\big(\Delta V \text{ over MPoR}\big) \quad \text{or} \quad \text{ES}_{99\%/99.5\%},$$

computed by CCP models (e.g. SPAN-style scenario grids, filtered historical simulation) or, for non-cleared bilateral trades, by the standardized ISDA SIMM — a sensitivity-based (delta/vega/curvature) margin model mandated under the Uncleared Margin Rules (UMR). A simple parametric illustration: a 10y swap with DV01 of \$70k and 10bp 99% 10-day rate move needs IM ≈ \$70k × 10 ≈ \$0.7m.

3. Central Counterparties (CCPs)

Post-2009 (G20 Pittsburgh), standardized OTC derivatives must clear through CCPs (LCH, CME, ICE, Eurex). The CCP interposes itself by novation: the bilateral trade becomes two trades, each facing the CCP — buyer to every seller, seller to every buyer.

  • Multilateral netting: offsetting exposures across all members collapse; net exposure (and collateral demand) falls dramatically relative to a bilateral web.
  • Loss waterfall: defaulter's IM → defaulter's default-fund contribution → CCP "skin in the game" → surviving members' default fund → assessments/recovery tools (VM gains haircutting). Mutualization starts only after the defaulter's own resources are exhausted ("defaulter pays" principle).
  • Default management: the CCP hedges then auctions the defaulter's portfolio to surviving members (tested in the 2018 Nasdaq Clearing power-spread default, which burned ⅔ of the commodity default fund).

4. Consequences for Valuation: the XVA World

Collateralization changes discounting and adds valuation adjustments:

  • OIS discounting: cash-collateralized trades earn the overnight rate on VM, so the CSA discount curve is OIS (€STR/SOFR), not LIBOR — the post-2008 multi-curve framework.
  • CVA/DVA: residual (uncollateralized or gap) counterparty risk: $\text{CVA} = (1-R)\int_0^T \mathbb{E}[\text{EE}(t)]\, dPD(t)$.
  • FVA: funding cost of posting/receiving collateral mismatches.
  • MVA: lifetime cost of funding initial margin — material for cleared and UMR portfolios.
  • KVA: cost of regulatory capital held against the trade.

5. The Trade-Off: Did CCPs Remove Risk?

Clearing transforms credit risk into liquidity risk and concentration risk. Margin calls are procyclical: volatility spikes → IM models demand more collateral → fire sales (the March 2020 "dash for cash" and 2022 UK gilt/LDI episode). CCPs themselves are now systemically critical single points of failure, supervised with their own recovery-and-resolution regimes. The system is safer against idiosyncratic default, but funding-stress dynamics are sharper and more synchronized.

Key Takeaways

  • VM tracks mark-to-market; IM covers the close-out gap over the margin period of risk.
  • CSA terms (thresholds, eligible collateral, haircuts) determine residual exposure — and the discount curve (OIS).
  • CCP novation + multilateral netting shrink exposures; the default waterfall mutualizes extreme losses.
  • ISDA SIMM standardizes bilateral IM under the Uncleared Margin Rules.
  • XVA desks price CVA, FVA, MVA, KVA — collateral economics is now part of the derivative's price.
  • Margin procyclicality converts credit risk into system-wide liquidity risk.

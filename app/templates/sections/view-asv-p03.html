Asymptotics of Stochastic Volatility

Small-Time vs. Large-Time Asymptotic Limits

Two different mathematical regimes with two different toolkits: geodesics rule short maturities, ergodic theory rules long ones — and the smile behaves accordingly.

1. The Two Regimes

2. Small-Time: Large Deviations Formalized

Freidlin-Wentzell/Varadhan: $\;\lim_{T\to0} T\ln\mathbb{P}(\ln S_T \approx k) = -\frac{d^2(k)}{2}$, hence the BBF-type identity

$$\sigma_{BS}(k)\big|_{T\to0} = \frac{|k|}{d(k)}$$

— moneyness over geodesic distance (model-enters only through $d$). Heston's small-time smile, SABR's backbone, and local-vol's harmonic-mean formula are all instances. Corrections in powers of $T$ (the heat-kernel $a_1, a_2$) give the practical formulas of Part 02. Mind the order of limits: small-$T$ at fixed strike; for wings-at-fixed-$T$, Lee's moment bounds rule instead (Part 05's territory).

3. Large-Time: Ergodicity and Flattening

With mean-reverting vol (Heston, OU-vols), the average realized variance $\frac{1}{T}\int_0^T v_s ds \to \bar{v}$ (ergodic theorem) — randomness in average vol dies, so fixed-strike smiles flatten toward a single Black vol $\sqrt{\bar v}$. The refined picture (Lewis; Forde-Jacquier for Heston): the limiting cumulant generating function $\Lambda(u) = \lim \frac{1}{T}\ln\mathbb{E}[e^{u\ln S_T}]$ exists; Gärtner-Ellis gives a large-deviation rate $\Lambda^*$ and a nontrivial limiting smile in scaled moneyness:

$$\sigma_\infty^2(\theta) \;=\; 2\left(2\Lambda^*(\theta) - \theta\right) \;\text{-type formulas}, \qquad k = \theta T,$$

computable from the model's principal eigenvalue (for Heston, explicit in the parameters). This is the regime relevant for long-dated FX/rates hybrids and ALM-horizon options.

4. Practical Consequences

  • Use the right expansion: Hagan/short-time formulas extrapolated to 10y misprice systematically (Part 02's drift); long-dated books should be marked with large-time-consistent models or full numerics.
  • Term-structure diagnostics: plot ATM skew × T — Markovian SV predicts a plateau (skew ∼ c/T); persistent power-law growth in the data is the empirical fingerprint behind rough-vol models (Part 06 adjacent).
  • Model selection by regime: ν, ρ are identified from short-dated smiles; mean-reversion speed κ and $\bar v$ from the long end — calibrate each parameter where its regime has power.
  • Sanity tests for numerics: any SV pricer must reproduce flattening at large T and the $|k|/d(k)$ law at small T — two free asymptotic unit tests.

Key Takeaways

  • Short maturity: geometry (geodesics, Varadhan) — smile = moneyness/distance with $T$-power corrections.
  • Long maturity: ergodicity — fixed-strike smiles flatten; a limiting smile survives only in $k \sim \theta T$ scaling (Gärtner-Ellis).
  • Markovian SV forces ATM skew ∼ 1/T at the long end — data's slower decay motivates rough volatility.
  • Calibrate each parameter in the regime that identifies it; use the asymptotics as free unit tests for pricers.

Caps, Floors & Swaptions (Black Lognormal)

The vanilla interest-rate options market and its quoting standard: Black-76 applied to forward rates and forward swap rates, made rigorous by the right choice of numeraire.

1. Caps & Floors: Strips of Options on Forward Rates

A cap pays the holder whenever a floating rate fixes above strike $K$: for each period $[T_{i-1}, T_i]$ with accrual $\delta_i$ and notional $N$, the caplet pays $N\delta_i \max(L_i - K, 0)$ at $T_i$, where $L_i$ is the rate fixed at $T_{i-1}$. A cap = sum of caplets; a floor = sum of floorlets paying $\max(K - L_i, 0)$. Borrowers buy caps to insure floating debt; the cap/floor parity is

$$\text{Cap} - \text{Floor} = \text{Payer Swap (at strike } K).$$

2. Black's Formula for Caplets — and Why It Is Legitimate

Quote the forward rate $F_i$ for period $i$ and discount factor $P(0, T_i)$:

$$\text{Caplet}_i = N \delta_i P(0, T_i)\left[F_i N(d_1) - K N(d_2)\right], \qquad d_{1,2} = \frac{\ln(F_i/K) \pm \sigma_i^2 T_{i-1}/2}{\sigma_i \sqrt{T_{i-1}}}.$$

This is not an ad-hoc borrowing of Black-76: under the $T_i$-forward measure (numeraire $P(t, T_i)$), the forward rate $F_i(t)$ is a martingale, so assuming lognormal dynamics $dF_i = \sigma_i F_i\, dW^{T_i}$ makes the formula exact. Each caplet uses its own measure — which is precisely why a strip of caplets is easy but joint dynamics (needed for exotics) require the LIBOR Market Model.

3. Swaptions: One Option on a Swap Rate

A payer swaption gives the right at $T_0$ to enter a swap paying fixed $K$ on tenor $T_0 \dots T_n$. With forward swap rate $S_0$ and annuity $A(0) = \sum_i \delta_i P(0, T_i)$:

$$\text{PayerSwaption} = N A(0)\left[S_0 N(d_1) - K N(d_2)\right], \qquad d_{1,2} = \frac{\ln(S_0/K) \pm \sigma^2 T_0/2}{\sigma\sqrt{T_0}}.$$

Justification mirrors the caplet: under the annuity (swap) measure, the forward swap rate is a martingale. The swaption surface is quoted on the (option expiry × swap tenor) grid — e.g. "1y10y" — in implied vols. Receiver swaptions price by the put side. Note: a cap is a basket of options (one per fixing); a swaption is one option on a basket (average of forwards) — so caps ≥ swaptions on comparable terms, and the gap prices forward-rate decorrelation.

4. Lognormal vs. Normal Quoting

  • Black (lognormal) vols: the historical standard; undefined for $F < 0$.
  • Shifted Black: lognormal on $F + s$ (shift $s$ ≈ 1–3%) — the patch adopted when EUR/CHF rates went negative.
  • Bachelier (normal) vols: $dF = \sigma_N dW$; quoted in basis points/year, handles any sign, now the dominant convention in EUR and common in USD. Caplet price: $P(0,T_i)N\delta_i\left[(F-K)N(d) + \sigma_N\sqrt{T}\,\phi(d)\right]$, $d = (F-K)/(\sigma_N\sqrt{T})$.
  • Conversions between conventions are smile-dependent — "the vol" means nothing without the convention attached.

5. Stripping, Smiles & SABR

Markets quote flat cap vols (one vol repricing the whole cap); desks strip them into per-caplet (spot) vols by bootstrapping shorter caps out of longer ones. Across strikes, caplets and swaptions exhibit smiles/skews, almost universally interpolated with SABR per expiry×tenor bucket — its Hagan expansion gives implied vol directly, and its parameters (β, ρ, ν) organize the desk's risk (backbone, skew, smile). Post-LIBOR, the same machinery applies to compounded-in-arrears SOFR/€STR caplets with an adjustment for the accrual-period averaging.

Key Takeaways

  • Caplets: Black-76 on the forward rate, exact under the $T_i$-forward measure; cap = strip of caplets.
  • Swaptions: Black-76 on the forward swap rate under the annuity measure, scaled by the annuity.
  • Cap − Floor = Swap; Cap (basket of options) ≥ Swaption (option on basket): the spread prices correlation.
  • Negative rates forced shifted-lognormal and normal (Bachelier) quoting conventions.
  • Vol surfaces are stripped, then smiled with SABR — the parametrization of rate-options risk.

Taylor Series Approximations

Local polynomial pictures of nonlinear functions ŌĆö the engine behind Greeks, duration/convexity, It├┤'s lemma and every risk report you will ever read.

1. The Expansion

$$f(x + h) = f(x) + f'(x)h + \frac{f''(x)}{2!}h^2 + \frac{f'''(x)}{3!}h^3 + \dots + \frac{f^{(n)}(x)}{n!}h^n + R_n,$$

with Lagrange remainder $R_n = \frac{f^{(n+1)}(\xi)}{(n+1)!}h^{n+1}$ for some $\xi$ between $x$ and $x+h$ ŌĆö the error is controlled by the next term's size. Workhorse series worth memorizing:

$$e^h = 1 + h + \tfrac{h^2}{2} + \dots, \qquad \ln(1 + h) = h - \tfrac{h^2}{2} + \tfrac{h^3}{3} - \dots, \qquad \frac{1}{1-h} = 1 + h + h^2 + \dots \;(|h| < 1).$$

$\ln(1+h) \approx h$ is why small simple returns Ōēł log returns; the $-h^2/2$ correction is why they diverge for big moves (and where "volatility drag" comes from).

2. Finance Is Second-Order Taylor

  • Option P&L: $\delta V \approx \Delta\,\delta S + \tfrac{1}{2}\Gamma\,\delta S^2 + \Theta\,\delta t + \text{Vega}\,\delta\sigma + \dots$ ŌĆö the desk risk report is a truncated multivariate Taylor expansion; the cross-term $\partial^2 V/\partial S\partial\sigma$ is vanna.
  • Bonds: $\frac{\delta P}{P} \approx -D\,\delta y + \tfrac{1}{2}C\,\delta y^2$ ŌĆö duration and convexity are nothing but the first two Taylor coefficients in yield.
  • Delta-gamma VaR: approximate the portfolio as quadratic in risk factors, then compute the quantile (Cornish-Fisher) ŌĆö fast, and accurate exactly while $h$ stays small. 2008's lesson: the remainder term is a market regime, not a footnote.
  • It├┤ preview: for $dX = \mu dt + \sigma dW$, the second-order term $\frac{1}{2}f''\,dX^2$ does not vanish because $dW^2 = dt$ ŌĆö It├┤'s lemma is Taylor to second order plus that one substitution (Part 10).

3. Worked Example: ATM Straddle Rule

Expand the BS call around the money with $b = 0$, small $\sigma\sqrt{T}$:

$$C_{ATM} \approx 0.4\, S\, \sigma\sqrt{T},$$

(precisely $S\sigma\sqrt{T}\,\phi(0)\cdot$ correction, $\phi(0) = 0.3989$). The ATM straddle Ōēł $0.8 S\sigma\sqrt{T}$: a one-line vol-to-price converter used on every options desk, and the Brenner-Subrahmanyam implied-vol seed inverted. Accuracy check: Žā = 20%, T = 0.25 ŌåÆ $\sigma\sqrt{T} = 0.10$, error of the truncation ~0.1% ŌĆö third-order terms are $O((\sigma\sqrt{T})^3)$.

4. When Taylor Fails

  • Kinks and digitals: no derivatives at the kink ŌĆö expansions across a strike near expiry are meaningless (gamma explodes; the remainder dominates).
  • Radius of convergence: $\ln(1+h)$ diverges for $|h| > 1$; geometric series likewise ŌĆö extrapolating fitted polynomials beyond their data is the classic smile-fitting accident (SVI's linear wings exist precisely to avoid it).
  • Large moves: delta-gamma approximations understate losses for convex-short books in jumps; full revaluation in stress tests is the institutional remedy.

Key Takeaways

  • Greeks, duration/convexity and delta-gamma VaR are Taylor coefficients wearing work clothes.
  • $\ln(1+h) \approx h - h^2/2$: returns arithmetic, volatility drag, and log-vs-simple in one line.
  • $C_{ATM} \approx 0.4 S\sigma\sqrt{T}$ ŌĆö the most used back-of-envelope formula in options.
  • Respect the remainder: kinks, jumps and convergence radii are where truncation bites.

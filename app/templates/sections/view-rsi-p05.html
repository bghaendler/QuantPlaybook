Risk-Sensitive Investing — Part 05

Asset-Liability Management under Jump Diffusion

Investing against obligations, not in a vacuum: the surplus framework, why liabilities change the optimal portfolio, and what jumps do to funding risk.

1. Surplus, Not Wealth, Is the Objective

Pensions, insurers and endowments optimize surplus $S_t = A_t - L_t$ (assets minus liabilities) or the funding ratio $A_t/L_t$. Liabilities are themselves stochastic — discounted future payouts, sensitive to rates and (for insurers) longevity/claims. The objective becomes utility of terminal surplus or a shortfall/ruin probability, which reshapes everything:

$$d(A/L) = \frac{A}{L}\Big[(\text{asset return} - \text{liability growth})dt + \sigma_A dW_A - \sigma_L dW_L + \text{jumps}\Big].$$

The relevant risk is the mismatch, not asset volatility alone — a "riskless" cash position is risky for a long-duration liability, and long bonds that hedge the liability are the true low-risk holding.

2. Optimal ALM Allocation

The Merton solution (Part 01) in surplus space splits into two pieces:

$$\pi^*_{ALM} = \underbrace{\frac{1}{\gamma}\Sigma^{-1}(\mu - r)}_{\text{speculative (Kelly-like)}} \;+\; \underbrace{\big(1 - \tfrac{1}{\gamma}\big)\,\beta_{A,L}}_{\text{liability-hedging}}.$$
  • Liability-hedging demand: hold the asset combination most correlated with liabilities (duration-matched bonds, inflation linkers) — independent of the equity premium; this is the LDI (liability-driven investing) core.
  • Speculative demand: the Part 01–03 growth tilt, scaled by risk aversion, funded by surplus capacity above the hedge.
  • As γ → ∞ (or funding ratio → 1, no buffer): pure liability hedging — the pension that can't afford risk holds the matching portfolio. As surplus grows, room for the speculative sleeve grows — state-dependent, like drawdown-constrained Kelly (Part 03).

3. Jumps and Funding Risk

  • Asset jumps (equity crashes) hit the funding ratio discretely — a continuous hedge can't catch them; ALM under jump-diffusion holds less in the speculative sleeve than the diffusion model and may buy explicit tail protection (cheap convexity, ARM Part 06).
  • Liability jumps: rate gaps reprice long liabilities suddenly; longevity shocks (mortality improvements) and catastrophe claims are pure jumps on the liability side — co-jumps between assets and liabilities (a crash that also cuts discount rates) are the dangerous case the jump-diffusion ALM is built to see.
  • Ruin/shortfall focus: jumps fatten the surplus tail, so the relevant metric shifts from variance to shortfall probability / expected shortfall of surplus — connecting directly to ARM's tail measures; risk-sensitive control (Part 02) naturally penalizes these tails.
  • Regulatory overlay: Solvency II / pension funding rules impose capital against exactly this tail — the optimization is constrained by solvency buffers, a reflecting-boundary problem (HFT Part 09 machinery).

4. Practice

  • Two-portfolio implementation: a hedging portfolio (LDI: duration/inflation-matched) plus a growth portfolio (return-seeking), with the split governed by the funding ratio and surplus risk budget — the institutional standard this theory underpins.
  • Glide paths: de-risk (shift toward the hedge) as funding improves or the horizon shortens — the dynamic-programming output, not a heuristic.
  • Stress co-jumps: the 2008/2022 scenarios (assets down, liabilities up or down sharply) are the binding tests; calibrate jump correlation conservatively (Part 04's warning).
  • Stochastic-factor extension (Part 06) adds rate/inflation factors driving both legs — the full model for long-horizon institutional ALM.

Key Takeaways

  • Optimize surplus/funding ratio, not wealth — risk is asset-liability mismatch, making matching bonds the true safe asset.
  • Allocation = liability-hedging demand (LDI) + risk-aversion-scaled speculative demand; the split tracks the funding ratio.
  • Jumps and co-jumps (crash + rate move, longevity, catastrophe) drive funding tails — shift the metric to surplus shortfall.
  • Implement as hedging + growth portfolios with a funding-driven glide path; stress co-jumps and respect solvency buffers.

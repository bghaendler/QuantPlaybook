1.1 Portfolio Weights & Budget Constraint

To represent asset allocation in a portfolio of $N$ assets, we use portfolio weights $w_i$, defined as the proportion of total wealth invested in each asset:

$$w_i = \frac{\text{Market Value of Asset } i}{\text{Total Market Value of Portfolio}}$$

Since all wealth must be fully invested, the weights must satisfy the budget equation (sum to exactly one):

$$\sum_{i=1}^{N} w_i = 1 \quad \text{or, in matrix notation} \quad \mathbf{w}^{\mathrm{T}} \mathbf{1}_N = 1$$
💡 Weights are dimensionless, independent of initial wealth, and sum to 1.

1.2 Core Assumptions & Elliptical Returns

All risky assets in Modern Portfolio Theory are assumed to be fully characterized by their first two statistical moments:

  • Expected Return ($\mu_i$): The mean value of future returns.
  • Volatility ($\sigma_i$): Standard deviation of returns.
  • Pairwise Correlation ($\rho_{ij}$): Linear relationship between returns.

This mean-variance assumption holds as long as the joint distribution of asset returns is elliptical (such as Normal or Student's $t$ distributions with fat tails).

Matrix expected return & risk: $\mu_{\Pi} = \mathbf{w}^{\mathrm{T}} \boldsymbol{\mu} \quad \sigma_{\Pi} = \sqrt{\mathbf{w}^{\mathrm{T}} \mathbf{\Sigma} \mathbf{w}}$

View Analytical Derivations for Two-Asset Portfolios

The mathematical properties of the opportunity set of two risky assets $A$ and $B$ are summarized below:

  • Variance Formulation: $$\sigma_{\Pi}^2 = w_A^2\sigma_A^2 + (1-w_A)^2\sigma_B^2 + 2\rho_{AB}w_A(1-w_A)\sigma_A\sigma_B$$
  • Derivation of Global Minimum Variance Portfolio (GMVP): Setting the derivative $\frac{d\sigma_{\Pi}^2}{dw_A} = 0$ yields the weight minimizing unconstrained risk: $$w_A^G = \frac{\sigma_B^2 - \rho_{AB}\sigma_A\sigma_B}{\sigma_A^2 + \sigma_B^2 - 2\rho_{AB}\sigma_A\sigma_B} = \frac{\sigma_B(\sigma_B - \rho_{AB}\sigma_A)}{\sigma_A^2 + \sigma_B^2 - 2\rho_{AB}\sigma_A\sigma_B}$$
  • Zero-Variance Portfolio under Perfect Negative Correlation ($\rho_{AB} = -1$): Under $\rho_{AB} = -1$, the portfolio volatility collapses to a linear equation: $$\sigma_{\Pi} = |w_A\sigma_A - (1-w_A)\sigma_B| = 0 \implies w_A^Z = \frac{\sigma_B}{\sigma_A + \sigma_B}$$

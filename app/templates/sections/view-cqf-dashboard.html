The **Black-Scholes analytical option price** is derived under the assumption that the stock price follows a Geometric Brownian Motion. The formulas for Call ($C$) and Put ($P$) option values are:

$$C(S, t) = S N(d_1) - K e^{-r T} N(d_2) \quad , \quad P(S, t) = K e^{-r T} N(-d_2) - S N(-d_1)$$

where $N(x)$ is the cumulative standard normal distribution function, and $d_1$ and $d_2$ are defined as:

$$d_1 = \frac{\ln(S/K) + (r + \frac{1}{2}\sigma^2)T}{\sigma\sqrt{T}} \quad , \quad d_2 = d_1 - \sigma\sqrt{T}$$

The options sensitivities (**Greeks**) measure the derivatives of the pricing formula:

  • **Delta ($\Delta = \frac{\partial V}{\partial S}$)**: $\Delta_C = N(d_1)$ ; $\Delta_P = N(d_1) - 1$
  • **Gamma ($\Gamma = \frac{\partial^2 V}{\partial S^2}$)**: $\Gamma_C = \Gamma_P = \frac{n(d_1)}{S \sigma \sqrt{T}}$
  • **Vega ($\mathcal{V} = \frac{\partial V}{\partial \sigma}$)**: $\mathcal{V}_C = \mathcal{V}_P = S \sqrt{T} n(d_1)$
  • **Theta ($\Theta = \frac{\partial V}{\partial T}$)**: $\Theta_C = -\frac{S n(d_1) \sigma}{2\sqrt{T}} - rKe^{-rT}N(d_2)$ ; $\Theta_P = -\frac{S n(d_1) \sigma}{2\sqrt{T}} + rKe^{-rT}N(-d_2)$

Under the **Parametric Normal Method**, the portfolio value change $\Delta V$ is modeled as a normal variable with mean $0$ and standard deviation $\sigma_{port}$ over a horizon of $N$ days:

$$\sigma_{port} = V_0 \times \sigma_{ann} \times \sqrt{\frac{N}{252}}$$

**Value at Risk (VaR)** solves for the critical loss threshold such that the probability of experiencing a larger loss is exactly $1 - \alpha$:

$$\text{VaR}_{\alpha} = Z_{\alpha} \times \sigma_{port}$$

where $Z_{\alpha} = \Phi^{-1}(\alpha)$ is the inverse standard normal cumulative distribution function (e.g. $1.6449$ for $95\%$, $2.3263$ for $99\%$).

**Expected Shortfall (ES)**, also known as Conditional VaR, measures the *conditional expectation of loss* given that the loss exceeds the VaR threshold. Under the standard normal distribution, this has a convenient analytical form:

$$\text{ES}_{\alpha} = \text{E}[\text{Loss} \mid \text{Loss} \ge \text{VaR}_{\alpha}] = \sigma_{port} \times \left( \frac{\phi(Z_{\alpha})}{1 - \alpha} \right)$$

where $\phi(z) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} e^{-z^2/2}$ is the standard normal probability density function (PDF). This proves that Expected Shortfall is strictly larger than Value at Risk because it averages the entire tail area.

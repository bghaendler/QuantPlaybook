Express your answer in basis points and round to 2 decimal places.

Step-by-Step Derivation:

1. Forward Rate Formulation in Vasicek: The forward rate SDE in terms of the short rate is: $$ f(t, T) = r(t) e^{-\kappa(T-t)} + \theta(1 - e^{-\kappa(T-t)}) - \frac{\sigma^2}{2\kappa^2}(1 - e^{-\kappa(T-t)})^2 $$

2. Maturity Limit ($T \to t$): Since $f(T, T) = r(T)$, taking the conditional expectation under the risk-neutral measure $\mathbb{Q}$ gives: $$ \mathbb{E}^{\mathbb{Q}}_t [f(T, T)] = \mathbb{E}^{\mathbb{Q}}_t [r(T)] = r(t)e^{-\kappa(T-t)} + \theta(1 - e^{-\kappa(T-t)}) $$

3. Convexity Adjustment: The difference is: $$ \gamma^*(t, T) = \mathbb{E}^{\mathbb{Q}}_t [f(T, T)] - f(t, T) = \frac{\sigma^2}{2\kappa^2}(1 - e^{-\kappa(T-t)})^2 $$

4. Numerical Application ($t=0, T=1$): - Volatility: $\sigma = 0.01$ - Mean reversion: $\kappa = 0.86$ $$ \gamma^*(0, 1) = \frac{0.01^2}{2 \times 0.86^2}(1 - e^{-0.86})^2 \approx 6.7603 \times 10^{-5} \times (0.5768)^2 \approx 2.249 \times 10^{-5} \text{ (or } \mathbf{0.22 \text{ bps}} \text{)} $$

Correct Answer: 0.22 bps.

Question 2 Multiple Choice

2. Consider a cap and a floor with the same cap/floor strike rate and identical tenor structure. Denote their value by $Cp(0)$ and $Fl(0)$ and suppose that the cap is out-of-the-money. Which of the following statements is true?

Show Mathematical Solution & Derivation

Theoretical Explanation:

1. Cap-Floor Parity: The algebraic relationship between caps, floors, and swaps is: $$ Cp(0) - Fl(0) = V_{swap}(0) = A(0)(R_{swap}(0) - K) $$ where $A(0) = \sum_{i=1}^M \delta P(0, T_i) > 0$ is the Annuity factor, $R_{swap}(0)$ is the spot par swap rate, and $K$ is the strike rate.

2. Moneyness Condition: If the cap is out-of-the-money (OTM), the strike rate $K$ exceeds the par interest rate $R_{swap}(0)$: $$ K > R_{swap}(0) \implies R_{swap}(0) - K < 0 $$

3. Inequality Result: Because the swap value is negative, we have: $$ Cp(0) - Fl(0) < 0 \implies Cp(0) < Fl(0) $$

Correct Option: $Cp(0) < Fl(0)$.

Question 3 True / False

3. Is the following statement true or false?
"An out-of-the-money cap can contain in-the-money caplets."

Show Mathematical Solution & Derivation

Theoretical Explanation:

A cap is a portfolio of caplets. The overall moneyness of the cap is determined by comparing its strike $K$ with the spot par swap rate $R_{swap}(0)$:

  • If $K > R_{swap}(0)$, the cap is out-of-the-money.

However, the moneyness of each individual caplet $i$ depends on its corresponding forward rate $F(0, T_{i-1}, T_i)$:

  • Caplet $i$ is in-the-money if $F(0, T_{i-1}, T_i) > K$.

In an upward-sloping yield curve, the forward rates for later periods can be significantly higher than the average spot swap rate: $F(0, T_{i-1}, T_i) > R_{swap}(0)$. If the curve is steep enough, there will be later forward rates that exceed the strike $K$, making their individual caplets in-the-money, even though the overall cap is out-of-the-money because $K > R_{swap}(0)$.

Correct Answer: True.

Multi-Convention Pricing Solution:

This problem has two standard interpretations depending on whether the first period's caplet is included:

Interpretation 1: Exclude First Caplet (Standard Market Convention)

In the market, the first caplet resetting at $T_0=0$ is excluded because its floating rate $F(0, 0, 0.25) = 6\%$ is already known at $t=0$, making it a deterministic swap payment rather than an option. The cap consists of 7 caplets for $i=2, \dots, 8$.

Step-by-Step Proof of Swaption/Cap Parity Identity:

1. Cap-Floor Parity: The relationship is: $$ Cp(0) - Fl(0) = V_{swap}(0) $$ where $V_{swap}(0)$ is the value of the spot/forwardpayer interest rate swap.

2. Payer-Receiver Swaption Parity: The swaption parity states: $$ Swpt_p(0) - Swpt_r(0) = V_{swap}(0) $$ Since both left-hand-side differences equal the same forward swap value $V_{swap}(0)$, they must be equal!

3. Unified Parity Equation: $$ Cp(0) - Fl(0) = Swpt_p(0) - Swpt_r(0) $$

4. Numerical Solution: - Payer Swaption: $Swpt_p(0) = 1.50\%$ - Receiver Swaption: $Swpt_r(0) = 1.80\%$ - Cap: $Cp(0) = 2.00\%$ $$ 2.00\% - Fl(0) = 1.50\% - 1.80\% = -0.30\% $$ $$ Fl(0) = 2.00\% - (-0.30\%) = \mathbf{2.30\%} $$

Step-by-Step ZCB Put Option Pricing Proof:

A cap consists of multiple caplets. In the Vasicek model, caplets are priced as put options on zero-coupon bonds. A caplet resetting at $T_{i-1}$ and paying at $T_i$ with strike $\kappa$ is equivalent to $1+\delta\kappa$ put options on the ZCB $P(\cdot, T_i)$ expiring at $T_{i-1}$ with strike $X = \frac{1}{1 + \delta\kappa}$.

We evaluate this numerically using two standard interpretations for the ATM strike rate $\kappa$:

Interpretation 1: Exclude First Caplet (Standard market convention)

1. Forward Strike: Excludes the first period's deterministic option. The ATM par swap rate is: $$ K \approx \mathbf{9.0054\%} \implies X = \frac{1}{1 + 0.25 \times 0.090054} \approx 0.977988 $$

2. Analytical Put Pricing: We price each of the 119 puts for $i=1, \dots, 119$ using: $$ \text{Put}_i = X P(0, T_{i-1}) \Phi(-d_2) - P(0, T_i) \Phi(-d_1) $$ $$ \sigma_{p, i} = \sigma_V B(T_{i-1}, T_i) \sqrt{\frac{1 - e^{-2\kappa T_{i-1}}}{2\kappa}}, \quad B(\tau) = \frac{1 - e^{-\kappa\tau}}{\kappa} $$

3. Result: Summing over all 119 caplets yields: $$ \text{Total Price} \approx \mathbf{4.16\%} $$

Interpretation 2: Include First Caplet (Textbook convention)

1. Strike: Includes the first period (deterministic payoff is 0 since $F(0, 0, 0.25) \approx 8.18\% < 8.9859\%$). The ATM strike is: $$ K \approx \mathbf{8.9859\%} $$

2. Result: Summing over all 120 caplets yields: $$ \text{Total Price} \approx \mathbf{4.26\%} $$

Accepted Answers: 4.16% (standard) or 4.26% (alternative).

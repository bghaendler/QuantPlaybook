4. Cox-Ingersoll-Ross (CIR) Model

To avoid negative interest rates, the CIR model scales the volatility by the square root of the rate:

$$ dr(t) = \kappa(\theta - r(t))dt + \sigma\sqrt{r(t)} dW^*(t) $$

Assuming the Feller condition $2\kappa\theta \ge \sigma^2$, the rate strictly stays positive. Like Vasicek, the CIR model also yields exponential affine bond prices:

$$ P(t, T) = e^{A(T-t) - B(T-t)r(t)} $$

The Riccati equations change slightly due to the new volatility structure, but $A(t)$ and $B(t)$ still have explicit closed-form expressions involving hyperbolic functions.

Plain English Notes

The Big Picture: An elegant upgrade to the Vasicek model that inherently prevents negative interest rates.

  • Square-Root Volatility: As the interest rate approaches zero, the noise ($\sigma \sqrt{r(t)}$) also drops to zero. The process "calms down" near the boundary, preventing it from crossing into negative territory.
  • Example: Imagine a bouncing ball that loses its bounciness as it gets closer to the floor. The mean reversion pulls it up, and the vanishing volatility ensures it can never fall through the floor!

Practice Exercise: The CIR Limiting Distribution

The limiting distribution of the CIR model is a Gamma distribution. Compare the limiting variances of the Vasicek and CIR models using the parameters $\kappa = 0.5$, $\theta = 0.04$, and $\sigma = 0.15$.

👉 Show Step-by-Step Solution
  1. Vasicek Variance Formula: $Var(r(\infty)) = \frac{\sigma^2}{2\kappa}$
  2. Vasicek Variance = $\frac{0.15^2}{2 \times 0.5} = \frac{0.0225}{1} = 0.0225$
  3. CIR Variance Formula: $Var(r(\infty)) = \frac{\sigma^2 \theta}{2\kappa}$
  4. CIR Variance = $\frac{0.15^2 \times 0.04}{2 \times 0.5} = 0.0225 \times 0.04 = 0.0009$

Conclusion: The CIR variance ($0.0009$) is vastly smaller than the Vasicek variance ($0.0225$) for the same parameters! Because the CIR model scales noise by $\sqrt{r(t)}$, and the long-term rate $\theta$ is small ($0.04$), the effective volatility dampens significantly over time, creating a much tighter distribution.

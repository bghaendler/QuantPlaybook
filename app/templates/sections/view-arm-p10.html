Advanced Risk Management

Multi-Asset VaR Projection Mechanics

Scaling Part 08 to real portfolios: covariance engineering, factor compression, the full simulation pipeline, and risk attribution back to positions.

1. The Covariance Matrix Is the Hard Part

For $n$ assets, $\Sigma$ has $n(n+1)/2$ parameters — at $n = 500$, that's 125k numbers from perhaps 500 daily observations: the sample covariance is singular for $n > T$ and noisy long before. Standard repairs:

  • Factor models: $\Sigma = B\,\Sigma_F\,B^\top + D$ — market/sector/style factors (or PCA factors, cf. the term-structure course) reduce dimension from $n^2$ to $n \times k$; simulation then draws $k$ factors + idiosyncratics.
  • Shrinkage (Ledoit-Wolf): $\hat\Sigma = \delta F + (1-\delta)S$ toward a structured target — optimal δ in closed form; the default fix for mid-size books.
  • EWMA/DCC layers: time-varying vols (filtered HS logic, Part 05) with slower-moving correlations.
  • PSD repair: stress overlays and pairwise estimates break positive semi-definiteness — eigenvalue clipping or Higham's nearest-correlation algorithm before Cholesky (the correlation-tool section demonstrates the failure live).

2. The Pipeline at Scale

  1. Map positions → risk factors (the unglamorous 80%): equities to betas, bonds to key-rate buckets, options to underliers + vol surface nodes.
  2. Estimate the factor covariance (above) on the chosen window/weighting.
  3. Decompose: Cholesky $L$ ($\Sigma = LL^\top$) — or eigen-decomposition when near-singular; for $Z \in \mathbb{R}^{N \times k}$ standard normals, correlated draws are $ZL^\top$ (one BLAS call).
  4. Revalue: full pricing for nonlinear positions, sensitivities for verified-linear ones; this dominates runtime — hence grids/AAD/GPU (the AD masterclass and GPU workshop exist for this step).
  5. Aggregate: portfolio P&L per scenario → VaR/ES quantiles, with sampling bands (Part 08 discipline).

Upgrades drop in modularly: t or filtered-historical innovations (Parts 03/05), copulas for tail dependence beyond Gaussian (Part 06's warning — Gaussian correlation has zero tail dependence), stressed parameter sets (Part 07).

3. Attribution: Whose Risk Is It?

Euler decomposition allocates portfolio risk to positions exactly (for homogeneous measures like volatility, VaR, ES):

$$\mathrm{VaR} = \sum_i w_i\,\frac{\partial \mathrm{VaR}}{\partial w_i}, \qquad \text{component VaR}_i = w_i \cdot \frac{\mathrm{cov}(r_i, r_p)}{\sigma_p}\cdot\frac{\mathrm{VaR}}{\sigma_p}\;(\text{Gaussian case}).$$
  • Marginal VaR (∂VaR/∂w) ranks the next dollar; component VaR sums to the total — the desk report's two columns.
  • In simulation, estimate ES contributions as $\mathbb{E}[w_ir_i \mid L > \mathrm{VaR}]$ — average each position's P&L over tail scenarios; noisy (few tail draws) — use the kernel-smoothed or importance-sampled versions.
  • Attribution is what converts a VaR number into VaR management: limits, hedging priorities, and the diversification accounting of Part 02's cascade.

4. Validation Checklist for a Multi-Asset Engine

  • Limiting cases: ρ→identity ⇒ sum of standalone VaRs; one-asset reduction matches Part 08; Gaussian-linear book matches the analytic formula.
  • PSD & conditioning: smallest eigenvalue logged each run; condition number alerts before Cholesky failures.
  • Backtest at portfolio and sub-portfolio level (Part 07 tests) — aggregation can hide offsetting sub-book failures.
  • Stress the correlation: ±0.2 parallel shifts and "crisis ρ" overlays — if VaR moves more with ρ than with vols, say so in the report (it usually does for diversified books).

Key Takeaways

  • At scale the covariance matrix is the model: factors + shrinkage + PSD repair before any simulation.
  • Pipeline: factor-map → estimate → Cholesky → revalue (the cost center) → quantile, with modular non-Gaussian upgrades.
  • Euler/component decomposition turns the headline number into per-desk management information.
  • Validate against analytic limits, log eigenvalues, backtest sub-books, and stress ρ — correlation drives diversified-book VaR.

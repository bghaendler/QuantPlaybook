2.2 Exact Methods & Underdetermined Systems

Solving for the smoothest exact-matching discount curve in underdetermined systems using the Pseudoinverse of Weighted Increments.

The Underdetermined Cash Flow System

When pricing fixed-income securities, we observe $n$ market instruments (e.g., $n = 9$ Gilt bonds) but must resolve the discount factors for all unique cash flow dates $t_j$ ($N = 104$ dates). This results in an underdetermined linear system:

$$ C d = p \quad (n \ll N) $$

Since there are infinite curves that satisfy this matching equation, we need a mathematical regularization criteria to pick the "best" or "smoothest" curve. This is achieved by minimizing the Euclidean norm of weighted increments.

Weighted Increments Minimization

We transform the system by looking at the first differences of the discount factors scaled by the time intervals $\delta_j = T_j - T_{j-1}$:

$$ \Delta_j = \frac{P(0, T_j) - P(0, T_{j-1})}{\sqrt{\delta_j}} $$

The pseudoinverse solution $\Delta^* = A^\top (AA^\top)^{-1} (p - C \mathbf{1})$ minimizes the sum of squared increments, which acts as a proxy for the continuous smoothness integral $\int |P'(T)|^2 dT$.

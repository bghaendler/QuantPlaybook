Maths Primer Interest Rates

Module 1.1: Interest Rates & Discount Bonds

Core definitions, compounding frequencies, yield curves, the short rate, money market accounts, and discount factors.
Interest rates represent the cost of borrowing capital. Compounding frequency determines how rates are quoted, scaling from simple linear conventions (LIBOR) to continuous logarithmic limits.

Compounding Frequencies

Interest represents the rent paid by a borrower of money to a lender over a period of time $[t, T]$. The compounding frequency determines how we annualize and quote this return:

Simple Rate $L(t, T)$

$$ 1 + R = 1 + (T - t)L(t, T) $$

Continuous $y(t, T)$

$$ 1 + R = e^{(T - t)y(t, T)} $$

For any positive interest return ($R > 0$), simple compounding interest rates are mathematically higher than their continuously compounded yield counterparts: $L(t, T) > y(t, T)$.

$n$-Fold Compounding

We define the $n$-fold compounded interest rate $y_n(t, T)$ corresponding to the lending period $[t, T]$ through:

$$ P(t, T) = \left( 1 + \frac{y_n(t, T)}{n} \right)^{-n(T-t)} $$

Elementary calculus shows that as the compounding frequency $n$ approaches infinity, we obtain continuous compounding as the limit: $\lim_{n \to \infty} y_n(t, T) = y(t, T)$.

The Money Market Account

Investing 1 unit of currency at the short rate $r(t)$, and continuously reinvesting it, grows into the money market account $B(t)$:

$$ B(t) = e^{\int_0^t r(s) ds} $$

The Money Market Account is a risk-free asset because its return over $[t, t+dt]$ is determined by $r(t)$ which is known at the beginning of the time interval (locally previsible and lacking local volatility).

Term Structure & Short Rate

  • Term Structure: Prevailing term structure is obtained by freezing $t$ and varying $T$. Economic fluctuations cause this curve to shift, twist, and bend.
  • Short Rate $r(t)$: At the short end, compounding frequencies coincide: $$ \lim_{T \to t} L(t, T) = \lim_{T \to t} y(t, T) = r(t) $$
  • Discount Bond: Zero-coupon bond pays 1 at maturity $T$. Its price curve represents the discount curve, typically smooth, decreasing, and satisfying $P(T, T) = 1$.

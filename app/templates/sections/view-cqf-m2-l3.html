Coherent Risk Measures, Cornish-Fisher Quantiles, Subadditivity Violations, and Backtesting

1. Conceptual Foundations of Risk

To establish risk frameworks, we distinguish between quantifiable models and fundamental uncertainties:

  • Knightian Uncertainty (Frank Knight, 1921): Risk describes situations where outcomes and their exact probabilities are known. Uncertainty occurs when probabilities are unknown and unmeasurable.
  • Black Swan Events (Taleb): Rare, highly consequential, and completely unpredictable events (unknown unknowns) rather than conceivable tail events (known unknowns).
  • Non-Quantitative Pillars (Thomas Coleman): True risk management is heavily psychological and operational (people, corporate behavior, and cognitive biases) rather than purely numerical.

Prevalent Psychological Biases:

Overconfidence: Prevalent among managers, leading to highly under-hedged positions.
Base Rate Neglect: Ignoring the general probability of a population in favor of specific salient details.

2. Coherent Risk Measures (Artzner et al.)

A risk measure $\mathcal{R}$ is mathematically classified as coherent if it satisfies four axioms:

1. Monotonicity: If portfolio $Y \ge X$ in all states, risk must be lower: $\mathcal{R}(Y) \le \mathcal{R}(X)$.
2. Subadditivity: Combined portfolio risk cannot exceed individual risks: $\mathcal{R}(X+Y) \le \mathcal{R}(X) + \mathcal{R}(Y)$.
3. Positive Homogeneity: Scaling size scales risk: $\mathcal{R}(hX) = h\mathcal{R}(X)$ for $h \ge 0$.
4. Translational Invariance: Adding cash $n$ reduces risk: $\mathcal{R}(X+n) = \mathcal{R}(X) - n$.

Value at Risk & Expected Shortfall Formulations

1. Parametric (Delta-Normal) VaR

Under normality assumption, P&L is $X \sim N(\mu, \sigma^2)$:

$$\text{VaR}_c(X) = \mu + \Phi^{-1}(1 - c)\sigma$$

2. Derivation of Expected Shortfall under Normality

Expected Shortfall is defined as the average tail loss: $\text{ES}_c(X) = E[X \mid X \le \text{VaR}_c(X)]$. Substituting $\text{VaR}_u$ and integrating:

$$\text{ES}_c(X) = \frac{1}{1-c} \int_0^{1-c} \left( \mu + \sigma \Phi^{-1}(1-u) \right) du = \mu - \sigma \frac{\phi\left(\Phi^{-1}(c)\right)}{1-c}$$

3. Cornish-Fisher Non-Normal Expansion

For non-normal distributions, standard percentiles ($Z$) are adjusted using skewness ($S$) and excess kurtosis ($K$) to calculate $Z^*$:

$$Z^* = Z + \frac{S}{6}(Z^2 - 1) + \frac{K}{24}(Z^3 - 3Z) - \frac{S^2}{36}(2Z^3 - 5Z)$$

Specifically, the left-tail $97.5\%$ quantile becomes: $Z^* = -1.96 + 0.474 S - 0.0687 K - 0.146 S^2$.

Historical & Monte Carlo Estimation

To compute risk metrics in practical finance, we evaluate three standard loss distribution estimation structures:

1. Parametric (Variance-Covariance)

Assumes risk factors follow a parametric distribution (typically normal) and projects quantiles using simple analytic parameters.

2. Historical Simulation (Non-Parametric)

Revalues current portfolio positions using actual historical price records (e.g. past 10 years). It ranks continuously compounded returns and isolates the exact empirical percentile without distribution assumptions.

3. Monte Carlo Simulation (Stochastic)

Generates thousands of randomized future price paths by calibrating stochastic processes (such as GBM). It rankings simulated paths to map tail percentiles, introducing model risk but capturing complex options horizons.

Backtesting Framework & Basel Regulatory Zones

Backtesting involves validating VaR models by tracking the number of daily trading exceptions (where actual portfolio losses exceed the estimated VaR threshold) over a standard 250-day window:

🟢 Green Zone

Exceptions: 0 to 4

Model is considered fully accurate. Standard capital requirement factor is applied.

🟡 Yellow Zone

Exceptions: 5 to 9

Regulators flag warning. Standard capital requirement multiplier is scaled up.

🔴 Red Zone

Exceptions: 10+

Model is statistically flawed. Immediate recalibration required; massive capital penalties.

📖 Real-World Case Study (Deutsche Bank, 2024): Deutsche Bank reported only 2 exceptions in 2024 (Solidly Green Zone). During the extreme pandemic lockdown market regime of 2020, they recorded 7 exceptions (Yellow warning), though regulators universally waived capital penalties due to the unprecedented external shock.

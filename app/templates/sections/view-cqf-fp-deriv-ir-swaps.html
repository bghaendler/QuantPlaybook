2-Year Semi-Annual EURIBOR Swap

In a classic interest rate swap, coupons are paid periodically (e.g. semi-annually) based on a Term Reference Rate like EURIBOR. Reference rates represent the averages at which European banks lend to one another across standard tenors.

1-Week Compound GBP SONIA OIS

In an Overnight Index Swap (OIS), the floating rate payer compounding daily index values published daily by central banks (e.g. GBP SONIA, USD SOFR, EUR €STR) across the accrual period. Fixed rate payments are known upfront.

GBP SONIA 1-Week Example: Notional principal is £100,000,000. Fixed rate is locked at 4.75% p.a. (7 days). Floating rate references daily compounding of overnight SONIA. Liability Hedging with Payer Swaps

Corporates with floating-rate debt can hedge against rising interest rates using a **Payer Swap** (paying fixed, receiving floating).

Explanation: The Corporate receives floating interest from the Swap Trader, which perfectly offsets their floating interest liability owed to the Lender Bank. The only net unhedged cash outflow remaining for the Corporate is the **4.52% Fixed Swap Rate**, converting their volatile floating loan into a highly predictable fixed liability.

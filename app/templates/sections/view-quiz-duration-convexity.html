Question 1 (ZCB Macaulay Duration) Multiple Choice

In continuous time, what is the Macaulay duration of a zero-coupon bond with maturity $T$? (Assume today is $t = 0$ as in the simplified notation of the slides).

Show Mathematical Solution & Derivation

Step-by-Step Mathematical Derivation:

The price of a zero-coupon bond paying $1$ at maturity $T$ under yield $y$ is:

$$ p(s) = e^{-(y + s)T} $$

At $s = 0$, $p(0) = e^{-y T}$. We find the relative first-order sensitivity (duration) $D$ by differentiating with respect to the parallel shift $s$:

$$ \frac{\partial p(s)}{\partial s} = -T e^{-(y + s)T} $$ $$ \frac{\partial p(s)}{\partial s} \Big|_{s=0} = -T e^{-y T} $$

Plugging this derivative into the duration definition:

$$ D = -\frac{1}{p(0)} \frac{\partial p(s)}{\partial s} \Big|_{s=0} = -\frac{1}{e^{-y T}} \bigl( -T e^{-y T} \bigr) = \mathbf{T} $$

Thus, the Macaulay duration of a ZCB is exactly equal to its maturity $T$.

Correct Option: C. T

Question 2 (Relative Price Sensitivity) Numeric Input

Revisit Example 1 from the slides: a 10-year 6% coupon bond under a flat 3% yield curve has a price of $125.14$ and a duration $D = 8.06$. If the yield curve shifts parallelly upwards by $+100$ basis points ($\Delta y = +1.00\% = 0.01$), what is the first-order estimated percentage change in the bond's price? Express your answer in percentage points (e.g. -8.06 for -8.06%) and round to two decimal places.

Show Mathematical Solution & Derivation

Step-by-Step Mathematical Explanation:

The first-order Taylor approximation for relative price changes under parallel yield shift $\Delta y$ is:

$$ \frac{\Delta p}{p} \approx -D \Delta y $$

Given $D = 8.06$ and yield shift $\Delta y = +0.01$ (upward $+100$ basis points):

$$ \frac{\Delta p}{p} \approx -8.06 \times 0.01 = -0.0806 \text{ (or } \mathbf{-8.06\%}\text{)} $$

So the relative bond price decline is estimated to be exactly -8.06%.

Correct Answer: -8.06

Question 3 (Duration Immunization Matching) Numeric Input

You hold a long position in one unit of the 10-year 6% bond ($\Pi(0) = 125.14$, $D_{\Pi} = 8.06$). You wish to immunize this position against small parallel yield shifts using a 5-year 3% coupon bond ($H(0) = 99.79$, $D_H = 4.72$). Calculate the quantity $q$ of the 5-year bond you must hold. Round your answer to two decimal places.

Show Mathematical Solution & Derivation

Step-by-Step Mathematical Derivation:

To immunize the portfolio against small parallel shifts of the yield curve, the net first-order price sensitivity must be zero. Let the hedge portfolio value be $V(s) = \Pi(s) + q H(s)$. Differentiating with respect to parallel shift $s$ at $s = 0$ gives:

$$ \frac{\partial V(s)}{\partial s} \Big|_{s=0} = \frac{\partial \Pi(s)}{\partial s} \Big|_{s=0} + q \frac{\partial H(s)}{\partial s} \Big|_{s=0} = 0 $$

Recall the definition of duration: $\frac{\partial p}{\partial s}\big|_{0} = -D \times p(0)$. Substituting this in yields:

$$ -D_{\Pi} \Pi(0) - q D_H H(0) = 0 $$

Solving for the hedge quantity $q$:

$$ q = -\frac{D_{\Pi} \Pi(0)}{D_H H(0)} $$

Substituting the given bond specs:

$$ q = -\frac{8.06 \times 125.14}{4.72 \times 99.79} = -\frac{1008.6284}{471.0088} \approx \mathbf{-2.14} $$

So you must short exactly 2.14 units of the 5-year bond to immunize the position.

Correct Answer: -2.14

Question 4 (Second-Order Approximation) Numeric Input

Consider a bond with price $p = 100$, duration $D = 6.76$, and convexity $C = 85.329$. If the yield curve shifts parallelly upwards by $+2.00\%$ ($\Delta y = 0.02$), estimate the percentage change in the bond's price using the second-order (duration and convexity) Taylor approximation. Express your answer in percentage points (e.g. -11.81 for -11.81%) and round to two decimal places.

Show Mathematical Solution & Derivation

Step-by-Step Mathematical Derivation:

The second-order Taylor series approximation for relative price changes under parallel yield shift $\Delta y$ is:

$$ \frac{\Delta p}{p} \approx -D \Delta y + \frac{1}{2} C (\Delta y)^2 $$

Substitute $D = 6.76$, $C = 85.329$, and $\Delta y = 0.02$ into the formula:

$$ \frac{\Delta p}{p} \approx -6.76 \times 0.02 + 0.5 \times 85.329 \times (0.02)^2 $$ $$ \frac{\Delta p}{p} \approx -0.1352 + 42.6645 \times 0.0004 $$ $$ \frac{\Delta p}{p} \approx -0.1352 + 0.0170658 = -0.1181342 \text{ (or } \mathbf{-11.81\%}\text{)} $$

So the estimated price change is exactly -11.81%.

Correct Answer: -11.81

Question 5 (Convexity Hedging System) Multiple Choice

Revisit the convexity hedging slide. You have a portfolio $\Pi$ to be hedged with price $32,863.5$, duration $6.76$, and convexity $85.329$. You have two hedging instruments:
• $H_1$: price $97.962$, duration $8.813$, convexity $99.081$.
• $H_2$: price $108.039$, duration $2.704$, convexity $10.168$.
Which of the following is the correct linear system of equations to solve for the hedging units $(q_1, q_2)$?

Show Mathematical Solution & Derivation

Step-by-Step Mathematical Explanation:

Let the immunized portfolio value be $V(s) = \Pi(s) + q_1 H_1(s) + q_2 H_2(s)$. To immunize against both first and second-order parallel shifts, we require:

$$ \frac{\partial V(s)}{\partial s} \Big|_{s=0} = 0 \quad \text{and} \quad \frac{\partial^2 V(s)}{\partial s^2} \Big|_{s=0} = 0 $$

Recall the definitions of duration $D$ and convexity $C$ for any asset $A$:

$$ \frac{\partial A}{\partial s} \Big|_0 = -D_A A(0) \quad \text{and} \quad \frac{\partial^2 A}{\partial s^2} \Big|_0 = C_A A(0) $$

Substituting these into the portfolio sensitivity requirements gives:

  1. First-Order Sensitivity (Duration): $$ -D_{\Pi}\Pi(0) - q_1 D_{H_1}H_1(0) - q_2 D_{H_2}H_2(0) = 0 $$ $$ \implies -q_1 D_{H_1}H_1(0) - q_2 D_{H_2}H_2(0) = D_{\Pi}\Pi(0) $$
  2. Second-Order Sensitivity (Convexity): $$ C_{\Pi}\Pi(0) + q_1 C_{H_1}H_1(0) + q_2 C_{H_2}H_2(0) = 0 $$ $$ \implies q_1 C_{H_1}H_1(0) + q_2 C_{H_2}H_2(0) = -C_{\Pi}\Pi(0) $$

This is exactly the system shown in Option A.

Correct Option: A

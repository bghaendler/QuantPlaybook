1.12 Covariance & Correlation

Joint dependencies, correlation boundaries, and dimensionless Pearson coefficients.

1.12.1 Covariance & Pearson Coefficents

Covariance measures the joint linear statistical dependence between two random variables $X$ and $Y$:

$$\text{Cov}(X,Y) = \mathbb{E}[(X - \mathbb{E}[X])(Y - \mathbb{E}[Y])] = \mathbb{E}[XY] - \mathbb{E}[X]\mathbb{E}[Y]$$

To eliminate units and obtain a standardized, dimensionless measure of association, we define the Pearson Correlation Coefficient $\rho_{XY}$:

$$\rho_{XY} = \frac{\text{Cov}(X,Y)}{\sigma_X \sigma_Y} \quad \text{where} \quad -1 \le \rho_{XY} \le 1$$

  • $\rho_{XY} = 1$: Perfect positive linear correlation.
  • $\rho_{XY} = -1$: Perfect negative linear correlation.
  • $\rho_{XY} = 0$: Linearly uncorrelated.

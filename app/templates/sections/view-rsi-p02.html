Risk-Sensitive Investing — Part 02

Risk-Sensitive Control vs. Mean-Variance Optimization

The risk-sensitive criterion — exponential-of-growth utility — as the dynamic, higher-moment-aware upgrade of Markowitz, and the precise sense in which it nests both Kelly and mean-variance.

1. The Risk-Sensitive Criterion

Maximize the long-run risk-adjusted growth rate:

$$J_\theta = \liminf_{T\to\infty}\; -\frac{2}{\theta T}\,\ln\,\mathbb{E}\Big[e^{-\frac{\theta}{2}\ln W_T}\Big],$$

with risk-sensitivity θ > 0. Expanding the cumulant generating function for small θ:

$$J_\theta \;\approx\; \underbrace{\text{growth rate}}_{\text{Kelly}} \;-\; \frac{\theta}{4}\,\underbrace{\text{variance of growth}}_{\text{penalty}} \;+\; O(\theta^2\,\text{higher cumulants}),$$

— mean-variance on log-wealth growth, plus automatic penalties on skewness/kurtosis through the higher cumulants: the exponential never truncates the expansion. θ → 0 recovers Kelly (pure growth); larger θ behaves like an effective risk aversion γ = 1 + θ/2 (fractional Kelly — Part 03's bridge).

2. What It Fixes Relative to Markowitz

Mean-variance (one period)Risk-sensitive control
HorizonSingle period, then re-solvedDynamic, infinite-horizon ergodic criterion
CompoundingArithmetic returns (ignores vol drag)Log-wealth: $-\sigma^2/2$ drag priced in natively
Higher momentsAbsent (quadratic utility)All cumulants penalized via the exponential
Time consistencyDynamic MV is time-inconsistent (pre-commitment vs. recursive)Time-consistent by construction (it's an HJB)
Factor dynamicsExogenous estimatesStates in the control problem (hedging demands)

3. The Solution Structure (Bielecki-Pliska / Davis-Lleo)

With affine factor dynamics $dx = (a + Ax)dt + \Lambda dW$ driving expected returns, the risk-sensitive HJB reduces to a matrix Riccati ODE — the same equation family as Kalman filters and affine term-structure models (MM Part 12's lineage). The optimal portfolio:

$$\pi^*_t = \frac{1}{1 + \theta/2}\,\Sigma^{-1}\Big[(\mu(x_t) - r) \;+\; \text{(hedging term from the Riccati solution)}\Big],$$

— a scaled Merton weight plus a factor-hedging tilt. Everything from Part 01 carries over with γ replaced by 1 + θ/2; the criterion's ergodic nature makes the weights stationary policies rather than horizon-dependent schedules (the Part 07 comparison).

4. Practice Notes

  • Calibrating θ: back it out from a target — long-run vol of the strategy, acceptable 10-year drawdown quantile, or matching a fractional-Kelly habit (θ = 2(1/f − 1) for fraction f) — more communicable than "γ = 3".
  • Estimation risk dominates: $\Sigma^{-1}(\mu - r)$ amplifies μ errors exactly like Markowitz — the cure is identical (shrinkage, Black-Litterman priors: Part 04), the criterion does not exempt you.
  • Where it shines: multi-period institutional mandates with factor predictability (the hedging demands are the value-add) and leverage-sensitive strategies (vol drag and tail cumulants priced natively — cf. Part 06's jump extensions).
  • Diagnostics: simulate the policy and check the realized growth-vs-variance trade sits on the θ-frontier; off-frontier results flag mis-specified factor dynamics.

Key Takeaways

  • Risk-sensitive control = exponential utility on growth: Kelly minus θ/4 × growth variance minus higher-cumulant penalties.
  • It nests Kelly (θ→0) and behaves as fractional Kelly / CRRA with γ = 1 + θ/2 — one dial spans the family.
  • Affine factors ⇒ Riccati ODEs ⇒ Merton weights scaled and tilted by hedging demands; time-consistent, unlike dynamic MV.
  • θ is communicable via drawdown/vol targets; estimation risk still rules — pair with shrinkage/BL priors.

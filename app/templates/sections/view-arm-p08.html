Monte Carlo VaR Projection for Two Assets

The minimal complete example of simulation-based VaR: two correlated assets, Cholesky, full revaluation, quantile extraction — every multi-asset engine is this, scaled up.

1. Setup and Algorithm

Portfolio: $w_1$ in asset 1, $w_2$ in asset 2; daily vols $\sigma_1, \sigma_2$, correlation $\rho$ (estimated per the equities course; or t-innovations per Part 03). The engine:

  1. Draw $Z_1, Z_2 \sim \mathcal{N}(0,1)$ i.i.d. (inverse-CDF; seeds fixed — Part 09 hygiene from MM).
  2. Correlate (Cholesky): $\;\epsilon_1 = Z_1, \quad \epsilon_2 = \rho Z_1 + \sqrt{1 - \rho^2}\,Z_2$.
  3. Generate returns $r_i = \mu_i + \sigma_i\epsilon_i$ (μ ≈ 0 at one day) — or full GBM/t variants.
  4. Revalue: $\Delta V = w_1V_1(e^{r_1} - 1) + w_2V_2(e^{r_2} - 1)$ (full revaluation; options through their pricers).
  5. Repeat $N$ = 100k times; VaR₉₉ = −(1st percentile of ΔV), ES = mean beyond.

2. Worked Numbers

$5m each in two assets; $\sigma_1 = 1.6\%$, $\sigma_2 = 1.0\%$ daily, $\rho = 0.3$. Portfolio daily vol:

$$\sigma_p = \sqrt{w_1^2\sigma_1^2 + w_2^2\sigma_2^2 + 2\rho w_1w_2\sigma_1\sigma_2}\;(\text{in \$}) = \sqrt{80^2 + 50^2 + 2(.3)(80)(50)}\,\text{k} \approx \$106.6\text{k}.$$

Analytic check: VaR₉₉ ≈ 2.326 × 106.6k ≈ $248k; the Gaussian MC must reproduce this within its standard error (~±1% at 100k paths) — always validate the engine on the case with a closed form before trusting it where there isn't one. Diversification visible: undiversified sum of standalone VaRs ≈ 2.326×(80+50)k = $302k ⇒ benefit ≈ $54k; at ρ = 1 the benefit vanishes, at ρ = −0.5, σ_p ≈ \$70k — re-run the engine across ρ to see correlation as the third asset.

3. Why Bother With MC When the Formula Exists?

  • Nonlinearity: add one option and the analytic quantile dies; MC's full revaluation prices the gamma/vega into the tail correctly (delta-normal VaR on an option book understates short-gamma risk badly).
  • Non-Gaussian inputs: swap step 1 for t-draws or filtered-historical innovations — the engine is unchanged (the Part 03/05 upgrades drop in).
  • Horizon flexibility: path-dependent revaluation over 10 days with intermediate margining/rebalancing — impossible in closed form.
  • The cost: sampling noise (quantify: bootstrap the P&L vector or use the binomial band on the tail count) and model risk now twice — in dynamics and in correlation (Part 09 scrutinizes the generator; Part 10 scales the correlation machinery).

4. Implementation Notes (Spreadsheet → Code)

rng = np.random.default_rng(7)
Z = rng.standard_normal((100_000, 2))
eps1 = Z[:,0]; eps2 = rho*Z[:,0] + np.sqrt(1-rho**2)*Z[:,1]
pnl = w1*(np.exp(s1*eps1)-1) + w2*(np.exp(s2*eps2)-1)
var99 = -np.percentile(pnl, 1)
es99  = -pnl[pnl <= -var99].mean()
se    = pnl.std() / np.sqrt(len(pnl))     # report it

Excel version: two NORM.S.INV(RAND()) columns, the Cholesky combination in a third, P&L in a fourth, PERCENTILE.INC(range, 0.01) at the bottom — a Data-Table re-run gives the sampling distribution of VaR itself (MM Part 14 mechanics). Antithetics and Sobol sequences cut the tail-quantile noise ~2–5× at no model cost.

Key Takeaways

  • Two assets contain the whole method: draw → Cholesky-correlate → revalue → quantile.
  • Validate against the closed-form Gaussian case before adding anything the formula can't do.
  • MC earns its cost on nonlinearity, non-Gaussian inputs and multi-day path effects — not on linear Gaussian books.
  • Report VaR's own sampling error; correlation is a model input as consequential as either volatility.

Plain English Notes

The Big Picture: Monte Carlo VaR projects future portfolio values by simulating thousands of paths for multiple assets, accounting for their correlation and non-linear options payoffs.

  • Simulating P&L: We draw correlated random paths for all assets, revalue the portfolio in each scenario, and extract the 99% loss threshold.
  • Cholesky Decomposition: The mathematical tool used to inject historical correlations into independent random number generators.
  • Non-Linear Risk: Essential for options portfolios where delta, gamma, and vega make price changes non-linear.

Bootstrap Method: Iteratively solve for the discount factor \(P(T_n)\) by matching the present value of swap cash flows to par:

$$ P(T_n) = \frac{1 - S_n \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} \Delta t_i P(T_i)}{1 + S_n \Delta t_n} $$

Pseudoinverse Method: Given the underdetermined system \( M P = b \), introduce a cumulative sum matrix \( C \) and a time-weighted diagonal matrix \( W = \text{diag}(\sqrt{\Delta t}) \). We solve for the perturbation \( y \) that minimizes the first derivative's \( L_2 \) norm:

$$ \min_y \|y\|^2 \quad \text{s.t.} \quad M C W y = b - M \mathbf{1} $$

$$ y = (MCW)^+ (b - M \mathbf{1}) \implies P = \mathbf{1} + C W y $$

Compute the covariance matrix \( \Sigma \) of monthly yield changes \( \Delta Y \). Decompose into eigenvalues \( \lambda_j \) and eigenvectors \( A \):

$$ \Sigma = A \Lambda A^T $$

To approximate the portfolio's monthly price change \( \Delta V \):

$$ \Delta V \approx \frac{\partial V}{\partial t} \Delta t + \sum_{i} \frac{\partial V}{\partial y_i} \Delta y_i $$

Where \( \frac{\partial V}{\partial y_i} = -CF_i \cdot T_i \cdot \exp(-y_i T_i) \). We reconstruct \( \Delta y_i \) using only the first two PCs and calculate the empirical standard deviation of the resulting \( \Delta V \) series.

We build a smoothing spline curve using a specific inner product matrix \( H \) constructed from the maturities \( t_i \):

$$ H_{i,j} = t_i t_j + \frac{1}{2} \min(t_i, t_j)^2 \max(t_i, t_j) - \frac{1}{6} \min(t_i, t_j)^3 $$

Solve the linear system for the weights \( \beta \):

$$ \begin{bmatrix} 0 & T^T \\ T & H + \frac{1}{\alpha} I \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \beta_0 \\ \beta \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ Y \cdot T \end{bmatrix} $$

The interpolated yield at any target maturity \( T \) is given by:

$$ y(T) = \beta_0 + \frac{1}{T} \sum_{i=1}^N \beta_i H(T, T_i) $$

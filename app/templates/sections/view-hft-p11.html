High-Frequency Trading & Market Microstructure

High-Frequency Data Manipulation Labs (MATLAB/Python)

The unglamorous 80%: cleaning tick data, aligning quotes and trades, building bars, and the lab exercises that turn raw tape into research-grade datasets.

1. Raw Tick Data and Its Pathologies

  • Structure: trades (price, size, venue, conditions) and quotes (bid/ask/sizes per venue) as separate event streams; nanosecond timestamps (exchange vs. SIP vs. capture clocks differ — pick one and document it).
  • Standard cleaning rules (Barndorff-Nielsen et al.): drop zero/negative prices and sizes; drop locked/crossed quotes ($b \ge a$); filter condition codes (cancels, corrections, late prints, odd settlement); winsorize prints further than $k$ MADs from a centered rolling median — outliers are usually bad data, but log what you delete.
  • Multi-venue consolidation: build the NBBO by merging per-venue best quotes on the event clock; beware one-venue glitches polluting the consolidated series.

2. Alignment: The As-Of Join

The core operation of microstructure research: attach to each trade the quote prevailing just before it — a backward as-of join (never interpolate quotes forward!):

# Python/pandas
trades = trades.sort_values('ts'); quotes = quotes.sort_values('ts')
merged = pd.merge_asof(trades, quotes, on='ts',
                       direction='backward', tolerance=pd.Timedelta('5s'))
merged['mid'] = (merged.bid + merged.ask) / 2
merged['sign'] = np.where(merged.price > merged.mid, 1,
                  np.where(merged.price < merged.mid, -1, 0))  # Lee-Ready core

% MATLAB: timetable + synchronize(tt_trades, tt_quotes, 'union', 'previous')
% or retime(quotes_tt, trades_times, 'previous')

From the join: effective spreads $2|p - m|$, Lee-Ready trade signs (tick test breaks midpoint ties), and markouts $m_{t+\Delta} - m_t$ per fill — the Part 06 toxicity metrics, three lines from raw tape.

3. Bars: Resampling the Event Stream

  • Time bars (1s/1min): simple, but mix busy and dead intervals — heteroskedastic by construction (Part 05).
  • Tick / volume / dollar bars: sample every $N$ trades / shares / dollars — closer to i.i.d. Gaussian returns (sampling in "business time"); dollar bars are the robust default across price levels.
  • Last-vs-prevailing conventions: bar prices from last trade vs. mid-at-close differ systematically by bounce; use mids for return computation, trades for volume.
  • Signature plot lab: compute RV at 1s/5s/30s/1m/5m sampling — the upward explosion at fine scales is microstructure noise (Parts 01, 09), and picking the elbow is the standard RV-frequency choice.

4. Lab Exercises (The Course Set)

  1. Clean & consolidate: apply the filter battery to one day of TAQ-style data; report deletion counts per rule (expect ~0.1–1%).
  2. Spread & depth profiles: reproduce Part 05's intraday U-shapes from your cleaned data, medians by 5-minute bin.
  3. Lee-Ready + OFI: sign trades, build order-flow imbalance, regress next-bar mid returns on OFI — recover Part 01's predictability (in-sample $R^2$ of a few % at seconds horizons is success).
  4. Kyle-λ: estimate Part 03's impact regression per stock-day; plot λ against 1/(depth) across names.
  5. Performance discipline: vectorize everything (no per-tick loops); a day of liquid-name ticks is ~10⁶–10⁷ events — pandas/MATLAB timetables handle it, Python loops do not.

Key Takeaways

  • Clean with the standard filter battery, log deletions, and never trust locked/crossed or condition-coded prints.
  • The backward as-of join (merge_asof / retime-previous) is the atomic operation — quotes prevailing, never future.
  • Prefer volume/dollar bars for statistics; read the signature plot before choosing any sampling frequency.
  • Every headline result of Parts 01–06 (spreads, OFI predictability, λ, markouts) reproduces in a few vectorized lines on clean data.

Plain English Notes

The Big Picture: High-frequency data is massive and noisy. Manipulating and cleaning millions of tick-by-tick records is the first step in calibrating intraday trading signals.

  • Tick-by-Tick Data: Captures every single quote change and trade execution, resulting in gigabytes of data per stock per day.
  • Data Cleaning: Filtering out bad quotes, trade cancellations, and exchange glitches that can corrupt backtests.
  • Resampling: Converting irregular tick data into regular time intervals (like 1-second bars) or volume-based buckets (volume bars).

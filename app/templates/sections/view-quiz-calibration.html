Question 1 Multiple Choice

1. Which of the following statements is true? For calibrating a model to cap prices we minimize squared vega weighted cap price errors, because...

Show Mathematical Solution & Derivation

Theoretical Explanation:

In practice, we prefer to minimize the difference between the market implied volatilities and the model implied volatilities ($\sum (\hat{\sigma}_n^\theta - \sigma_n)^2$) because volatilities are standardized and comparable across maturities. However, inverting the Black or Bachelier pricing formula numerically at every step of the optimizer is computationally expensive.

To avoid numerical inversion, we apply a first-order Taylor expansion of the pricing function $C_n(\sigma)$ around the market implied vol $\sigma_n$:

$$ \hat{C}_n^\theta \approx C_n + \text{vega}_n (\hat{\sigma}_n^\theta - \sigma_n) $$

Rearranging this yields an analytical expression for the implied volatility error:

$$ \hat{\sigma}_n^\theta - \sigma_n \approx \frac{\hat{C}_n^\theta - C_n}{\text{vega}_n} $$

Thus, minimizing the squared vega-weighted price errors $\sum \left( \frac{\hat{C}_n^\theta - C_n}{\text{vega}_n} \right)^2$ is mathematically equivalent (to first-order) to minimizing the implied volatility errors, but runs hundreds of times faster because it is fully analytical!

Correct Option: vega weighted cap price errors approximate the implied volatility errors.

Mathematical Analysis of All Options:

  • Statement 1 (True): By definition, the strike rate $\kappa$ of an at-the-money (ATM) cap equals the par swap rate of the corresponding forward-starting interest rate swap matching the cap's tenor structure.
  • Statement 2 (False): Black implied volatility can be calculated for any cap at any strike rate, as long as the market price exceeds the option's intrinsic value and is below its theoretical upper bound.
  • Statement 3 (False): Black vega is the price derivative with respect to relative volatility $\sigma_B$, whereas Bachelier vega is with respect to absolute volatility $\sigma_N$. They differ by a scaling factor of approximately the forward rate: $\text{vega}_{Black} \approx F \times \text{vega}_{Bachelier}$.
  • Statement 4 (True): A floorlet pay-off at $T_i$ is $\delta(K - L_i(T_{i-1}))^+$. Under standard log-normal/normal measures, this is algebraically equivalent to $(1 + \delta K)$ call options on the zero-coupon bond $P(\cdot, T_i)$ expiring at $T_{i-1}$ with strike rate $K_{bond} = \frac{1}{1 + \delta K}$.

Correct Options: Statements 1 and 4.

Question 3 Multiple Choice

3. What market data do we need to compute the Black implied volatility for an ATM cap?

Show Mathematical Solution & Derivation

Theoretical Explanation:

A cap is a portfolio of caplets. To price a cap under Black's framework, we need:

  • The discount factors $P(0, T_i)$ for each cash flow payment date.
  • The forward LIBOR rates $F(0, T_{i-1}, T_i)$ for each reset period, which are derived directly from the discount factors.
  • The ATM strike rate $K$, which is the par swap rate and is also determined by the discount curve.

If we only have the cap price and the ATM strike rate, we cannot compute the implied volatility because we lack the discount factors and forward rates for each period in the cap strip. We need the entire discount curve to obtain these parameters consistently. Thus, we need the cap price and the discount curve.

Correct Option: The cap price and the discount curve.

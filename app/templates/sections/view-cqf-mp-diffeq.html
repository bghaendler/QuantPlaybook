1. First Order Separable ODE

A separable ODE can be written in the general form where both sides depend on a single variable:

$$\frac{dy}{dx} = f(x)\,g(y)$$

Separating and integrating both sides yields:

$$\int \frac{1}{g(y)}\,dy = \int f(x)\,dx + C$$

In finance, separable ODEs appear naturally in continuous-time growth models such as exponential asset decay, population dynamics, and simple interest rate models where the rate of change depends multiplicatively on the current state.

2. Second Order Constant Coefficient ODE

The standard homogeneous second-order ODE with constant coefficients takes the form:

$$ay'' + by' + cy = 0$$

The auxiliary (characteristic) equation is:

$$ar^2 + br + c = 0$$

The nature of the roots determines the solution form:

  • Distinct real roots $r_1, r_2$:   $y = Ae^{r_1 x} + Be^{r_2 x}$
  • Repeated root $r$:   $y = (A + Bx)e^{rx}$
  • Complex roots $\alpha \pm i\beta$:   $y = e^{\alpha x}(A\cos\beta x + B\sin\beta x)$

These solutions underpin oscillatory pricing models in quantitative finance, including mean-reverting processes and damped vibration analogues used in portfolio risk analysis.

The Raw SVI Slice

Total implied variance as a function of log-moneyness $k = \ln(K/F)$:

$$w(k) = a + b\left[\rho\,(k - m) + \sqrt{(k - m)^2 + \sigma^2}\right], \qquad \sigma_{imp}(k) = \sqrt{w(k)/T}.$$
  • a — overall variance level · b — wing slope (vol of vol) · ρ — skew/rotation · m — horizontal shift · σ — ATM curvature smoothing.
  • Wings are asymptotically linear in $k$ (Lee's moment bounds), which is why SVI extrapolates sanely where polynomials explode.

No-Arbitrage Conditions & Fitting Practice

  • Butterfly arbitrage (negative density): checked via Gatheral's $g(k) \ge 0$ function — the diagnostic above flags violations; a simple necessary condition is $b(1 + |\rho|) \le 4/T$ for the wings.
  • Calendar arbitrage: total variance slices must not cross — $w(k, T_2) \ge w(k, T_1)$ for $T_2 > T_1$; the SVI-JW reparametrization and Gatheral-Jacquier "Surface SVI" enforce this by construction.
  • Fitting: quasi-explicit calibration (Zeliade): for fixed $(m, \sigma)$, the model is linear in $(a, b\rho, b)$ — solve by constrained least squares inside a 2-D search. Robust, fast, smooth parameter evolution day to day.
  • Why SVI won: wings consistent with moment bounds, few parameters with trader-readable meaning, exact Heston large-T consistency, and clean Dupire local-vol extraction from the fitted surface.

High-Frequency Trading & Market Microstructure — Part 10

Stochastic Control Theory & Trade Execution

Dynamic programming for trading: the HJB equation, the Avellaneda-Stoikov market-making solution, and execution as a controlled diffusion problem.

1. The Control Framework

State $X_t$ (price, inventory $q_t$, cash) evolves under a control $u_t$ (trade rate, quote offsets); maximize expected utility of terminal wealth. The value function $V(t, x)$ satisfies the Hamilton-Jacobi-Bellman equation:

$$\partial_t V + \sup_{u}\Big\{\mathcal{L}^{u}V + f(x, u)\Big\} = 0, \qquad V(T, x) = \text{terminal payoff},$$

where $\mathcal{L}^u$ is the controlled generator. Dynamic programming replaces a path-space optimization with a PDE: solve backwards, the maximizing $u^*(t,x)$ is the policy. Verification theorems certify that a smooth solution is genuinely optimal. The deterministic Almgren-Chriss schedule (Part 07) is this machinery with no feedback; the gain here is closed-loop policies that react to inventory and prices.

2. Avellaneda-Stoikov Market Making (The Canonical Solve)

Mid follows $dS = \sigma dW$; quoting at distances $\delta^{\pm}$ from mid attracts fills with intensity $\lambda(\delta) = Ae^{-k\delta}$; exponential utility with risk aversion $\gamma$. The HJB system reduces (via the ansatz $V = -e^{-\gamma(w + \theta(t, q, s))}$) to closed-form quotes:

$$\text{reservation price: } r(t) = s - q\,\gamma\sigma^2(T - t), \qquad \text{total spread: } \delta^+ + \delta^- = \gamma\sigma^2(T - t) + \frac{2}{\gamma}\ln\!\left(1 + \frac{\gamma}{k}\right).$$
  • Inventory skew: long $q$ ⇒ reservation price below mid ⇒ quotes skew to sell — the controller sheds risk automatically.
  • Spread anatomy: a risk term (σ, γ, horizon) + a liquidity-elasticity term ($k$): exactly Part 01's inventory + adverse-selection decomposition, now derived rather than asserted.
  • Extensions: inventory limits (reflecting boundaries, Part 09), Hawkes-driven intensities, adverse-selection terms (Guéant-Lehalle-Tapia generalizations with ODE-system solutions).

3. Optimal Execution as Control

  • Liquidation with temporary impact $\dot{x} = -u$, cost $\eta u^2$, risk $\sigma^2x^2$: HJB yields the same sinh trajectory as Part 07 — but now state-dependent variants are easy: stochastic σ (regime urgency, Part 08), price-dependent alpha (trade faster into favorable drift), terminal penalties for residual inventory.
  • Limit-vs-market order control: impulse/optimal-switching formulations — quasi-variational inequalities decide when to cross the spread vs. keep resting; numerically: policy iteration on a discretized state grid.
  • Order routing as control: the bandit/assignment view of Part 06 is the discrete-action sibling of the same DP principle.

4. Solving in Practice

  • Dimensionality: 1–3 state dimensions → finite differences with policy iteration (boundary care per Part 09); higher → approximate DP / neural policy methods; reinforcement learning is HJB by sampling when dynamics aren't trusted analytically (cf. the ML masterclass).
  • Ansatz first: exponential utility + linear-quadratic structures collapse HJB to ODE systems — try the literature's ansatz before discretizing a PDE.
  • Robustness beats elegance: mis-specified $\lambda(\delta)$ or σ degrades the "optimal" policy below a tuned heuristic — control output should be policy shapes (skew with inventory, widen with vol and horizon) with parameters fitted in production telemetry.
  • Sanity anchors: γ→0 must recover risk-neutral limits (TWAP-like schedules, symmetric quotes); horizon→0 must liquidate/flatten — check the corners.

Key Takeaways

  • HJB turns trading into a backward PDE; the argmax of the Hamiltonian is the live policy.
  • Avellaneda-Stoikov: reservation price skews with inventory, spread = risk term + $\frac{2}{\gamma}\ln(1 + \gamma/k)$ — market making solved in closed form.
  • Execution control generalizes Almgren-Chriss to feedback policies; impulse control governs limit-vs-market decisions.
  • Use ansatz reductions where they exist, FD/policy iteration in low dimension, RL beyond — and ship the policy shape, recalibrated from telemetry.

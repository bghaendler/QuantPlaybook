Complex Analysis for Quants — Part 12

Definite Real Trigonometric Contour Evaluation

Integrals of the form $\int_0^{2\pi} R(\cos\theta, \sin\theta)\,d\theta$: substitute the unit circle, collect poles inside, done.

1. The Substitution

On the unit circle $z = e^{i\theta}$, the whole trigonometric alphabet becomes rational in $z$:

$$\cos\theta = \frac{1}{2}\left(z + \frac{1}{z}\right), \qquad \sin\theta = \frac{1}{2i}\left(z - \frac{1}{z}\right), \qquad d\theta = \frac{dz}{iz}.$$

So any rational integrand in $\cos\theta, \sin\theta$ over a full period becomes a closed contour integral of a rational function:

$$\int_0^{2\pi} R(\cos\theta, \sin\theta)\,d\theta = \oint_{|z|=1} R\!\left(\tfrac{z + z^{-1}}{2}, \tfrac{z - z^{-1}}{2i}\right)\frac{dz}{iz},$$

evaluated by summing residues of the poles strictly inside the unit circle (Cauchy's formula / Part 15 machinery).

2. Worked Example: The Poisson-Kernel Integral

For $|a| < 1$, compute $I = \displaystyle\int_0^{2\pi}\frac{d\theta}{1 - 2a\cos\theta + a^2}$. Substituting:

$$I = \oint_{|z|=1}\frac{1}{1 - a(z + z^{-1}) + a^2}\cdot\frac{dz}{iz} = \frac{1}{i}\oint\frac{dz}{(1 - az)(z - a)} \cdot \frac{1}{1}.$$

The factorization $-az^2 + (1 + a^2)z - a = -a(z - a)(z - 1/a)$ puts one pole at $z = a$ (inside) and one at $z = 1/a$ (outside). Picking up the inside pole:

$$I = 2\pi i \cdot \frac{1}{i}\cdot\frac{1}{1 - a^2} = \frac{2\pi}{1 - a^2}.$$

This is exactly the normalization of the Poisson kernel from Part 07 — the disk's Dirichlet solver, integrated by the method it motivates. The same integral computes the variance ratio in AR(1) spectral densities ($a$ = autoregressive coefficient): spectral analysis of time series runs on this template.

3. More Patterns From the Same Mold

  • $\displaystyle\int_0^{2\pi}\frac{d\theta}{p + q\cos\theta} = \frac{2\pi}{\sqrt{p^2 - q^2}}$ ($p > |q|$): quadratic in $z$, one root inside — the master formula for this family.
  • $\displaystyle\int_0^{2\pi}\cos^{2n}\theta\,d\theta = 2\pi\binom{2n}{n}2^{-2n}$: binomially expand $(z + z^{-1})^{2n}/2^{2n}$, keep the $z^{-1}$ coefficient — coefficient extraction as in Part 11 (and de Moivre linearization from Part 02, now automated).
  • Numerators with $\cos k\theta$: write as $\frac{1}{2}(z^k + z^{-k})$; only the terms producing a net $z^{-1}$ after division survive.
  • Half-range integrals $\int_0^\pi$: use symmetry to extend to $[0, 2\pi]$ first (even integrands in $\theta$), then substitute.

4. Where Quants Meet These Integrals

  • Spectral densities: autocovariances of ARMA processes are exactly $\frac{1}{2\pi}\int_0^{2\pi} f(\omega)e^{ik\omega}d\omega$ with rational $f$ in $\cos\omega$ — unit-circle residues give closed-form autocorrelations (and the AR(1) example above).
  • Characteristic functions of wrapped/circular distributions (von Mises) used in intraday seasonality modeling normalize through these integrals.
  • Numerical sanity: these closed forms make excellent unit tests for quadrature code — exact answers with nontrivial structure.

Key Takeaways

  • $z = e^{i\theta}$ converts full-period trig integrals to unit-circle contour integrals of rational functions.
  • Only poles strictly inside $|z| = 1$ contribute; factor the denominator and check moduli.
  • The Poisson-kernel normalization $\frac{2\pi}{1 - a^2}$ and the $\frac{2\pi}{\sqrt{p^2 - q^2}}$ family are the reusable results.
  • Same machinery powers ARMA spectral/autocovariance calculations — time-series analysis on the unit circle.

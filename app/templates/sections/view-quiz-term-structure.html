Question 1 Numeric Input

1. Suppose the spot date $t_0$ is 1 October 2012. You observe a 3-month USD LIBOR deposit rate $L(t_0, S_3) = 0.36\%$ maturing on 3 January 2013, with an exact year fraction of $\delta(t_0, S_3) = \frac{94}{360}$ under the Actual/360 day-count convention.

What is the corresponding zero-coupon bond discount factor price $P(t_0, S_3)$? Round your answer to 6 decimal places (e.g. write 0.999061).

Show Mathematical Solution & Derivation

Step-by-Step Analytical Solution:

For standard deposit/simple money market instruments under Actual/360, ZCB discount factor prices are calculated using simple interest discounting:

$$ P(t_0, S_3) = \frac{1}{1 + \delta(t_0, S_3) \cdot L(t_0, S_3)} $$

Substituting the given values into the formula:

$$ L(t_0, S_3) = 0.36\% = 0.0036 $$ $$ \delta(t_0, S_3) = \frac{94}{360} \approx 0.261111 $$ $$ P(t_0, S_3) = \frac{1}{1 + \frac{94}{360} \cdot 0.0036} = \frac{1}{1 + 0.00094} = \frac{1}{1.00094} \approx \mathbf{0.99906088} $$

Correct Answer: 0.999061

Question 2 Numeric Input

2. Due to the maturity overlap where $S_2 < T_1 < S_3$, we must linearly interpolate simple rates to find the boundary rate $L(t_0, T_1)$.

Given: 1-month LIBOR rate $L(t_0, S_2) = 0.21\%$ (days=35) and 3-month LIBOR rate $L(t_0, S_3) = 0.36\%$ (days=94). The first futures reset date $T_1$ falls exactly 79 days from spot.

What is the linearly interpolated boundary simple rate $L(t_0, T_1)$? Express your answer in percentage points (e.g. write 0.3219 for 0.3219%) and round to 4 decimal places.

%
Show Mathematical Solution & Derivation

Step-by-Step Analytical Solution:

The interpolation weight $q$ is defined by the day difference from the target date $T_1$ to the bounding dates $S_2$ and $S_3$:

$$ q = \frac{\delta(T_1, S_3)}{\delta(S_2, S_3)} = \frac{94 - 79}{94 - 35} = \frac{15}{59} \approx 0.254237 $$

Applying the linear interpolation of simple rates:

$$ L(t_0, T_1) = q \cdot L(t_0, S_2) + (1-q) \cdot L(t_0, S_3) $$ $$ L(t_0, T_1) = \frac{15}{59} \cdot (0.21\%) + \left(1 - \frac{15}{59}\right) \cdot (0.36\%) $$ $$ L(t_0, T_1) = \frac{15}{59} \cdot (0.21\%) + \frac{44}{59} \cdot (0.36\%) = \frac{3.15 + 15.84}{59}\% = \frac{18.99}{59}\% \approx \mathbf{0.321864\%} $$

Correct Answer: 0.3219

Question 3 Numeric Input

3. Suppose you observe a 2-year spot starting annual swap rate $R_{\text{swap}}(t_0, U_2) = 2.0\%$ (coupons paid at year 1 and year 2, with year fraction $\delta \equiv 1.0$ year).

You have already solved the 1-year discount factor $P(t_0, U_1) = 0.98$.

What is the solved 2-year discount factor price $P(t_0, U_2)$ using the inverted swap bootstrap formula? Round your answer to 4 decimal places (e.g. write 0.9612).

Show Mathematical Solution & Derivation

Step-by-Step Analytical Solution:

For a spot-starting swap, the swap rate is the fixed coupon rate that sets the initial swap value to zero. Inverting this relation yields the general bootstrapping formula:

$$ P(t_0, U_n) = \frac{1 - R_{\text{swap}} \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} \delta_i P(t_0, U_i)}{1 + R_{\text{swap}} \delta_n} $$

Substituting the given values into the formula for $n=2$:

$$ R_{\text{swap}} = 2.0\% = 0.02, \quad \delta_1 = \delta_2 = 1.0 $$ $$ P(t_0, U_2) = \frac{1 - 0.02 \cdot (1.0 \cdot P(t_0, U_1))}{1 + 0.02 \cdot 1.0} $$ $$ P(t_0, U_2) = \frac{1 - 0.02 \cdot 0.98}{1.02} = \frac{1 - 0.0196}{1.02} = \frac{0.9804}{1.02} \approx \mathbf{0.961176} $$

Correct Answer: 0.9612

Question 4 Multiple Choice

4. In underdetermined cash flow systems, we represent the discount factors in terms of the increments $\Delta$ as $d = M^{-1} \left( W^{-1} \Delta + (1, 0, \dots, 0)^\top \right)$.

Which of the following equations represents the correct matching equation $C d = p$ expressed in terms of the increments vector $\Delta$?

Show Mathematical Solution & Derivation

Step-by-Step Analytical Solution:

Let's substitute the discount factor representation $d = M^{-1} \left( W^{-1} \Delta + (1, 0, \dots, 0)^\top \right)$ directly into the cash flow matching equation $C d = p$:

$$ C \left[ M^{-1} \left( W^{-1} \Delta + (1, 0, \dots, 0)^\top \right) \right] = p $$

Expanding the left-hand side:

$$ C M^{-1} W^{-1} \Delta + C M^{-1} (1, 0, \dots, 0)^\top = p $$

Subtracting the constant term $C M^{-1} (1, 0, \dots, 0)^\top$ from both sides yields the final matching equation:

$$ C M^{-1} W^{-1} \Delta = p - C M^{-1} (1, 0, \dots, 0)^\top $$

Correct Option: A.

Question 5 Multiple Choice

5. In the Nelson-Siegel parametric yield family: $y(T) = \beta_0 I_0(T) + \beta_1 I_1(T) + \beta_2 I_2(T)$, the coefficients have stable physical shapes.

Which of the following physical interpretations of the parameters ($\beta_0, \beta_1, \beta_2$) is correct?

Show Mathematical Solution & Derivation

Step-by-Step Analytical Solution:

Let's check the basis functions $I_i(T)$ across maturities $T$ (Slide 7):

  • Level factor ($\beta_0$): The basis $I_0(T) = 1.0$ is constant for all maturities $T$.
  • Slope factor ($\beta_1$): The basis $I_1(T) = \frac{1 - e^{-a T}}{a T}$ decays monotonically from 1.0 (at $T=0$) to 0.0 (as $T \to \infty$).
  • Curvature factor ($\beta_2$): The basis $I_2(T) = \frac{1 - e^{-a T}}{a T} - e^{-a T}$ starts at 0.0, humps in the medium-term, and decays back to 0.0.

Therefore, the correct interpretation is that $\beta_0$ represents Level, $\beta_1$ represents Slope, and $\beta_2$ represents Curvature.

Correct Option: B.

Question 6 Numeric Input

6. Suppose that you perform Principal Component Analysis (PCA) on Swiss Government yield changes. The sorted eigenvalues of the covariance matrix are:

$\lambda_1 = 1.8058 \times 10^{-5}$
$\lambda_2 = 3.3688 \times 10^{-6}$
and the sum of all 8 eigenvalues is exactly $2.2662 \times 10^{-5}$.

What is the proportion of total variability in yield changes explained by the first two principal components combined? Express your answer in percentage points (e.g. write 94.55 for 94.55%) and round to 2 decimal places.

%
Show Mathematical Solution & Derivation

Step-by-Step Analytical Solution:

The proportion of variability in a random vector $X$ explained by the first $k$ principal components is the sum of the first $k$ eigenvalues divided by the sum of all eigenvalues:

$$ \text{Explained Ratio} = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^k \lambda_i}{\sum_{i=1}^n \lambda_i} $$

For $k=2$ using the given eigenvalues:

$$ \text{Sum of first 2} = \lambda_1 + \lambda_2 = 1.8058 \times 10^{-5} + 3.3688 \times 10^{-6} = 2.14268 \times 10^{-5} $$ $$ \text{Total sum} = 2.2662 \times 10^{-5} $$ $$ \text{Ratio} = \frac{2.14268 \times 10^{-5}}{2.2662 \times 10^{-5}} = \frac{2.14268}{2.2662} \approx 0.9454902 \quad (\text{or } \mathbf{94.55\%}) $$

Correct Answer: 94.55

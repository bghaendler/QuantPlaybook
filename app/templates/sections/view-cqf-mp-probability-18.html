1.8 Cumulative Distribution Functions (CDF)

Cumulative summation, integration bounds, PDF mapping derivatives, and boundary limits.

1.8.1 CDF Mathematical Formulations

The Cumulative Distribution Function, denoted $F(x)$, represents the probability that a random variable $X$ takes a value less than or equal to $x$:

$$F(x) = P(X \le x)$$

For Continuous Variables: The CDF is the integral of the PDF up to the boundary $x$:

$$F(x) = \int_{-\infty}^{x} f(t) \, dt$$

Conversely, by the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus, the probability density function is the local derivative of the CDF:

$$f(x) = \frac{d}{dx} F(x)$$

CDFs must satisfy: $\lim_{x \to -\infty} F(x) = 0$ and $\lim_{x \to +\infty} F(x) = 1$.

Question 1 Multiple Choice

1. Which of the following statements is true? A cap is out-of-the-money if its cap rate $\kappa$ is...

Show Mathematical Solution & Derivation

Step-by-Step Explanation:

A cap protects the holder against high interest rates. It consists of a strip of caplets whose discounted payoff is given by the difference between the simple spot rate and the strike rate $\kappa$, positive part: $\delta (L(T_{i-1}, T_i) - \kappa)^+$.

By Cap-Floor Parity, the difference between a Cap and a Floor is the value of a payer swap with fixed rate $\kappa$:

$$ Cp(t) - Fl(t) = V_p(t) $$

The payer swap value is proportional to the difference between the prevailing swap rate $R_{swap}(t)$ and the strike rate $\kappa$:

$$ V_p(t) = \bigl( R_{swap}(t) - \kappa \bigr) \sum_{i=1}^n \delta P(t, T_i) $$

A cap is defined to be:

  • In-the-Money (ITM) if $\kappa < R_{swap}$ (the strike is below the swap rate, making the option highly valuable).
  • At-the-Money (ATM) if $\kappa = R_{swap}$ (the swap value is zero).
  • Out-of-the-Money (OTM) if $\kappa > R_{swap}$ (the strike is higher than the swap rate, so market rates are currently lower than the protection limit).

Correct Answer: larger than the corresponding swap rate, $\kappa > R_{swap}$

Question 2 Multiple Choice

2. Which of the following statements is true? A floor is out-of-the-money if its floor rate $\kappa$ is...

Show Mathematical Solution & Derivation

Step-by-Step Explanation:

A floor protects the holder against too low interest rates. It pays off when the spot rate drops below the strike rate $\kappa$ (payoff is $\delta(\kappa - L(T_{i-1}, T_i))^+$).

By Cap-Floor Parity, we can see the relative moneyness relationship is the exact opposite (converse) of a cap:

  • In-the-Money (ITM) if $\kappa > R_{swap}$ (the strike is above the swap rate, making the floor option highly valuable).
  • At-the-Money (ATM) if $\kappa = R_{swap}$ (values of Cap and Floor are equal, swap value is zero).
  • Out-of-the-Money (OTM) if $\kappa < R_{swap}$ (the floor strike is below the prevailing swap rate, so market rates are higher than the floor and it is unlikely to trigger).

Correct Answer: smaller than the corresponding swap rate, $\kappa < R_{swap}$

Question 3 Multiple Select

3. Consider an ordinary swap contract that swaps fixed for floating and an alternative swap contract with the same characteristics, except that the floating payments are floored at some fixed lower bound $\kappa$. Denote by $R_1(0)$ and $R_2(0)$ the fixed rates that make the values of the respective swap contracts equal to zero. Denote by $Cp(0)$ and $Fl(0)$ the value of a cap and a floor with notional 1, cap/floor rate $\kappa$ and the same tenor structure $T_0 < T_1 < \dots < T_M$, with $T_i - T_{i-1} \equiv \delta$, as the swap contracts. Which of the following statements are true?

Show Mathematical Solution & Derivation

Step-by-Step Mathematical Derivation:

Let's represent the value of the two swap contracts at $t=0$ where we pay fixed and receive floating.

1. Value of ordinary swap $V_1(0)$:

$$ V_1(0) = \sum_{i=1}^M \delta P(0, T_i) \left( F(0, T_{i-1}, T_i) - R_1 \right) $$

Since $R_1(0)$ makes the value equal to zero, we have:

$$ R_1(0) \sum_{i=1}^M \delta P(0, T_i) = \sum_{i=1}^M \delta P(0, T_i) F(0, T_{i-1}, T_i) $$

2. Value of alternative swap $V_2(0)$:

In this contract, the floating payment is floored at $\kappa$. The payoff is $\max(L(T_{i-1}, T_i), \kappa) = L(T_{i-1}, T_i) + (\kappa - L(T_{i-1}, T_i))^+$. This is a standard spot rate plus a floorlet payoff!

$$ V_2(0) = \sum_{i=1}^M \delta P(0, T_i) \left( F(0, T_{i-1}, T_i) - R_2 \right) + \sum_{i=1}^M Fll_i(0) $$

where $Fll_i(0) = P(0, T_i) \mathbb{E}^{\mathbb{Q}^{T_i}}_0 \left[ \delta (\kappa - L(T_{i-1}, T_i))^+ \right]$. Summing over all period intervals gives the total floor price $Fl(0) = \sum_{i=1}^M Fll_i(0)$.

Since $R_2(0)$ makes $V_2(0) = 0$, we have:

$$ R_2(0) \sum_{i=1}^M \delta P(0, T_i) = \sum_{i=1}^M \delta P(0, T_i) F(0, T_{i-1}, T_i) + Fl(0) $$

Substitute the ordinary swap relation into this expression:

$$ R_2(0) \sum_{i=1}^M \delta P(0, T_i) = R_1(0) \sum_{i=1}^M \delta P(0, T_i) + Fl(0) $$

Dividing by $\delta \sum_{i=1}^M P(0, T_i)$ gives Statement 1:

$$ R_2(0) = R_1(0) + \frac{Fl(0)}{\delta \sum_{i=1}^M P(0, T_i)} $$

3. Deriving the Cap Relation:

Recall the Cap-Floor Parity Relation: $Cp(0) - Fl(0) = V_p(0)$, where $V_p(0)$ is the value of a payer swap with strike $\kappa$:

$$ Cp(0) - Fl(0) = \sum_{i=1}^M \delta P(0, T_i) F(0, T_{i-1}, T_i) - \kappa \sum_{i=1}^M \delta P(0, T_i) $$

$$ Cp(0) - Fl(0) = \left( R_1(0) - \kappa \right) \sum_{i=1}^M \delta P(0, T_i) $$

Rearranging this yields the floor price expressed in terms of the cap price:

$$ Fl(0) = Cp(0) - \left( R_1(0) - \kappa \right) \delta \sum_{i=1}^M P(0, T_i) $$

Substitute this back into our expression for $R_2(0)$ in Statement 1:

$$ R_2(0) = R_1(0) + \frac{Cp(0) - (R_1(0) - \kappa) \delta \sum_{i=1}^M P(0, T_i)}{\delta \sum_{i=1}^M P(0, T_i)} $$

$$ R_2(0) = R_1(0) + \frac{Cp(0)}{\delta \sum_{i=1}^M P(0, T_i)} - R_1(0) + \kappa $$

The $R_1(0)$ terms cancel out! This yields Statement 3:

$$ R_2(0) = \kappa + \frac{Cp(0)}{\delta \sum_{i=1}^M P(0, T_i)} $$

Correct Answers: Statement 1 and Statement 3 are correct.

Question 4 Multiple Choice

4. Consider a cap and a floor with the same cap/floor rate and identical tenor structure. Denote their value by $Cp(0)$ and $Fl(0)$ and suppose that the cap is at-the-money. Which of the following statements is true?

Show Mathematical Solution & Derivation

Step-by-Step Explanation:

Recall the Cap-Floor Swap Parity Relation:

$$ Cp(0) - Fl(0) = V_p(0) $$

where $V_p(0)$ is the value of the payer swap with fixed rate $\kappa$ and notional 1.

By definition, a cap and a floor are at-the-money (ATM) if their values are equal. This occurs when the payer swap value is exactly equal to zero ($V_p(0) = 0$), which happens when the strike rate $\kappa$ equals the forward swap rate $R_{swap}(0)$:

$$ Cp(0) - Fl(0) = 0 \implies Cp(0) = Fl(0) $$

Correct Answer: $Cp(0) = Fl(0)$

Question 5 True / False

5. Is the following statement true or false?
A cap composed of out-of-the-money caplets is out-of-the-money.

Show Mathematical Solution & Derivation

Mathematical Proof & Explanation:

Let's prove this statement algebraically. A caplet for the period $[T_{i-1}, T_i]$ is out-of-the-money (OTM) if its strike $\kappa$ is strictly greater than the corresponding forward simple spot rate $F(t, T_{i-1}, T_i)$:

$$ \kappa > F(t, T_{i-1}, T_i) \quad \forall \; i = 1, \dots, n $$

The forward swap rate $R_{swap}(t)$ is defined as the weighted average of the forward spot rates $F(t, T_{i-1}, T_i)$ with positive weights $w_i > 0$ summing to 1:

$$ R_{swap}(t) = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^n \delta P(t, T_i) F(t, T_{i-1}, T_i)}{\sum_{i=1}^n \delta P(t, T_i)} = \sum_{i=1}^n w_i F(t, T_{i-1}, T_i) $$

where the weights are $w_i = \frac{\delta P(t, T_i)}{\sum_{j=1}^n \delta P(t, T_j)} > 0$, satisfying $\sum_{i=1}^n w_i = 1$.

Since $\kappa > F(t, T_{i-1}, T_i)$ for all $i \in \{1, \dots, n\}$, and weights $w_i > 0$, we have:

$$ R_{swap}(t) = \sum_{i=1}^n w_i F(t, T_{i-1}, T_i) < \sum_{i=1}^n w_i \kappa = \kappa \sum_{i=1}^n w_i = \kappa $$

So, $\kappa > R_{swap}(t)$ always holds. By definition, a cap is out-of-the-money (OTM) if the strike rate is greater than the swap rate, $\kappa > R_{swap}(t)$.

This proves that if every caplet is out-of-the-money, the cap is guaranteed to be out-of-the-money. Thus, the statement is strictly True.

Correct Answer: True

Interest Rate Derivatives

3. Swaptions

Swaptions (Options on Swaps)

An interest rate swaption is an option that gives the holder the right, but not the obligation, to enter into an interest rate swap at a future expiry date $T_0$ with a pre-specified fixed strike rate $K$. Swaptions are classified into two primary types:

  • Payer Swaption: Gives the holder the right to pay fixed and receive floating. This is an option to enter a payer swap, protecting the holder against rising interest rates.
  • Receiver Swaption: Gives the holder the right to receive fixed and pay floating. This is an option to enter a receiver swap, protecting the holder against falling interest rates.

Usually, the swaption expiry date $T_0$ equals the first reset date of the underlying swap. Unlike caps and floors, a swaption payoff cannot be decomposed into more elementary payoffs (like caplets/floorlets). This is because the positive part of a sum is not equal to the sum of positive parts: $\left( \sum w_i (R_i - K) \right)^+ \neq \sum \left( w_i (R_i - K) \right)^+$.

Consequently, the joint distribution and correlations between different forward rates directly impact the valuation of a swaption, making them highly sensitive to the structure of the interest rate model.

Key Concepts

  • Expiry & Tenor: An "$X$ year into $Y$ year" swaption expires in $X$ years, and the underlying swap has a length of $Y$ years.
  • Annuity Measure: Swaption pricing is modeled under the swap (annuity) measure $\mathbb{Q}^A$ where the annuity is the numeraire.
  • Moneyness Relation: A payer swaption is ATM when the strike $K$ equals the forward swap rate $R_{swap}(t)$.
  • Synthetic Options: Buying a receiver swaption allows companies to synthetically create callable bonds to refinance debt if rates drop.

Swaption Payoffs & Level Factor

Let $T_0$ be the swaption expiry (first reset date), and $T_1 < T_2 < \dots < T_n$ be the payment dates of the underlying swap of length $T_n - T_0$. The accrual period is $\delta = T_i - T_{i-1}$.

The value of a forward payer swap at expiry $T_0$ is:

$$ V_p(T_0) = N \sum_{i=1}^n P(T_0, T_i)\delta \bigl( R_{swap}(T_0) - K \bigr) = N A(T_0) \bigl( R_{swap}(T_0) - K \bigr) $$

where $N$ is the notional, $K$ is the strike rate, and $A(T) = \sum_{i=1}^n \delta P(T, T_i)$ is the annuity factor (or level factor).

The payer swaption holder will only exercise if the swap value at expiry is positive, so the payoff is:

$$ \text{Payoff}_p(T_0) = N A(T_0) \bigl( R_{swap}(T_0) - K \bigr)^+ $$

For a receiver swaption, the payoff is the positive part of the receiver swap:

$$ \text{Payoff}_r(T_0) = N A(T_0) \bigl( K - R_{swap}(T_0) \bigr)^+ $$

Swaption Moneyness

Since the payoff depends on the relationship between the strike rate $K$ and the prevailing forward swap rate $R_{swap}(t)$, swaption moneyness is classified as follows:

Moneyness State Condition Payer Swaption Receiver Swaption
At-the-Money (ATM) $K = R_{swap}(t)$ $\text{Swpt}_p = \text{Swpt}_r$ $\text{Swpt}_r = \text{Swpt}_p$
In-the-Money (ITM) Payer: $K < R_{swap}$
Receiver: $K > R_{swap}$		 Lower strike (Valuable) Higher strike (Valuable)
Out-of-the-Money (OTM) Payer: $K > R_{swap}$
Receiver: $K < R_{swap}$		 Higher strike Lower strike
Payer-Receiver Parity: The difference between a payer and receiver swaption price is exactly equal to the forward starting swap value: $$\text{Swpt}_p(t) - \text{Swpt}_r(t) = N A(t) \bigl( R_{swap}(t) - K \bigr) = V_{swap}(t)$$

Interactive Application: Synthetic Callable Bonds

A classic application of swaptions is for a corporation to synthetically create a callable bond when the original bond's indenture cannot be legally changed. Let's see how this works step-by-step.

The Scenario: A corporation has issued a 10-year bond with a 4.00% coupon on a principal $N$. At year 5, the corporation wants to add the right to prepay (call) the bond at par ($N$). If interest rates drop below 4%, they want to refinance their debt. The Solution: The company buys a 5-year into 5-year receiver swaption with a strike rate of 4.00%.

By combining the straight bond coupon (-4.00%) with the exercised swap (+4.00% fixed, -Floating), the fixed coupon is 100% cancelled out! The corporation effectively pays Floating from years 5 to 10 and pays the par value at year 10. In interest rate theory, this is exactly mathematically equivalent to calling the bond at par at year 5!

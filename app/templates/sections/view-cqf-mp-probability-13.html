1.3 Conditional Probability & Bayes' Theorem

Joint dependencies, sequential multiplications, and prior-to-posterior likelihood updates.

1.3.1 Conditional Probability & Bayes' Rule

The probability of event $B$ scales dynamically if we know a dependent event $A$ has already occurred:

$$P(A|B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)} \implies P(A \cap B) = P(A|B) \times P(B)$$

Equating these products yields **Bayes' Theorem** (prior-to-posterior mapping):

$$P(B|A) = \frac{P(A|B) \times P(B)}{P(A)}$$

1.10 Expectation Algebra

Linearity rules, variance shift constraints, independence simplifications, and portfolio combination properties.

1.10.1 Expectation & Variance Scaling Rules

Expectation $\mathbb{E}[X]$ behaves as a strict linear operator, while Variance $\text{Var}(X)$ scales quadratically under constant shift transformations. Let $a, b, c$ be real constants:

$\bullet \ \mathbb{E}[X + a] = \mathbb{E}[X] + a$
$\bullet \ \mathbb{E}[aX] = a\mathbb{E}[X]$
$\bullet \ \mathbb{E}[X + Y] = \mathbb{E}[X] + \mathbb{E}[Y]$ (always holds, even for dependent variables)
$\bullet \ \text{Var}(X + a) = \text{Var}(X)$ (constant translations do not affect dispersion)
$\bullet \ \text{Var}(aX) = a^2\text{Var}(X)$ (quadratic scaling)
$\bullet \ \text{Var}(b) = 0$

Under Independence: If $X$ and $Y$ are statistically independent:

$$\mathbb{E}[XY] = \mathbb{E}[X]\mathbb{E}[Y]$$ $$\text{Var}(X + Y) = \text{Var}(X) + \text{Var}(Y)$$

1.7 Probability Distributions (PMF & PDF)

Discrete mass functions, continuous density functions, integration properties, and normalizations.

1.7.1 Probability Functions & Integration

Depending on the type of Random Variable (Discrete or Continuous), the probability distribution is defined differently:

  • Discrete RVs (Probability Mass Function, PMF): Maps individual outcomes to specific probability values. E.g. Sum of two fair dice: $P(X=7) = \frac{6}{36} = \frac{1}{6}$.
  • Continuous RVs (Probability Density Function, PDF): Because continuous random variables take an infinite number of exact values, the probability of hitting any specific value is strictly zero: $P(X=x) = 0$. Instead, we define a PDF $f(x)$ where probability corresponds to area under the curve:

$$P(a < X < b) = \int_a^b f(x) \, dx$$

Two Core Properties of a PDF:

  • $f(x) \ge 0$ for all $x \in \mathbb{R}$ (no negative probability densities).
  • $\int_{-\infty}^{+\infty} f(x) \, dx = 1$ (the total area under the density curve is exactly 1).

Asymptotics of Stochastic Volatility

Large Deviation Principles in Option Pricing

The unifying formalism behind every expansion in this series: rate functions, Varadhan and Gärtner-Ellis, and how LDP turns "rare event" into "computable exponent".

1. The LDP Template

A family $(X^\epsilon)$ satisfies a large deviation principle with rate function $I$ if, for nice sets $A$:

$$\mathbb{P}(X^\epsilon \in A) \;\asymp\; \exp\!\Big(-\frac{1}{\epsilon}\inf_{x \in A} I(x)\Big), \qquad \epsilon \to 0.$$

Probabilities of rare events decay exponentially, and only the cheapest point of the set matters ("the most likely of the unlikely paths"). The two production theorems:

  • Schilder/Freidlin-Wentzell: small-noise diffusion paths — $I(\phi) = \frac{1}{2}\int|\dot\phi - b(\phi)|^2/\sigma^2(\phi)\,dt$: rare events follow energy-minimizing (geodesic) paths — Part 01's geometry is this theorem.
  • Gärtner-Ellis: from the limiting cumulant function $\Lambda(u) = \lim\epsilon\ln\mathbb{E}[e^{u X^\epsilon/\epsilon}]$, the rate is its Legendre transform $I = \Lambda^*$ — Part 03's large-time smile is this theorem.

2. Option Prices as Rare-Event Probabilities

Deep OTM options are large deviation functionals: with $\epsilon = T$ (short time) or $\epsilon = 1/T$ (long time, scaled strikes):

$$-\lim\,\epsilon\ln C(K) = I(k) \quad\Longrightarrow\quad \sigma_{BS}(k) = \frac{|k|}{\sqrt{2I(k)}}\;(\text{short time}),$$

so the entire asymptotic smile is the rate function in disguise. The series in one diagram:

3. LDP as a Computational Device

  • Saddlepoint pricing: the Lugannani-Rice/saddlepoint approximations of tail probabilities are the practical, finite-$\epsilon$ face of Gärtner-Ellis — used for CDO tranche losses, portfolio VaR (ARM connection) and quick wing quotes from any characteristic function.
  • Importance sampling done right: the optimal exponential tilt for rare-event Monte Carlo is the LDP-optimal change of measure (tilt to make the rare event typical) — variance reduction by factors of 10³+ for deep barriers/far wings; the rate function tells you the tilt before any simulation.
  • Optimal paths as diagnostics: the minimizing path (instanton) shows how the rare event happens — e.g. for a far OTM equity put under SV: vol spikes early, spot then dives — matching crash phenomenology and guiding stress-scenario design (reverse stress testing's mathematical cousin).

4. Limits of the Lens

  • LDP gives exponents, not constants — a price known "up to subexponential factors" can still be off 2× in level: pair with heat-kernel prefactors (Part 01) or saddlepoint corrections for usable numbers.
  • Order-of-limits discipline (Part 03's warning): small-T-then-wing ≠ wing-then-small-T; every formula carries its regime.
  • Non-Markovian frontiers: rough volatility satisfies modified LDPs (rate functionals over fractional paths) reproducing the $T^{-0.4}$ skew — the active research edge extending exactly this framework.

Key Takeaways

  • LDP: rare-event probabilities ≍ $e^{-I/\epsilon}$; only the cheapest path matters — geodesics and Legendre transforms compute $I$.
  • Asymptotic smiles are rate functions: Freidlin-Wentzell (short T), Gärtner-Ellis (long T), moment bounds (wings) — one framework, the whole series.
  • Saddlepoints and LDP-optimal importance sampling turn the theory into pricing and variance-reduction machinery.
  • Exponents need prefactors for production numbers; respect regimes; rough vol is this framework's frontier.

Advanced Risk Management — Part 03

Non-Normal Distributions in Risk Assets

The distributions that actually fit returns: Student-t, skewed families, mixtures and EVT tails — and how each reshapes the risk numbers built on them.

1. How Returns Violate Normality

Daily equity-index returns typically show excess kurtosis 5–30, skewness −0.3 to −1, and extreme days far beyond Gaussian reach — the August 1987-style "10σ+" events that a normal model prices at once-per-universe frequencies. Diagnostics that make it visible:

  • QQ-plots against the normal: S-shaped departure in both tails (heavier), often asymmetric (left worse).
  • Jarque-Bera / Anderson-Darling tests: reject normality at any sample size that matters — the question is never if but how non-normal.
  • Aggregational Gaussianity: monthly returns look more normal than daily (CLT working through time) — tail modeling matters most exactly at risk-management horizons.

Two routes to fat tails — unconditional (a heavy-tailed law) vs. conditional (Gaussian innovations + stochastic volatility/GARCH): real data needs both (t-innovations inside a GARCH layer), since clustering alone underexplains tail thickness.

3. Impact on Risk Numbers

  • VaR scaling breaks: normal 99% VaR = 2.33σ; a t₄ at the same σ puts 99% VaR ≈ 3.0σ and 99.9% far beyond — the divergence grows with confidence level, i.e. exactly where regulation looks.
  • ES vs. VaR gap widens: under normality ES₉₇.₅ ≈ VaR₉₉; under fat tails ES exceeds VaR by much more — one reason FRTB switched to ES.
  • Square-root-of-time fails: $\sqrt{h}$ scaling assumes i.i.d. normality; clustering and tails make 10-day risk ≠ √10 × 1-day (typically more in stress).
  • Cornish-Fisher quick fix: $z^{CF} = z + \frac{\gamma_1}{6}(z^2 - 1) + \frac{\gamma_2}{24}(z^3 - 3z) - \frac{\gamma_1^2}{36}(2z^3 - 5z)$ — moment-based quantile correction; usable to ~99%, unreliable beyond (use EVT there).

4. Fitting and Choosing

  • MLE for t/skew-t is routine; check ν stability across subsamples (Part 01's instability lens).
  • EVT recipe: threshold at ~95th percentile via mean-excess-plot linearity, fit GPD ($\xi$ = tail index ≈ 0.2–0.4 for equities), extrapolate quantiles; mind the bias-variance trade in threshold choice (HFT Part 04's Hill discipline applies verbatim).
  • Selection criterion: out-of-sample tail calibration — do 1% exceedances occur 1% of the time? (formal backtests in Part 07) — not AIC on the full density; risk cares only about the tail it quotes.

Key Takeaways

  • Returns are fat-tailed and left-skewed at risk horizons; normality fails hardest at high confidence levels.
  • Use t/skew-t bodies, GARCH conditioning, and GPD tails — conditional and unconditional heaviness are both real.
  • Fat tails widen VaR, widen the ES-VaR gap further, and break √t scaling — recompute, don't rescale.
  • Judge fits by out-of-sample tail coverage; Cornish-Fisher to ~99%, EVT beyond.

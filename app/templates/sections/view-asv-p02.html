Asymptotics of Stochastic Volatility

SABR Implied Volatility Smile Corrections

From the geometric backbone to the full Hagan formula: the correction terms, what each parameter does to the smile, and the modern fixes for the formula's known pathologies.

1. The Full Hagan Expansion

For general $\beta$ (CEV backbone $dF = \alpha F^\beta dW_1$), the industry formula is the Part 01 geometry times correction factors:

$$\sigma_{BS}(K, T) \approx \frac{\alpha}{(FK)^{\frac{1-\beta}{2}}\left[1 + \frac{(1-\beta)^2}{24}\ln^2\frac{F}{K} + \dots\right]}\cdot\frac{\zeta}{\hat{x}(\zeta)}\cdot\left[1 + \left(\tfrac{(1-\beta)^2\alpha^2}{24(FK)^{1-\beta}} + \tfrac{\rho\beta\nu\alpha}{4(FK)^{\frac{1-\beta}{2}}} + \tfrac{2 - 3\rho^2}{24}\nu^2\right)T\right]$$

Three blocks: the CEV/local-vol prefactor (backbone skew from β), the geometric factor $\zeta/\hat{x}$ (stochastic-vol smile from ρ, ν — Part 01), and the $O(T)$ correction (the heat-kernel $a_1$ term: drift of vol, vol-of-vol convexity, correlation adjustment).

2. Parameter Anatomy (Trader's Reading)

  • α: overall level — ATM vol ≈ $\alpha/F^{1-\beta}$ to leading order; quoted-vol handle.
  • β: backbone — how ATM vol moves with F ($\sigma_{ATM} \sim F^{\beta - 1}$): β = 1 sticky-log-normal, β = 0 normal/sticky-strike-ish; usually fixed by convention (0.5 rates, 1 FX/equity) because β and ρ are nearly unidentifiable from one smile.
  • ρ: skew tilt — negative ρ steepens the put wing (spot-vol correlation).
  • ν: smile curvature — convexity in both wings; the $\frac{2-3\rho^2}{24}\nu^2T$ term lifts ATM vol with maturity.

Calibration: fix β; fit (α, ρ, ν) per maturity — α from ATM (a cubic in α via the bracketed term), ρ/ν from skew/curvature: fast, stable, near-orthogonal — the formula's enduring selling point.

3. Known Pathologies

  • Negative density wings: the expansion is asymptotic, not arbitrage-free — at low strikes (especially low rates, β < 1, large $\nu^2T$) the implied Black density $\partial^2C/\partial K^2$ goes negative; butterflies misprice.
  • Zero/negative strikes: rates markets needed smiles through 0 — log-normal-based formulas fail structurally; shifted SABR (displace $F \to F + s$) and normal-vol variants are the production patch.
  • Long maturities: $O(T)$ truncation drifts — ATM term-structure fits degrade beyond a few years at high ν (Part 03's large-time analysis explains the breakdown).

4. Modern Fixes

  • Obloj/Berestycki refinements: corrected $\zeta$, $\hat x$ choices — consistent leading order, better wings at small cost.
  • Hagan's arbitrage-free PDE SABR: solve the 1-D effective local-vol PDE for the density directly — no negative densities by construction; the formula becomes the PDE's initial guess.
  • Antonov et al. exact zero-correlation SABR (and mapped ρ ≠ 0): closed-form via Bessel integrals — the accuracy benchmark.
  • Practice: quote/calibrate with Hagan for speed; switch to PDE/exact for risk on wings and CMS-type payoffs that integrate the whole smile (their values are dominated by exactly the region where the formula lies).

Key Takeaways

  • Hagan = CEV prefactor × hyperbolic backbone × $O(T)$ heat-kernel correction.
  • β backbone (fixed by convention), ρ skew, ν curvature, α level — near-orthogonal calibration is why SABR won.
  • Asymptotic ≠ arbitrage-free: negative wing densities, zero-strike failure, long-maturity drift are structural.
  • Shifted/normal variants, arbitrage-free PDE SABR and Antonov exact results are the production-grade repairs.

Plain English Notes

The Big Picture: The SABR model provides an analytical approximation for implied volatility, allowing traders to fit volatility smiles instantly without simulation.

  • SABR Formula: Espen Gaarder Haug and Patrick Hagan derived a closed-form approximation that maps SABR parameters directly to Black-Scholes implied vol.
  • Backbone Volatility: The parameter $\beta$ controls the slope of the smile as the asset price moves, capturing the 'local vol' effect.
  • Smile Parameters: $\alpha$ (vol level), $\rho$ (skewness/correlation), and $\nu$ (vol-of-vol, controlling the smile curvature).

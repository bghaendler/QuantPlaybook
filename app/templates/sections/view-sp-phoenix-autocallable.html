Phoenix Autocallable Note

The flagship of modern retail structuring: conditional coupons with memory, automatic early redemption, and a barrier put underneath — four option layers in one wrapper.

1. Mechanics

Observation dates $t_1, \dots, t_n$ (quarterly/annually), underlying $S$ (often worst-of). Three trigger levels as % of initial:

  • Autocall trigger $A$ (typically 100%): if $S_{t_i} \ge A$, the note redeems early at par + coupon. Game over.
  • Coupon barrier $B$ (typically 70–80%): if $S_{t_i} \ge B$, the period coupon $c$ is paid. Phoenix memory: previously missed coupons are paid too ("rises from the ashes").
  • Protection barrier $H$ (typically 50–70%, usually European): at maturity, if never autocalled and $S_T < H$, redemption is $N \cdot S_T/S_0$ — the down-and-in put bites.

Decomposition: bond + strip of conditional (memory) digitals − down-and-in put − the issuer's autocall feature (a knock-out on everything else). No closed form: priced by Monte Carlo or PDE on calibrated smile dynamics.

2. The Risk Geometry

  • Duration uncertainty: in rallies the note dies at the first observation (reinvestment risk, ~3–12 month realized life); in sell-offs it extends to full maturity exactly when the put risk is alive — the investor is short an extension option.
  • Coupon risk: coupons stop in drawdowns (below $B$) though memory may recover them; deep drawdowns kill coupons and trigger the barrier loss.
  • Worst-of versions (most issuance): all triggers read the worst performer — short correlation, short dispersion, and the protection barrier breaks on the single worst name.
  • The investor profile: long carry, short vol, short skew, short correlation, short forward vol. Expected life and yield look great precisely because so many risk premia are being sold simultaneously.

3. Dealer Hedging & Market Footprint

  • Dealers (long the note's mirror) are structurally long vega and long forward skew from autocall books; their re-hedging is a known macro flow: as spot falls toward coupon/protection barriers, dealer vega and gamma positions flip and grow, amplifying vol moves (documented in Korea/Japan autocall episodes and the 2020 crash).
  • Model requirements: smile dynamics (SLV), discrete-date triggers, dividends (long-dated single names), stochastic rates for long tenors, correlation (worst-of) — the autocallable is the de facto SLV benchmark product.
  • Greeks are discontinuous at each observation date/trigger: delta jumps when an autocall nearly triggers; "peak vega" sits around the protection barrier 1–2 years out.

Key Takeaways

  • Phoenix = conditional memory coupons + autocall + European barrier put, on (usually) a worst-of basket.
  • Short extension option: lives briefly in good markets, extends fully in bad ones.
  • The coupon aggregates vol, skew, correlation and forward-vol premia — count the options sold.
  • Dealer autocall books drive observable vega/gamma flows around barrier zones.
  • Simulation on SLV with correlation is the pricing floor; no decomposition is exact here.

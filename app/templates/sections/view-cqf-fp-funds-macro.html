Fundamentals: Basics of Macroeconomics

GDP metrics, fiscal vs. monetary policy levers, sovereign mandates, and real-life timelines.

Plain English Notes

The Big Picture: an economy has two control panels. Governments tax and spend (fiscal policy); central banks set the price of money (monetary policy). Markets spend their whole life guessing the next move on each panel.

  • GDP is the scoreboard: total value of what a country produces. Growth too slow → unemployment; too fast → inflation. Policy tries to keep it in the corridor.
  • The trade-off is real: in the sandbox below, cutting rates boosts growth and inflation; hiking does the reverse. There is no lever that moves only one dial — that tension is the whole job of a central bank.
  • Mandates differ: the Fed must balance inflation and employment (dual mandate); the ECB targets inflation first. Same tools, different priorities — which is why their decisions diverge.
  • COVID was the textbook live: rates to zero + massive QE + fiscal stimulus (2020), then the fastest hiking cycle in 40 years when inflation arrived (2022) — the case-study tab walks the timeline.

The Macroeconomic Policy Sandbox

As policymaker, you steer the economy using Fiscal Policy (government taxation $t$ and spending $G$, managing budget deficits) and Monetary Policy (adjusting central bank interest rates). Your objective is to sustain growth near the Trend Growth Rate (2.5%) without triggering runaway inflation or severe unemployment.

The Sovereign Monetary Policy Making Bodies

Each major economy delegates interest rate decisions to specialized central bank committees. Neil Graham's slide outlines their comparative frameworks:

Central Bank Policy Body Name Members Meeting Frequency Name of Policy Interest Rate
Federal Reserve (Fed) Federal Open Market Committee (FOMC) 12 8 times per year Fed Funds Target Range (FFTR)
European Central Bank (ECB) The Governing Council 34 Every 6 weeks Refinancing (Refi) Rate & Deposit Facility
Bank of England (BoE) Monetary Policy Committee (MPC) 9 8 times per year Bank Rate
Bank of Japan (BoJ) The Policy Board (MPM) 9 8 times per year Discount Rate

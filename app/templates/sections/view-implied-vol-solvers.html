Implied Vol: Bisection, Newton-Raphson, Corrado-Miller

Inverting Black-Scholes: the root-finding problem solved millions of times a day, its numerical hazards, and the closed-form approximations that seed it.

1. The Problem

Given a market price $C_{mkt}$, find $\sigma$ such that

$$f(\sigma) = C_{BS}(\sigma) - C_{mkt} = 0.$$

Existence and uniqueness are guaranteed on the no-arbitrage interval: $C_{BS}$ is continuous and strictly increasing in $\sigma$ (vega > 0), running from intrinsic value (σ→0) to the spot bound (σ→∞). The function is well-behaved but its slope — vega — collapses for deep ITM/OTM and short-dated options, which is where solvers misbehave.

2. Bisection: Slow and Indestructible

Bracket the root, e.g. $\sigma \in [10^{-4}, 5]$, and halve:

while (hi - lo) > tol:
    mid = (lo + hi) / 2
    if C_BS(mid) > C_mkt: hi = mid
    else:                 lo = mid

Linear convergence: error halves each step, so ~45 iterations for $10^{-12}$ accuracy regardless of the contract. Never diverges, needs no derivative — the fallback of choice when robustness beats speed.

3. Newton-Raphson: Fast with a Failure Mode

Use vega as the derivative:

$$\sigma_{n+1} = \sigma_n - \frac{C_{BS}(\sigma_n) - C_{mkt}}{\text{vega}(\sigma_n)}, \qquad \text{vega} = S e^{(b-r)T}\phi(d_1)\sqrt{T}.$$

Quadratic convergence — 3–4 iterations to machine precision from a decent start. Two hazards:

  • Tiny vega (deep ITM/OTM, short T): the step $f/f'$ explodes, overshooting to negative or huge σ. Standard cure: clamp the step or fall back to bisection when vega < ε (a "safeguarded Newton" / Brent's method).
  • Bad seed: convergence is only local. Seeds below: Brenner-Subrahmanyam or Corrado-Miller.

The state of the art, Jäckel's "Let's Be Rational" (2015), uses rational approximations per branch to deliver implied vol to machine precision in two iterations — the reference implementation for production systems.

4. Closed-Form Approximations

Brenner-Subrahmanyam (1988), ATM-forward only: from $c_{ATM} \approx 0.4\, S\sigma\sqrt{T}$,

$$\sigma \approx \frac{C}{0.4\, S \sqrt{T}} \;=\; \sqrt{\frac{2\pi}{T}}\,\frac{C}{S}.$$

Corrado-Miller (1996) extends away from the money. With $X' = Ke^{-rT}$:

$$\sigma\sqrt{T} \approx \frac{\sqrt{2\pi}}{S + X'}\left[C - \frac{S - X'}{2} + \sqrt{\left(C - \frac{S - X'}{2}\right)^2 - \frac{(S - X')^2}{\pi}}\right].$$

Accurate to a few basis points of vol within roughly ±10% moneyness; degrades (the square root can even go negative) far from the money. Use it as the Newton seed, not the final answer.

5. Practical Checklist

  • Validate first: reject prices outside no-arbitrage bounds $[\max(Se^{(b-r)T} - Ke^{-rT}, 0),\, Se^{(b-r)T}]$ — no σ exists for them.
  • Use OTM options: invert puts below the forward, calls above (bigger time value, bigger vega, better conditioning); parity converts.
  • Seed with Corrado-Miller (or Brenner-Subrahmanyam ATM), iterate safeguarded Newton, fallback bisection.
  • Vectorize: surfaces mean thousands of inversions per snapshot — branch-free formulations (Jäckel) matter at scale.
  • Quote convention: implied vol is the market's price metric, not a forecast — the smile across strikes is the next section's subject.

Key Takeaways

  • Implied vol exists and is unique inside no-arbitrage bounds because vega > 0.
  • Bisection: linear, unconditionally safe. Newton: quadratic, needs vega ≥ ε and a good seed.
  • Brenner-Subrahmanyam: $\sigma \approx \sqrt{2\pi/T}\,C/S$ at-the-money; Corrado-Miller extends off-ATM.
  • Production answer: validated inputs + seeded, safeguarded Newton (or Jäckel's rational method).
  • Low-vega regions (deep ITM/OTM, short-dated) are where naive solvers blow up.

Guest Lecture

Natural Language Processing for the SDGs

Turning text into measurement: NLP applied to the UN Sustainable Development Goals, ESG analytics, and the quant pipeline that connects unstructured language to investable signals.

1. The Measurement Problem

The SDGs and ESG investing share a bottleneck: the relevant information lives in text — corporate reports, news, regulatory filings, NGO disclosures — not in clean numeric series. NLP is the bridge from this unstructured language to quantitative indicators that portfolios and policy can act on. The challenge is genuine signal extraction, not keyword counting: greenwashing, selective disclosure and inconsistent taxonomies make naive approaches misleading.

2. The NLP Pipeline

  • Classification: map text to SDG categories / ESG pillars — fine-tuned transformers (BERT-family) on labeled corpora; multi-label since a sentence can touch several goals.
  • Sentiment & stance: beyond polarity — is the entity committing, reporting, or being criticized? Aspect-based sentiment ties tone to specific issues.
  • Named-entity & relation extraction: who did what (company → action → impact) — building structured knowledge from prose.
  • Embeddings & semantic search: represent documents as vectors for similarity, clustering, and large-language-model retrieval (the LLM-quant talk's machinery).
  • Greenwashing detection: compare commitment language against action evidence — discrepancy as a red flag (an alpha and a risk signal).

3. From Text to Investable Signal

  • NLP-derived ESG scores feed the Black-Litterman views (RSI Part 04) or tilt factor portfolios — text becomes a quantitative input like any other.
  • Validation is essential: backtest the signal honestly (the portfolio-workshop standards) — ESG-alpha claims are riddled with look-ahead and provider-disagreement noise (ESG ratings correlate ~0.5 across vendors).
  • Event studies: controversy-detection signals (an NLP alert on emerging scandals) tested against subsequent returns — does the text lead the price?
  • Risk overlay: NLP-flagged controversies as a tail-risk screen (avoid blowups) rather than only a return signal — often the more robust use.

Key Takeaways

  • SDG/ESG measurement is fundamentally a text problem; NLP converts unstructured language into quantitative indicators.
  • Pipeline: classification → aspect sentiment/stance → entity-relation extraction → embeddings → greenwashing/discrepancy detection.
  • Text signals integrate as Black-Litterman views or factor tilts — but demand rigorous, look-ahead-free validation.
  • Controversy detection is often more robust as a tail-risk screen than as a pure return signal.

1.5 Classical Probability Problems

The Birthday paradox, Monty Hall's door swap puzzle, and cognitive biases in statistical thinking.

1.5.1 Famous Statistical Dilemmas

Two famous probability setups challenge basic human intuition, demonstrating why rigorous mathematics is required over gut feelings:

  • The Birthday Problem: The probability that at least 2 people in a group of $N$ share a birthday. Counter-intuitively, in a group of just 23 people, the probability is greater than 50% ($P \approx 50.73\%$), and for 50 people, it is 97%!
  • The Monty Hall Dilemma: A game show host offers you a choice of three doors. One has a car, two have goats. You choose a door (e.g. Door 1). Monty, who knows what's behind them, opens another door showing a goat (e.g. Door 3) and offers you to swap to Door 2. Mathematically, swapping doubles your chances of winning from $\frac{1}{3}$ to $\frac{2}{3}$!

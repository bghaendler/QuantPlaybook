Case Study 1: Speculating via Exchange-Traded Oil Futures

A speculator takes deliberate pricing risk. Suppose we are bearish on crude oil prices and enter a short position of 10 contracts (1 contract = 1,000 barrels) of June Crude Oil at a price of $79.94 / barrel. The exchange clearinghouse requires an Initial Margin deposit of $4,000 per contract ($40,000 total).

Interactive Daily Mark-to-Market Ledger

Change the daily closing price below to observe how the daily Variation Margin (VM) flows are immediately credited or debited to our margin ledger account:

Case Study 2: Hedging via Producer Short Positions

A farming corporation must establish a firm selling price for its wheat harvest in February before it is harvested in August to eliminate downside crop risk. Hedging is about removing pricing uncertainty entirely:

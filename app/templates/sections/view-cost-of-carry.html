Cost of Carry (b) Framework

One formula to price them all: how a single carry parameter $b$ unifies Black-Scholes, Merton, Black-76, Asay and Garman-Kohlhagen.

1. The Generalized Black-Scholes-Merton Formula

Let $b$ denote the cost of carry: the net rate at which holding the underlying costs (financing minus income). The generalized BSM price is

$$c = S e^{(b-r)T} N(d_1) - K e^{-rT} N(d_2), \qquad p = K e^{-rT} N(-d_2) - S e^{(b-r)T} N(-d_1),$$ $$d_1 = \frac{\ln(S/K) + (b + \sigma^2/2)T}{\sigma\sqrt{T}}, \qquad d_2 = d_1 - \sigma\sqrt{T}.$$

The factor $e^{(b-r)T}$ converts spot into the present value of the forward: indeed $F = S e^{bT}$, and the formula is Black-76 on that forward.

3. Why It Works: Carry Is Just the Hedge's Drift

The hedging argument never uses the real-world drift of $S$; it uses the cost of holding the hedge. A delta hedge in a stock costs financing $r$ and earns dividends $q$, so the risk-neutral drift of $S$ is $b = r - q$. For a futures hedge, no capital is tied up â€” the position costs nothing to carry â€” hence $b = 0$. The generalized PDE is

$$\frac{\partial V}{\partial t} + \tfrac{1}{2}\sigma^2 S^2 \frac{\partial^2 V}{\partial S^2} + b S \frac{\partial V}{\partial S} - rV = 0,$$

with $b$ in the convection term but $r$ in the discounting term â€” they coincide only in the textbook stock case.

4. Carry-Sensitive Greeks

Two distinct rate sensitivities exist under carry:

$$\rho = \frac{\partial c}{\partial r}, \qquad \text{carry rho} = \frac{\partial c}{\partial b} = T\, S e^{(b-r)T} N(d_1) > 0.$$

For FX options, $\partial c/\partial r_f = -T S e^{-r_f T} N(d_1)$ (foreign rho). Delta also changes: $\Delta_c = e^{(b-r)T} N(d_1)$, so an ITM call on a high-yield currency has delta noticeably below $N(d_1)$. Put-call parity generalizes to

$$c - p = S e^{(b-r)T} - K e^{-rT}.$$

5. Worked Example: FX Option

EUR/USD spot $S = 1.10$, domestic (USD) $r = 5\%$, foreign (EUR) $r_f = 3\%$ â†’ $b = 2\%$. Strike $K = 1.10$, $T = 1$, $\sigma = 10\%$.

$$d_1 = \frac{0 + (0.02 + 0.005)}{0.10} = 0.25, \qquad d_2 = 0.15.$$ $$c = 1.10\, e^{-0.03} N(0.25) - 1.10\, e^{-0.05} N(0.15) = 1.0675 \times 0.5987 - 1.0464 \times 0.5596 \approx 0.0535.$$

Key Takeaways

  • One formula with carry $b$ spans BSM ($b=r$), Merton ($r-q$), Black-76 ($0$), Asay ($0, r=0$), Garman-Kohlhagen ($r-r_f$).
  • $Se^{bT}$ is the forward; generalized BSM â‰¡ Black-76 applied to it.
  • $b$ enters the PDE drift; $r$ enters discounting â€” separate roles.
  • Commodities: $b = r + \text{storage} - \text{convenience yield}$; sign of $b - r$ â†” contango/backwardation.
  • Under carry there are two rhos: with respect to $r$ and to $b$ (or $r_f$, $q$).

2. Functions & Partials

Trigonometric periodicities, hyperbolic mappings, bivariate derivatives, chain rules, and total differentials.

1.5 Trigonometric & Hyperbolic Mappings

Trigonometric periodicities are defined for period $2\pi$. The reciprocal functions are $\sec x = 1/\cos x$, $\csc x = 1/\sin x$, and $\cot x = 1/\tan x$. Key circular identity: $\cos^2 x + \sin^2 x = 1$.

Hyperbolic Functions: Defined via exponentials:

$$\sinh x = \frac{e^x - e^{-x}}{2} \quad , \quad \cosh x = \frac{e^x + e^{-x}}{2} \quad , \quad \tanh x = \frac{\sinh x}{\cosh x}$$

Hyperbolic Identities: $\cosh^2 x - \sinh^2 x = 1$, and $\sinh(x+y) = \sinh x\cosh y + \cosh x\sinh y$.

Inverse Hyperbolic Functions (Logarithmic Proof): By treating $y = \sinh^{-1} x \implies x = \frac{e^y - e^{-y}}{2}$, multiplying by $e^y$ yields the quadratic $(e^y)^2 - 2x(e^y) - 1 = 0$. Solving and taking the positive root yields:

$$\sinh^{-1} x = \ln\left(x + \sqrt{x^2 + 1}\right) \quad , \quad \cosh^{-1} x = \ln\left(x + \sqrt{x^2 - 1}\right)$$ $$\tanh^{-1} x = \frac{1}{2}\ln\left(\frac{1+x}{1-x}\right)$$

1.6 Bivariate Derivatives & Chain Rules

In multivariate calculus, partial derivatives evaluate rates of change holding other independent variables fixed:

$$\frac{\partial f}{\partial x} = \lim_{\delta x \to 0} \frac{f(x+\delta x, y) - f(x, y)}{\delta x} \quad , \quad \frac{\partial f}{\partial y} = \lim_{\delta y \to 0} \frac{f(x, y+\delta y) - f(x, y)}{\delta y}$$

The Chain Rule (Case I): If $x = x(s)$ and $y = y(s)$, the total derivative with respect to $s$ is:

$$\frac{dF}{ds} = \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}\frac{dx}{ds} + \frac{\partial f}{\partial y}\frac{dy}{ds}$$

The Chain Rule (Case II): If $x = x(u,v)$ and $y = y(u,v)$, the partials are:

$$\frac{\partial F}{\partial u} = \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}\frac{\partial x}{\partial u} + \frac{\partial f}{\partial y}\frac{\partial y}{\partial u} \quad , \quad \frac{\partial F}{\partial v} = \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}\frac{\partial x}{\partial v} + \frac{\partial f}{\partial y}\frac{\partial y}{\partial v}$$

Total Differential ($df$): Examining the linear surface changes:

$$df = \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}dx + \frac{\partial f}{\partial y}dy$$

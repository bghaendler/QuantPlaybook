Complex Analysis for Quants

Geometric Moduli Interpretations

|z| as distance: circles, disks, annuli and half-planes as inequalities — the geometric vocabulary in which convergence regions and analyticity strips are written.

1. Modulus = Distance

$|z| = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}$ is the distance from the origin; $|z - z_0|$ the distance from $z_0$. Core identities:

$$|z_1z_2| = |z_1||z_2|, \qquad |z|^2 = z\bar{z}, \qquad \left|\frac{z_1}{z_2}\right| = \frac{|z_1|}{|z_2|}, \qquad |\bar{z}| = |z|.$$

and the inequalities that drive every convergence proof:

$$|z_1 + z_2| \le |z_1| + |z_2| \;\text{(triangle)}, \qquad |z_1 - z_2| \ge \bigl||z_1| - |z_2|\bigr| \;\text{(reverse)}.$$

The reverse triangle inequality is the workhorse for bounding denominators away from zero on contours — "on $|z| = R$, $|z^2 + 1| \ge R^2 - 1$" is the move that makes arc contributions vanish in Part 16.

2. Loci as Equations and Inequalities

SetGeometry
$|z - z_0| = r$Circle, center $z_0$, radius $r$
$|z - z_0| < r$Open disk — the domain of a Taylor series
$r_1 < |z - z_0| < r_2$Annulus — the domain of a Laurent series (Part 13)
$|z - a| = |z - b|$Perpendicular bisector of segment $ab$ (a line)
$|z - a| = k|z - b|$, $k \ne 1$Circle of Apollonius
$\mathrm{Im}\,z > 0$, $a < \mathrm{Im}\,z < b$Half-plane; horizontal strip — analyticity strips of characteristic functions

3. Where a Quant Meets These Sets

  • Unit disk $|z| < 1$: stability of time-series — an ARMA/GARCH process is stationary iff the AR polynomial's roots lie outside the unit circle (equivalently the companion-matrix eigenvalues inside it); the same criterion governs stability of finite-difference schemes (von Neumann analysis: amplification factor $|g| \le 1$).
  • Annuli: the natural home of Laurent expansions, hence of residue extraction around singularities of pricing transforms.
  • Strips $a < \mathrm{Im}\,u < b$: where $\mathbb{E}[e^{iuX}]$ extends analytically; the strip's edges are moment explosions (e.g. Heston's finite-moment boundary), and damping parameters must be chosen strictly inside.
  • Bounding on circles: ML-inequality estimates $\left|\int_\gamma f\right| \le \max_\gamma|f| \cdot \text{length}(\gamma)$ — modulus geometry is how arcs are killed in contour arguments.

4. Worked Micro-Examples

  • $|z - 1| + |z + 1| = 4$: an ellipse with foci $\pm 1$ — sum-of-distances definition read straight off the moduli.
  • $\mathrm{Re}\left(\frac{1}{z}\right) = \frac{x}{x^2 + y^2} = c$ ⇒ circle through the origin: inversion maps lines/circles to lines/circles (preview of Part 04's mappings).
  • On $|z| = 2$: $\left|\frac{1}{z^2 + 1}\right| \le \frac{1}{|z|^2 - 1} = \frac{1}{3}$ — reverse triangle inequality bounding an integrand for an ML estimate.

Key Takeaways

  • Modulus is distance; circles, disks, annuli, strips are inequalities in $|z - z_0|$ or $\mathrm{Im}\,z$.
  • The reverse triangle inequality bounds denominators on contours — the key step in killing arcs.
  • Unit-circle root conditions = stationarity (ARMA/GARCH) and FD scheme stability — the same geometry, twice.
  • Convergence regions (Taylor: disks; Laurent: annuli; char. functions: strips) are all moduli statements.

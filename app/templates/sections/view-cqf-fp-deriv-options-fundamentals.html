European vs. American Exercise Styles

Regular vanilla options are classified by their exercise flexibility. This is purely a label and has nothing to do with geography:

  • European Options: Can only be exercised at the exact moment of expiration ($t = T$).
  • American Options: Can be exercised at any time up to and including expiration ($0 \le t \le T$). This added flexibility typically increases the premium ($C_{Amer} \ge C_{Euro}$).

Cash vs. Physical Settlement

Depending on the underlying asset class, options are settled in one of two modes:

  • Physical Delivery: Standard for single stocks and commodities (e.g. delivering 1,000 physical barrels of crude oil or 100 shares of Amazon stock).
  • Cash Settlement: Standard for index derivatives (e.g., S&P 500 options). Since you cannot physically buy or split an index, the writer pays the holder the index point difference in cash multiplied by a factor (e.g. $25 per index point).

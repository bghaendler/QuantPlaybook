Diagnostics & Portfolio Risk

Portfolio variance: $\sigma_p^2 = w^\top \Sigma w$ with $\Sigma_{ij} = \rho_{ij}\sigma_i\sigma_j$. The diversification ratio is $\sum w_i\sigma_i / \sigma_p$ — the higher, the more correlation benefit captured.

Why PSD Validation Matters

  • Pairwise estimates from different windows/sources routinely assemble into a non-PSD "matrix" — implying negative portfolio variance for some weights. Monte Carlo (Cholesky) fails instantly; optimizers silently exploit the inconsistency.
  • Three-asset intuition: with $\rho_{12} = \rho_{13} = 0.9$, transitivity forces $\rho_{23} \ge 2(0.9)^2 - 1 = 0.62$. The checker enforces the determinant condition $1 - \rho_{12}^2 - \rho_{13}^2 - \rho_{23}^2 + 2\rho_{12}\rho_{13}\rho_{23} \ge 0$ and full eigenvalue positivity.
  • Repair methods when estimation breaks PSD: eigenvalue clipping (set negatives to 0, rescale), or Higham's nearest-correlation-matrix algorithm — both standard pre-processing before Cholesky.
  • Estimation upgrades for production: shrinkage (Ledoit-Wolf) toward structured targets, factor models, and stress overlays (crisis ρ → 1) for tail risk.

Central Banks & Reference Rates

Understand the mechanics of monetary policy, interbank reserve accounts, secured repo transactions, and modern risk-free reference rates.

Central Bank Reserve Accounts

Commercial banks hold reserve accounts directly with their respective central bank. These reserve balances are the cornerstone of the modern financial system, facilitating smooth interbank settlement.

When customers conduct cross-bank transactions, physical reserves are shifted between the banks' reserve accounts at the central bank. Overnight, the central bank pays interest on these reserve balances. This interest rate is the Policy Rate (or reference rate)—the primary lever of global monetary policy.

Key Takeaway When central banks "raise" or "lower" interest rates, they are directly adjusting the overnight policy rate paid on reserve balances.

Understanding Repo Haircuts

A haircut is the percentage reduction in the value of the asset used as collateral. For example, if you pledge €1.05M of assets with a 4.76% haircut, you can borrow exactly:

€1,050,000 × (1 - 0.0476) = €1,000,000

Haircuts protect lenders against fluctuations in the collateral asset price during the term of the repo.

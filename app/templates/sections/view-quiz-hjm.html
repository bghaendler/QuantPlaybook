Question 1 Multiple Choice

1. Consider the following Vasicek model for the short rate $r(t)$ under the risk-neutral measure $\mathbb{Q}$:

$$ dr(t) = 0.2(0.05 - r(t))dt + 0.01dW^*(t) $$

What is the drift $\alpha(t, T)$ of the instantaneous forward rate under the risk-neutral probability measure $\mathbb{Q}$?

Show Mathematical Solution & Derivation

Solution Derivation:

Under the risk-neutral probability measure $\mathbb{Q}$, the drift $\alpha(t, T)$ and the volatility $\sigma(t, T)$ of the instantaneous forward rates must satisfy the risk-neutral HJM drift condition to prevent arbitrage:

$$\alpha(t, T) = \sigma(t, T) \int_t^T \sigma(t, u) du$$

For the short rate Vasicek model, the instantaneous forward rate volatility function is:

$$\sigma(t, T) = \sigma_V e^{-\kappa(T-t)} = 0.01 e^{-0.2(T-t)}$$

Integrating the volatility function with respect to $u$ from $t$ to $T$ yields:

$$\int_t^T \sigma(t, u) du = 0.01 \int_t^T e^{-0.2(u-t)} du = 0.01 \left[ \frac{e^{-0.2(u-t)}}{-0.2} \right]_t^T = 0.05 \left( 1 - e^{-0.2(T-t)} \right)$$

Multiplying by the forward rate volatility $\sigma(t, T)$ yields the risk-neutral drift:

$$\alpha(t, T) = 0.01 e^{-0.2(T-t)} \times 0.05 \left( 1 - e^{-0.2(T-t)} \right) = 0.0005 e^{-0.2(T-t)} \left( 1 - e^{-0.2(T-t)} \right)$$

Correct Option: A.

Question 2 Multiple Choice

2. Using the same Vasicek model parameters from Q1 ($\sigma_V = 0.01$, $\kappa = 0.2$):

What is the volatility $\sigma(t, T)$ of the instantaneous forward rate?

Show Mathematical Solution & Derivation

Solution Derivation:

In the standard Vasicek short rate model under $\mathbb{Q}$, the yield curve has an affine bond pricing structure: $P(t, T) = \exp(A(t, T) - B(t, T)r(t))$, where the sensitivity of bond prices to short rates is:

$$B(t, T) = \frac{1 - e^{-\kappa(T-t)}}{\kappa}$$

The diffusion coefficient (volatility) of the ZCB is $v(t, T) = -\sigma_V B(t, T)$. The instantaneous forward rate volatility $\sigma(t, T)$ is related directly to the ZCB volatility function via differentiation:

$$\sigma(t, T) = \frac{\partial v(t, T)}{\partial T} = \sigma_V e^{-\kappa(T-t)}$$

Plugging in our parameters $\sigma_V = 0.01$ and $\kappa = 0.2$:

$$\sigma(t, T) = 0.01 e^{-0.2(T-t)}$$

Correct Option: A.

Question 3 True / False

3. Using the same Vasicek model from Question 1:

Is the risk-neutral HJM drift condition satisfied?

Show Mathematical Solution & Derivation

Theoretical Answer:

Yes. In any arbitrage-free term structure model specified directly under a risk-neutral (martingale) probability measure $\mathbb{Q}$, the drift of the instantaneous forward rates must satisfy the HJM drift condition:

$$\alpha(t, T) = \sigma(t, T) \int_t^T \sigma(t, u) du$$

Because the Vasicek model's short rate dynamics were defined directly under $\mathbb{Q}$, the resulting forward rate drift is mathematically guaranteed to satisfy the HJM condition to prevent arbitrage.

Correct Answer: Yes.

Question 4 Multiple Choice

4. Consider a Gaussian HJM model with constant volatility $\sigma(t, T) \equiv \sigma$ and constant market price of risk $\lambda(t) \equiv \lambda$. The $\mathbb{P}$-dynamics of the $T$-forward rate is:

$$ df(t, T) = (\sigma^2(T-t) + \sigma\lambda) dt + \sigma dW(t) $$

Which of the following represents the correct physical $\mathbb{P}$-dynamics of the corresponding continuously compounded yield process $y(t, T)$?

Show Mathematical Solution & Derivation

Solution Derivation:

The yield process $y(t, T)$ is the average of forward rates:

$$y(t, T) = \frac{1}{T-t} \int_t^T f(t, u) du$$

Let $Y(t, T) = (T-t) y(t, T) = \int_t^T f(t, u) du$. Applying Leibniz's integration rule to $d_t Y(t, T)$:

$$d Y(t, T) = -f(t, t)dt + \int_t^T df(t, u) du = -r(t)dt + \int_t^T \left[ (\sigma^2(u-t) + \sigma\lambda)dt + \sigma dW(t) \right] du$$

Evaluating the deterministic integrals from $t$ to $T$ gives:

$$d Y(t, T) = \left( -r(t) + \frac{1}{2}\sigma^2(T-t)^2 + \sigma\lambda(T-t) \right) dt + \sigma(T-t) dW(t)$$

Applying Itô's formula for the product/quotient $y(t, T) = \frac{Y(t, T)}{T-t}$:

$$dy(t, T) = d\left(\frac{Y(t,T)}{T-t}\right) = \frac{d Y}{T-t} + \frac{Y(t, T)}{(T-t)^2} dt = \frac{d Y}{T-t} + \frac{y(t, T)}{T-t} dt$$

Substituting $dY$ into this relation yields:

$$dy(t, T) = \left( \frac{y(t, T)-r(t)}{T-t} + \frac{1}{2}\sigma^2(T-t) + \sigma\lambda \right) dt + \sigma dW(t)$$

Correct Option: A.

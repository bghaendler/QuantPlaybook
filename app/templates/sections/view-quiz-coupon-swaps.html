Question 1 (Fixed Coupon Bond Pricing) Numeric Input

Suppose a 3-year fixed coupon bond has a par value of $100$ and pays annual coupons of $6\%$ (at the end of years 1, 2, and 3). If the continuously compounded spot yields are constant at $4\%$ across all maturities (flat yield curve $y(0, T) = 4\%$), what is the ex-dividend price of the bond today ($t=0$)? Round your answer to two decimal places.

Show Mathematical Solution & Derivation

Step-by-Step Mathematical Derivation:

Given continuously compounded yields $y(0, T) = 4\% = 0.04$, the Zero-Coupon Bond (discount factor) prices maturing in $T=1, 2, 3$ years are:

$$ P(0, 1) = e^{-0.04 \times 1} \approx 0.960789 $$ $$ P(0, 2) = e^{-0.04 \times 2} \approx 0.923116 $$ $$ P(0, 3) = e^{-0.04 \times 3} \approx 0.886920 $$

The annual coupon rate is $6\%$, so coupon payments are $c_1 = c_2 = c_3 = 6$ on a principal of $N = 100$. Today at $t = 0$, prior to any payment dates, the ex-dividend bond price is:

$$ p(0) = c_1 P(0, 1) + c_2 P(0, 2) + (c_3 + N) P(0, 3) $$ $$ p(0) = 6 \times 0.960789 + 6 \times 0.923116 + 106 \times 0.886920 $$ $$ p(0) \approx 5.764734 + 5.538696 + 94.013520 = 105.31695 \approx \mathbf{105.32} $$

Correct Answer: 105.32

Question 2 (Floating Rate Note Pricing) Multiple Choice

A floating rate note (FRN) is issued today at $t=0$ with principal $N = 100$. The reset and settlement dates are $T_0 = 1$, $T_1 = 2$, and $T_2 = 3$ (in years), where the floating coupons are tied to the simple spot rate $L(T_{i-1}, T_i)$. If we observe that the zero-coupon bond price maturing in 1 year is $P(0, 1) = 0.95$, what is the price of the FRN today at $t=0$, prior to the first reset date $T_0$?

Show Mathematical Solution & Derivation

Step-by-Step Mathematical Explanation:

According to the floating rate note replication theorem, we can perfectly hedge and replicate all coupon payments $C_i$ and the final principal $N$ by buying ZCBs maturing at the first reset date $T_0$ (which is $T_0 = 1$ year here).

Prior to the first reset date (at $t < T_0$), the price of the note is simply:

$$ p(t) = N P(t, T_0) $$

Evaluating this today at $t = 0$ with $T_0 = 1$ year and $P(0, 1) = 0.95$:

$$ p(0) = N P(0, 1) = 100 \times 0.95 = \mathbf{95.00} $$

Correct Option: B. 95.00

Question 3 (Spot Swap Rate Calculation) Numeric Input

Consider a spot-starting interest rate swap ($t = T_0 = 0$) with a length of 3 years and annual payments ($\delta = 1.0$). The zero-coupon bond prices are observed to be $P(0, 1) = 0.96$, $P(0, 2) = 0.91$, and $P(0, 3) = 0.85$. Calculate the spot swap rate $R_{swap}(0)$. Express your answer in percentage points (e.g. 5.50 for 5.50%) and round to two decimal places.

Show Mathematical Solution & Derivation

Step-by-Step Mathematical Derivation:

The swap starts today at spot $T_0 = 0$ (so $P(0, T_0) = P(0, 0) = 1.0$). Payment settlement dates are $T_1 = 1$, $T_2 = 2$, and $T_3 = 3$ with year fractions $\delta = 1.0$. The spot swap rate formula is:

$$ R_{swap}(0) = \frac{P(0, T_0) - P(0, T_3)}{\delta \sum_{i=1}^3 P(0, T_i)} = \frac{1.0 - P(0, 3)}{\delta \bigl( P(0, 1) + P(0, 2) + P(0, 3) \bigr)} $$

Substituting the observed discount factors:

$$ R_{swap}(0) = \frac{1.0 - 0.85}{1.0 \times (0.96 + 0.91 + 0.85)} = \frac{0.15}{2.72} $$ $$ R_{swap}(0) \approx 0.055147 \text{ (or } \mathbf{5.51\%}\text{)} $$

Expressed in percentage points, the par spot swap rate is exactly 5.51.

Correct Answer: 5.51

Question 4 (Forward Starting Swap Rate) Multiple Choice

Suppose you enter into a forward-starting payer swap today at $t=0$ where the first reset date is $T_0 = 1$ (one year from now), the swap length is 2 years, and payments are annual ($\delta = 1.0$). The maturity date is $T_2 = 3$. If the discount factors observed today are $P(0, 1) = 0.95$, $P(0, 2) = 0.90$, and $P(0, 3) = 0.84$, calculate the forward swap rate $R_{swap}(0)$.

Show Mathematical Solution & Derivation

Step-by-Step Mathematical Derivation:

A forward starting swap starts at future reset date $T_0 = 1$. The payments settle at $T_1 = 2$ and $T_2 = 3$ with year fractions $\delta = 1.0$. The forward swap rate is given by:

$$ R_{swap}(0) = \frac{P(0, T_0) - P(0, T_2)}{\delta \bigl( P(0, T_1) + P(0, T_2) \bigr)} = \frac{P(0, 1) - P(0, 3)}{\delta \bigl( P(0, 2) + P(0, 3) \bigr)} $$

Substitute the observed discount factors today:

$$ R_{swap}(0) = \frac{0.95 - 0.84}{1.0 \times (0.90 + 0.84)} = \frac{0.11}{1.74} $$ $$ R_{swap}(0) \approx 0.063218 \text{ (or } \mathbf{6.32\%}\text{)} $$

Thus, the forward swap rate is exactly 6.32%.

Correct Option: B. 6.32%

Question 5 (Lemma 1 - Coupon Bonds Par Value) True / False

Is the following statement true or false?
According to Lemma 1, a fixed coupon bond with annual coupon rate $c$ and par value $1.0$ trades at exactly par (ex-coupon price $= 1.0$) at the first reset date $T_0$ if and only if its coupon rate $c$ is equal to the corresponding spot swap rate $R_{swap}(T_0)$ for the same tenor structure.

Show Mathematical Solution & Derivation

Step-by-Step Mathematical Derivation:

This is exactly the statement of Lemma 1 in the lecture notes. Under the ex-coupon pricing convention at valuation time $t = T_0$, the price of a fixed-coupon bond with principal $N=1$, annual payment frequency $\delta$, and coupon rate $c$ is:

$$ p(T_0) = \sum_{i=1}^n P(T_0, T_i) \delta c + P(T_0, T_n) $$

We solve for the coupon rate $c$ that makes the bond trade exactly at par value ($p(T_0) = 1$):

$$ 1 = \sum_{i=1}^n P(T_0, T_i) \delta c + P(T_0, T_n) $$ $$ 1 - P(T_0, T_n) = c \delta \sum_{i=1}^n P(T_0, T_i) $$ $$ c = \frac{1 - P(T_0, T_n)}{\delta \sum_{i=1}^n P(T_0, T_i)} $$

The right-hand side is exactly the spot swap rate $R_{swap}(T_0)$ starting at $T_0$. Thus:

$$ c = R_{swap}(T_0) $$

Which completes the proof. The statement is strictly True.

Correct Answer: True

1.3 Coupon Bonds and Interest Rate Swaps

Pricing fixed coupon bonds, floating rate notes, and interest rate swaps, including the par-swap rate and the ex-dividend convention.
In fixed income markets, swaps and bonds form the primary trading contracts. Swaps exchange floating interest flows for fixed rate agreements, priced using ZCB replication.

Fixed Coupon Bonds

Bonds with maturities beyond a year typically pay coupons. If these coupon rates are fixed, the instrument is a fixed coupon bond. It is specified by:

  • Coupon payment dates $T_1 < T_2 < \dots < T_n$, where $T_n$ is the maturity.
  • Fixed coupon payments $c_1, c_2, \dots, c_n$ paid at each coupon date $T_i$.
  • A principal (notional) value $N$ paid at maturity $T_n$.

The price of the coupon bond at calendar date $t \le T_n$ is given by multiplying the remaining cash flows by the respective zero-coupon bond prices and summing them up.

The Ex-Dividend Pricing Convention

In interest rate markets, if the valuation date $t$ coincides exactly with a coupon date $T_i$, then the coupon itself is not part of the price anymore (the buyer does not receive it). In other words, the price is quoted ex-dividend.

Mathematically, the time-$t$ price of the coupon bond is:

$$ p(t) = \sum_{i=1}^n P(t, T_i) c_i \mathbb{1}_{\{t < T_i\}} + P(t, T_n) N $$

The indicator function $\mathbb{1}_{\{t < T_i\}}$ enforces the ex-dividend convention: coupons paid on or before $t$ are excluded.

Floating Rate Notes (FRNs) & Replication Proof

If coupon payments are floating instead of fixed, the instrument is a floating rate note (FRN). It is specified by a sequence of reset and settlement dates $T_0 < T_1 < \dots < T_n$, where $T_n$ is the maturity, and $T_0$ is the first reset date (there is no cash flow at $T_0$).

The floating coupon paid at $T_i$ is determined by the simple spot rate prevailing over the prior period $[T_{i-1}, T_i]$:

$$ C_i = (T_i - T_{i-1}) L(T_{i-1}, T_i) N $$

Replication Strategy (Absence of Arbitrage)

We can prove that the price of an FRN at any calendar date $t \le T_0$ is surprisingly simple: $p(t) = N P(t, T_0)$. We prove this by constructing a self-financing strategy that yields the exact same cash flows as the FRN:

Step 1: Inception at $t \le T_0$

Buy $N$ units of zero-coupon bonds maturing at $T_0$. The initial cost today is exactly:

$$ \text{Initial Cost} = N P(t, T_0) $$
Step 2: At Reset Date $T_0$

The ZCBs mature, paying $N$ cash. Reinvest this amount to buy ZCBs maturing at $T_1$. The number of $T_1$-bonds purchased is:

$$ \text{Bonds Held} = \frac{N}{P(T_0, T_1)} $$
Step 3: At Coupon Date $T_1$

The $T_1$-bonds mature, paying $\frac{N}{P(T_0, T_1)}$ units. This maturing payoff can be decomposed as:

$$ N + N \left(\frac{1}{P(T_0, T_1)} - 1\right) = N + C_1 $$

We pay out the floating coupon $C_1$, keep the principal $N$, and repeat the scheme for $T_2$!

Repeating this process until maturity $T_n$ perfectly matches the floating coupon payouts $C_i$ at each $T_i$, and pays the principal $N$ at maturity. Because the cash flows are replicated perfectly, the initial cost must equal the price, proving that:

$$ p(t) = N P(t, T_0) \quad \text{for } t \le T_0 $$ At the first reset date ($t = T_0$), the price of the FRN ex-coupon is exactly the par value: $p(T_0) = N$.

Interest Rate Swaps (IRS)

An Interest Rate Swap (IRS) is an over-the-counter derivative contract in which two parties exchange cash flows of a fixed coupon bond and a floating rate note. It is specified by:

  • Reset and settlement dates $T_0 < T_1 < \dots < T_n$.
  • A fixed rate $K$, a notional $N$, and constant payment frequency $\delta = T_i - T_{i-1}$.

In a payer interest rate swap settled in arrears, the holder pays the fixed rate $K$ and receives the floating rate. The net cash flows match holding an FRN and going short a fixed coupon bond.

The value of a payer swap at calendar date $t \le T_0$ is:

$$ V_p(t) = N \left( P(t, T_0) - P(t, T_n) - \delta K \sum_{i=1}^n P(t, T_i) \right) $$

The Swap Rate $R_{swap}(t)$

The Swap Rate is the unique fixed rate $K$ which makes the present value of the swap equal to zero at inception ($V_p(t) = 0$):

$$ R_{swap}(t) = \frac{P(t, T_0) - P(t, T_n)}{\delta \sum_{i=1}^n P(t, T_i)} $$

We can also express the swap rate as an annuity-weighted average of forward rates:

$$ R_{swap}(t) = \sum_{i=1}^n w_i(t) F(t, T_{i-1}, T_i) $$

with weights $w_i(t) = \frac{P(t, T_i)}{\sum_{j=1}^n P(t, T_j)}$.

Lemma 1 (Par Value Relation): A fixed-coupon bond has par value at $T_0$ if and only if its coupon rates equal the corresponding swap rate: $$ 1 = \sum_{i=1}^n P(T_0, T_i) \delta R_{swap}(T_0) + P(T_0, T_n) $$

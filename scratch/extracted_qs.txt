To calculate the number of units (or face amount) of a bond required to "perfectly" hedge another bond, you must neutralize the exposure to interest rate risk[1][2]. In practice, this is typically done by matching the **DV01** (Dollar Value of an '01)—the dollar change in the bond's price for a one-basis-point change in yield—of the two positions[2][3][4].

### The General Formula
To neutralize interest rate risk, you aim for a hedge where the net DV01 of the portfolio is zero[2][5]. The face amount required for the hedge is calculated as[5]:

$$\text{Hedge Face Amount} = \text{Face Amount of Position} \times \left( \frac{\text{DV01 of Position}}{\text{DV01 of Hedge Instrument}} \right)$$

*Note: If you are hedging a long position, you will typically need to take a short position in the hedge instrument, and vice versa[2][6].*

---

### Step-by-Step Calculation
1.  **Calculate the DV01 of the bond you want to hedge:** Determine how much the bond's price changes (in dollars) for a 1-basis-point (0.01%) move in interest rates[2][4].
2.  **Calculate the DV01 of the hedging bond:** Determine the price sensitivity of the instrument you are using to hedge[2][4].
3.  **Determine the Hedge Ratio:** Divide the DV01 of your current position by the DV01 of the hedge instrument[5][4].
4.  **Calculate the Required Amount:** Multiply the face value of your current position by this hedge ratio to find the face amount you need to buy or sell[5][6].
